- Institutional (forced poverty, exile, imprisonment)

1. Assumed-capacity (the fixed self-image, the story of what one can do) meets a constraint that breaks it.
2. Assumed-capacity shatters. The gap between "what was thought possible" and "what is actually possible" becomes undeniable.
3. Actual-capacity is revealed. The person can no longer pretend. The story collapses.
4. Reorientation. The person reorients from assumed-capacity to actual-capacity. The gap closes.

**Path A: Refusal to reorient**

A person meets a constraint but refuses to accept it. Holds the assumed-capacity as true despite evidence. Blames the constraint instead of examining the gap.

Result: Continuous misalignment-suffering. The energy cost of maintaining the false narrative exceeds the energy freed by reorientation. The person degrades. They suffer more. They learn nothing.

**Path B: Forced reorientation**

A person meets a constraint and cannot refuse. The assumed-capacity is physically demolished. Reorientation is not optional; it is survive-or-die.

Result: The gap closes. What was assumed is replaced by what is actual. The person begins to see with accuracy. The energy freed by dropping the false narrative becomes available for genuine capacity-building.

**The wisdom is identical in both cases when reorientation occurs. The constraint itself is merely the mechanism that makes refusal impossible.**

## The Implicit Judgment of Civilisation

But the reorientation also reveals something else.

When a person is forced to reorient, the quality of that reorientation is partially dependent on external support. The constraint may be inescapable, but the reorientation can be easier or harder depending on:

- Whether the person has access to knowledge about how to reorient
- Whether the social environment supports reorientation or punishes it
- Whether institutions cooperate with actual-capacity or demand the maintenance of assumed-capacity
- Whether the person's actual-capacity is met with resources, or abandoned

A civilisation (and the institutions within it) can be judged by a single metric: **its capacity to help others reorient from assumed-capacity to actual-capacity.**

A civilisation that supports reorientation:
- Creates space for people to see clearly
- Provides knowledge and tools for adaptation
- Does not demand the maintenance of false narratives
- Allocates resources to actual-capacity, not assumed-capacity

A civilisation that blocks reorientation:
- Demands the maintenance of false narratives
- Pathologizes or criminalizes the reorientation (calls it sickness, heresy, delusion)
- Isolates people undergoing reorientation
- Allocates resources to maintaining false self-images at scale
- Exhausts people trying to maintain the gap

**The test is not theoretical. It is observable in real time.**

A person with a constraint is a lens. Through that lens, civilisational structures are revealed:

- If support flows to the person reorienting, the civilisation values accuracy.
- If support is withdrawn, the civilisation fears the revelation.
- If the person is actively suppressed while reorienting, the civilisation is built on the maintenance of false narratives at any cost.
