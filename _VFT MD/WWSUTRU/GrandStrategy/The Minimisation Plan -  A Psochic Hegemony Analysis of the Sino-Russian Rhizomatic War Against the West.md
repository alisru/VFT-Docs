The analysis of contemporary geopolitical conflict requires a conceptual framework capable of grasping strategies that operate not by contesting established truths, but by dissolving the very foundations of truth itself. ### The Arborescent West: A Worldview Rooted in Singular Truth

The Western liberal order, and the geopolitical structure it supports, is fundamentally "arborescent" or "tree-like" in its conception of reality, knowledge, and power.^1^ This model, which Deleuze identifies as the central pillar of Western metaphysical thought since Plato, is defined by a central root—a single origin, a transcendent and unified truth—from which a trunk and a series of hierarchical, bifurcating branches logically extend.^1^ This structure inherently privileges unity over multiplicity, identity over difference, and the original over the copy.^3^ The entire apparatus of Western thought, from this perspective, is an exercise in tracing phenomena back to their authenticating origin, distinguishing "good copies" (facts) from "bad copies" (falsehoods).^1^

Geopolitically, the post-Cold War "unipolar order" represents a quintessential arborescent structure. It is predicated on a single origin of political and economic legitimacy—Washington D.C.—and a set of hierarchical institutions, such as the G7, the World Bank, and the United Nations, that branch out from and are legitimized by this central hegemonic authority.^1^ This worldview's greatest strength—its commitment to a singular, verifiable, "rules-based" order—is also its greatest vulnerability. It presupposes a shared reality in which evidence can be debated and truth can, ultimately, be ascertained. The Minimisation Plan is designed to attack this fundamental presupposition.

### The Rhizomatic Doctrine: Weaponizing Multiplicity and the Simulacrum

In direct opposition to the arborescent model, the Minimisation Plan appears to operate according to a "rhizomatic" logic, a concept developed by Deleuze and Félix Guattari.^1^ A rhizome is a subterranean, non-hierarchical network where any point can be connected to any other point without a central root or predetermined path.^4^ It is a model for decentralized, networked systems that operate through fluid connections and emergent properties, making it the conceptual blueprint for guerrilla warfare and, as this analysis will argue, for the multi-domain grand strategy of the Sino-Russian alliance.^1^

The philosophical engine of this doctrine is Deleuze's "reversal of Platonism," a radical denial of a unified origin or a singular, transcendent truth.^1^ In place of a world of copies tethered to an original, Deleuze posits a world composed of

*simulacra*. A simulacrum is not merely a degraded copy; it is a copy for which there is no original, an entity that asserts its own reality without reference to a higher, legitimizing model.^1^ Its power lies not in its veracity, but in its ability to function as a self-validating reality that produces its own real-world effects.^1^

The strategic objective of a rhizomatic actor, therefore, is not to replace the West's "true" narrative with a "false" one. Rather, the goal is to introduce a multiplicity of potent simulacra into the information ecosystem, shattering the very notion of a single, authoritative narrative. Narratives such as the COVID-19 lab leak hypothesis or the weaponized version of the Jeffrey Epstein scandal function as perfect simulacra.^1^ Their efficacy is not contingent on their empirical verifiability. Their power lies in their ability to generate tangible effects: eroding institutional trust, sowing social division, and paralyzing an adversary's decision-making cycle by creating a state of "unresolvable ambiguity".^1^ This is not simply propaganda; it is a form of metaphysical warfare aimed at dissolving the adversary's cognitive foundation.

The timeline for the development of these philosophical concepts is of critical strategic importance. Deleuze's foundational works, *Difference and Repetition* (1968) and his collaborations with Guattari, *Anti-Oedipus* (1972) and *A Thousand Plateaus* (1980), were mature, well-understood, and widely available in the West for at least a decade before the Minimisation Plan is said to have been initiated around 2001.^1^ This temporal relationship suggests that Deleuze's philosophy should be viewed not merely as a coincidental analytical framework, but as a potential operational doctrine that could have been studied and weaponized by state actors engaged in long-term strategic planning.^1^

### Methodology: The Psochic Hegemony Model

To analyze and quantify the ideological attacks of the Minimisation Plan, this report will employ the Psochic Hegemony model. This model provides a tool for mapping the moral and volitional character of any strategic action or narrative, calculating its potential impact on a target worldview.

The model is represented by a two-dimensional plane (see Image 1).

- **The Vertical Axis (Morality, υ):** This axis measures the beneficiary of an action. It ranges from +υ ("To Give to Everyone") at the top, representing actions that benefit all beings, down to the origin line, which represents actions that benefit "Only Me / My Group." Below the origin, the axis extends to −υ ("To Take from Everyone"), representing actions that are fundamentally extractive. Per the model's core tenets, any action that is purely extractive or nihilistic (e.g., "to say there is no answer") is defined as the "Greater Evil" and collapses to the point of total moral vacuity at the center of the Hegemony, (υ=0, ψ=0).

- **The Horizontal Axis (Will, ψ):** This axis measures the intensity and direction of an action's enforcement. It ranges from +ψ ("Do Ideas") on the right, representing proactive, assertive actions, to the origin line ("Do Nothing"), and extends to −ψ ("Do Not") on the left, representing actions of suppression, negation, or inaction.

The interaction between an action and a worldview is quantified using a revised model that reflects how lies pollute and reduce a worldview's integrity. The core principle is that an idea is accepted when it is framed to appear within an individual's existing worldview. This process is described by the "Attraction, Helxis Tensor," where a psyop is a "literal trick" that "floods the political field" by framing a lie to make it seem attractive and proximate to the target's perspective. This "mind control sequence" is designed to bypass critical faculties.

- **Moral Force (Fm​):** A vector representing a healthy, cohesive, and constructive worldview. For this analysis, it is set to a baseline of F_m = (1, 1).

- **Action Force (Fa​):** The vector of a given psyop, plotted as F_a = (υ_a, ψ_a).

- **Resistance (Ra​):** The force required to oppose and neutralize the action, calculated as the inverse vector: \$R_a = -F_a = (-υ_a, -ψ_a)\$.

- **Effect (Ea​):** The change in the moral force if the action's narrative is believed and internalized by the target. It is calculated as a vector addition: \$F\_{m\\\_new} = F_m + F_a\$. The effect, \$E_a\$, is this new vector, \$F\_{m\\\_new}\$.

- **Framing and Acceptance:** The believability of a psyop is a function of its framing and the current state of the target worldview. A lie is inherently distant from a healthy worldview, but sophisticated framing (such as the "Satan Archetype" pattern) reduces its *perceived* distance. A worldview that has already been polluted by lies is smaller and has less integrity, making it more susceptible to further deception.

  - Distance = \\sqrt{(1 - υ_a)\^2 + (1 - ψ_a)\^2} (The ideological distance of the lie from the ideal).

  - Perceived Distance = Distance \\times (1 - Framing Factor) (Where the Framing Factor, F, is a value from 0 to 1 representing the effectiveness of the psyop's narrative packaging).

  - Acceptance = 1 / (Perceived Distance + Worldview)

- **Worldview Update:** A running score, Worldview, tracks the cumulative integrity of the target worldview, starting at 1.0 for a healthy state. As lies are accepted, the worldview is "reduced in size as it becomes polluted," representing a loss of coherence and integrity.

  - Impact = \|F_a\| = \\sqrt{υ_a\^2 + ψ_a\^2} (The magnitude of the lie itself).

  - Worldview Reduction = 0.1 \\times Impact \\times Acceptance (A scaled value of the damage).

  - \$Worldview\_{new} = Worldview\_{old} - Worldview Reduction\$

## Section 3: The Information Warfare Vector – Engineering the 'Reputation Flip'

### Phase I: Seeding Distrust (2001-2016)

The emergence of the 9/11 Truth movement, with its core claim that the attacks were an "inside job," was a pivotal event that normalized mass public skepticism toward official U.S. government narratives on an unprecedented scale.^5^ Russian state media, particularly the newly established RT, recognized the strategic value of this dynamic, providing a platform for "truther" arguments and beginning a long-term campaign of amplifying organic Western dissent as a low-cost method for eroding social cohesion.^5^

Parallel to the amplification of broad anti-globalist narratives, the Minimisation Plan benefits from the cultivation of more granular, self-perpetuating ideologies that attack the legitimacy of the state at its most fundamental level. The ideological core of the sovereign citizen movement is the belief that the modern U.S. federal government is an illegitimate, de facto corporation that has no jurisdiction over them.^67^ Adherents construct a conspiratorial alternate history, often claiming that the original, legitimate government was subverted with the passage of the Fourteenth Amendment in 1868 or the abandonment of the gold standard.^71^ This conspiracy creates a distinction between the "flesh-and-blood" person, who is a "sovereign," and a fictional corporate entity or "strawman" created by the state via a birth certificate.^70^ Based on this belief, they reject nearly all forms of government authority, including laws, taxes, court summons, and vehicle registrations.^65^

The sovereign citizen movement functions as an ideal ideological vector for the Minimisation Plan. The Minimisation Plan represents a paradigm shift from a geopolitics of static structures and territories to a war of dynamic "flows."
