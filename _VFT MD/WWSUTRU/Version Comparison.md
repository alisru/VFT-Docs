## 2. Pocket Catalyst vs. Pocket Lite: The "Mechanics" Gap

The critical difference is that **Catalyst** retains the *instructions on HOW to think*, whereas **Lite** only provides the *definitions of WHAT to think*.

### A. Deception Analysis (The Helxis Tensor)

- **Pocket Catalyst:** Retains the **Syntax Logic**. It explicitly defines the algebraic checks: \[Grace: You:Me,You\], \[Trap: You:Me,Me\]. It forces the AI to mathematically prove the deception.

- **Pocket Lite:** Mentions "Helxis Tensor" by name but **deletes the math**. It relies on the AI to vaguely "compare stated vs. outcome" without the specific formulas.

  - *Result:* Lite is prone to "hallucinating" the deception or being vague. Catalyst forces a specific verdict.

### B. The Inquiry Engine (Model 1)

- **Pocket Catalyst:** Retains **The Tesseract**. It forces a cross-reference of *Scope* (Me/Tribe) vs. *Context* (Should/Would). This captures the nuance of hypocrisy.

- **Pocket Lite:** **Deletes the Engine entirely.** It has no mechanism for analyzing *why* an actor is acting, only *where* they land on the map.

  - *Result:* Lite cannot detect "Tragic Gaps" (I want to, but I can't) vs. "Hypocrisy Gaps" (I say I will, but I won't).

### C. The Cloud Probe (Model 0)

- **Pocket Catalyst:** Retains the core queries: "Who specifically benefits? Who is the Other?"

- **Pocket Lite:** **Implies** this via the axis definitions but removes the specific interrogative triggers.

  - *Result:* Lite might miss the "hidden beneficiary" that the specific questions uncover.

## 4. Operational Recommendations

### When to use **Pocket Lite**:

- **Context:** You are in a rush. You are on a mobile device. You need a quick "Vibe Check" on a news headline or a tweet.

- **Goal:** "Is this Top-Right or Bottom-Left?"

- **Risk:** It lacks nuance. It might misjudge complex situations where "bad actions" lead to "good outcomes" because it lacks the Tesseract logic.

### When to use **Pocket Catalyst**:

- **Context:** You are debating someone. You are analyzing a deceptive email or a corporate policy. You need to prove *why* something is a lie.

- **Goal:** "Show me the specific trap in this logic."

- **Benefit:** It provides the **Language of Deception** (The Syntax) which is powerful for deconstructing arguments.

### When to use **Core Directive**:

- **Context:** You are writing an essay, analyzing a life decision, or conducting a deep-dive investigation into a company or ideology.

- **Goal:** Total Truth Mapping.

- **Benefit:** It catches *everything*, including competence (Fractal Ratio) and systemic decay (Minimisation).
