# OPINION: The 'Vampire Economy': Why your rental crisis is a bank bailout in disguise

**By Alethekanon** \| *Analysis*

**SYDNEY** — If you want to understand why you cannot afford a home in Australia, stop looking at the person standing next to you in the rental queue and start looking at the structure of the queue itself.

The anger currently directed at Australia’s immigration intake is not irrational. When you see fifty people lining up for a mouldy one-bedroom apartment, or sit in traffic that didn’t exist five years ago, your eyes are telling you the truth: the container is full.

Critics of "Big Australia"—often dismissed as reactionaries by the inner-city intelligentsia—are mathematically correct on three critical points. We *cannot* build infrastructure fast enough to match the current intake. Importing hundreds of thousands of workers *does* suppress wage growth for the working class. And while the country gets richer in aggregate GDP, the individual is getting poorer in a per-capita recession.

These are facts. But if we stop there, we fall into the trap. We mistake the hostage for the hostage taker.

### The Hostage Situation

The Australian economy is currently being held hostage. The hostage taker is a financial alliance between the banking sector and the political class, and their weapon is a \$3 trillion mountain of mortgage debt.

This alliance has a catastrophic problem: the Australian housing market is a bubble. If demand ever normalizes, prices will fall. If prices fall by even 20 per cent, the banks risk insolvency, and politicians lose the "wealth effect" that keeps the boomer voting bloc pacified.

So, they have deployed a human shield.

The government is flooding the country with record numbers of migrants—not because they care about humanitarianism, and not because they are incompetent. They are doing it because excess demand is the only thing stopping the bubble from bursting.

The immigrant is not the invader. The immigrant is the sandbag the government is throwing onto the levee to stop the deflationary water from breaking through. They are being exploited just as much as you are—sold a dream of Australian prosperity only to become battery hens in a rental market designed to extract their savings to service a previous generation's debt.

Every time voters demand the government "stop the boats," they are ignored. Why? Because lowering immigration is effectively pulling the pin on the grenade.

If migration returns to normal levels, rental vacancies rise. Rents fall. Investors panic and sell. House prices crash. The banks fail.

This is the ransom note the government is holding. They are essentially saying: *"Accept the overcrowding, accept the traffic, accept the wage suppression—or we crash the economy."*

### The Feudal Endgame

If we do nothing, the future is already written. The system is not trying to get you back into a home; it is trying to transition you out of ownership entirely.

We are currently in the "Clearance Sale" phase. High interest rates and artificial scarcity are crushing the "Mom and Dad" investors and the aspiring first-home buyers.

Who is left standing? Institutional Capital.

Watch the government's pivot to "Build-to-Rent." They are preparing to hand the keys of the kingdom to Blackrock, superannuation funds, and foreign conglomerates. The solution they will offer is seductive but fatal: *"You will never own a home, but we will provide you a secure, long-term rental in a corporate-owned tower."*

This is corporate feudalism. We risk becoming a nation of permanent serfs, tithed 40 per cent of our income to a faceless corporation that also owns the politicians.

### The Way Out: The Abundance Protocol

Is there an exit? Can we escape the trap without crashing the economy or accepting feudalism?

Yes. But it requires a "system hack." We don't need a revolution; we need to break the mechanism of scarcity.

Currently, the housing market is designed to be high-friction. It takes two years and upwards of \$100,000 in consultant reports just to *ask* for permission to build. This friction protects the big developers—who can afford to land-bank and wait—and kills the small builders.

The solution is an **Emergency Housing Acceleration Package (EHAP)**. The philosophy is simple: weaponize speed.

Under this proposed model, the market splits. If you want to build luxury speculative assets, you stay in the current "Slow Lane." You pay the fees, you wait the years, you fight the council.

But if you agree to build affordable housing (with capped prices) using climate-ready standards, you get the "Fast Lane."

This involves a radical **"Council Surveyance Subsidy."** Currently, every builder has to pay for their own traffic, acoustic, and drainage reports. It is inefficient madness. Under EHAP, the Federal Government would fund councils to create a "Digital Twin" of entire precincts upfront. Traffic modeling, drainage, and heritage would be pre-certified by the council.

This shifts the burden from the applicant to the council. The builder doesn't need to prove the project works; the council has already proven the *zone* works.

Combined with a strict **60-Day Approval Guarantee**, this destroys the land-banking business model. If permission becomes automatic, fast, and free for affordable housing, the value shifts from holding land to building houses.

It would unleash an army of small-scale builders who can't afford a two-year court battle but *can* afford a six-month pre-approved build.

The choice facing Australia is not "Immigrants vs. Locals" or "NIMBYs vs. YIMBYs." The choice is "Artificial Scarcity" versus "The Abundance Protocol."

We are currently fighting over who gets the best view on a sinking ship. We need to stop bailing water and start fixing the hull. The tools are ready. The question is: do we have the will to use them, or are we too addicted to the ransom?
