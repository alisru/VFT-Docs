## Section I: Legislative Capture --- The \"One Big, Beautiful Bill Act\" (OBBBA)

On July 4, 2025, President Trump signed into law the \"One Big, Beautiful Bill Act,\" a sweeping piece of omnibus legislation that stands as the most significant domestic policy achievement of his second term.2 Publicly framed as a populist package of tax cuts for working families, the OBBBA is, in reality, a legislative Trojan horse. Its 870 pages contain a meticulously crafted blueprint for the fundamental and permanent restructuring of the American social contract and the financial underpinnings of the federal government.3 The bill\'s provisions align with precision to the Minimiser objective of weakening domestic institutions and social cohesion, thereby reducing the state\'s resilience to future shocks and accelerating its decline.1

### 1.1 The Omnibus as a Strategic Vehicle

The selection of an omnibus budget reconciliation bill as the legislative vehicle for the OBBBA was a critical strategic choice. Derived from the Latin for \"for everything,\" an omnibus bill packages numerous, often unrelated, topics into a single piece of legislation to be passed with a single vote.5 Historically, this legislative tool has been used for efficiency, particularly to pass complex appropriations at the end of a session when time is short.7 However, it is also a tool that is frequently criticized for its anti-democratic tendencies. By its very nature, it limits debate, prevents detailed scrutiny of individual provisions, and allows controversial measures to be hidden within a \"must-pass\" bill, effectively forcing legislators to vote for policies they might otherwise oppose to avoid outcomes like a government shutdown.9

The OBBBA exemplifies the weaponization of this legislative procedure. Structured as a budget reconciliation package, it was able to bypass the Senate\'s 60-vote filibuster threshold, a procedural hurdle that would have made its passage impossible under normal circumstances.3 This allowed for a transformative agenda, consolidating policy priorities from ten separate Senate committees, to be pushed through Congress with extraordinary speed and minimal deliberation.3 The bill passed the U.S. House of Representatives by a razor-thin 218-214 vote on July 3, 2025, and was signed into law the following day, a date chosen for its symbolic power.3

This approach is not merely a matter of legislative efficiency; it is a deliberate tactic to achieve a substantive outcome that lacks broad political consensus. The process itself becomes a tool for undermining democratic norms. Rather than building consensus through debate and compromise, as envisioned in a deliberative democracy, the strategy exploits a procedural loophole designed for budgetary adjustments to enact a radical restructuring of social and fiscal policy. This circumvention of normal legislative order serves the Minimisation Plan\'s goal of portraying democracy as a flawed and easily manipulated system. It erodes faith in the process by demonstrating that the rules can be bent to serve a narrow, ideological agenda, thereby contributing directly to the \"narrative of decay\" that is central to the Minimiser philosophy.1

### 1.2 Fiscal Restructuring and the Erosion of the Social Contract

At its core, the OBBBA is an act of profound fiscal re-engineering designed to permanently alter the financial capacity and ideological priorities of the U.S. government. The Bipartisan Policy Center estimates the legislation will increase the national debt by approximately \$3.4 trillion over ten years before accounting for interest costs. When additional interest expenses are factored in, the total cost rises to over \$4.1 trillion.\$\^{11}\$ This massive increase in deficit spending is the result of a deliberate and ideologically driven restructuring of the nation\'s tax and spending policies.

#### 1.2.1 Tax Policy as Ideological Statement

The bill\'s tax provisions are bifurcated into two distinct categories: permanent, high-cost benefits primarily aimed at corporations and high-income earners, and temporary, targeted benefits for the middle and working classes. This structure is not accidental; it is a sophisticated legislative maneuver designed to provide a populist \"cover\" for a long-term ideological project.

The fiscal centerpiece of the OBBBA is the permanent extension of the individual tax cuts enacted in the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA), which were originally scheduled to expire at the end of 2025.11 These provisions, which include lower individual income tax rates and a doubled standard deduction, account for the vast majority of the bill\'s cost, reducing federal revenues by an estimated 4.5trillionoverthenextdecade.\^{11}\$ The bill also makes the 37% top individual tax rate permanent and permanently repeals the personal exemption.

In stark contrast to these permanent changes, the OBBBA introduces a series of new, populist tax deductions that are explicitly temporary, effective only from 2025 through 2028.2 These were the provisions highlighted in the bill\'s public marketing campaign and include\$\^{13}\$:

- **No Tax on Tips:** A new deduction for up to 25,000inqualifiedtipincomeforworkersindesignatedoccupations.\^{2}\$

- **No Tax on Overtime:** A deduction for qualified overtime compensation, capped at \$12,500 for individuals and \$25,000 for joint filers.\$\^{2}\$

- **No Tax on Car Loan Interest:** A deduction for up to 10,000ininterestpaidonloansfornew,U.S.−assembledpersonalvehicles.\^{2}\$

- **New Deduction for Seniors:** An additional 6,000deductionforindividualsaged65andolder,whichphasesoutathigherincomelevels.\^{2}\$

This legislative structure perfectly embodies the \"Satan Archetype\" of deception outlined in the \"Framework for the Judgment of Ideas\".12 The temporary deductions for waitresses, nurses, auto workers, and seniors serve as **The Bait**: tangible, easily understood benefits for sympathetic and politically salient groups.13 This bait is then wrapped in **The Cover**: a broad, morally positive narrative of \"tax cuts for working families\" and a plan to \"jumpstart the economy\". This framing makes opposition politically treacherous, casting critics as elitists who are against relief for ordinary Americans. However, the **True Intent** of the legislation is revealed by its structure. The actual goal is twofold. First, it is to use the political cover of the temporary middle-class cuts to make the fiscally unsustainable tax cuts for corporations and the wealthy permanent. Second, it is to engineer a \"fiscal time bomb\" or \"fiscal cliff\" set to detonate in 2028. At that point, a future administration and Congress will face an impossible choice: either allow a massive, politically toxic tax hike on working families to occur just before an election, or make the temporary cuts permanent, thereby locking in an even more catastrophic fiscal trajectory. This is a classic Minimiser tactic of manufactured crisis.1 The bill is not merely a piece of economic policy; it is a trap, designed to make future governance appear chaotic and impossible, further eroding public faith in the system\'s ability to function rationally and responsibly.

#### 1.2.2 Dismantling the Social Safety Net

To partially offset the massive revenue losses from its tax cuts, the OBBBA enacts deep and structural reductions in federal spending on social safety net programs, totaling an estimated 1.4trillionovertenyears.\^{11}\$ These are not minor trims but fundamental changes to programs that form the bedrock of the American social contract.

The largest cuts are aimed at Medicaid, the federal-state health insurance program for low-income Americans. The Congressional Budget Office (CBO) projects that the bill\'s Medicaid provisions will reduce federal spending by 911billionoverthenextdecade,leadingtomillionsofAmericansbecominguninsured.\^{14}\$ The key provisions driving these cuts include:

- **Nationwide Work Requirements:** The bill mandates that, effective January 1, 2027, able-bodied adults aged 19-64 must work or participate in qualifying activities for at least 80 hours per month to maintain Medicaid eligibility. While exemptions exist for certain groups (e.g., pregnant women, parents of young children), this provision is expected to result in significant coverage losses due to both non-compliance and new administrative burdens on enrollees and states.14

- **Restrictions on State Funding:** The legislation freezes state provider taxes at their current levels.15 This is a significant structural change, as many states use taxes on healthcare providers (like hospitals) as a mechanism to help finance their share of Medicaid costs. By restricting this tool, the federal government is limiting the ability of states to maintain and fund their programs.

- **Limits on Noncitizen Eligibility:** Effective October 1, 2026, the bill cancels Medicaid eligibility for most humanitarian entrants, including refugees and asylees, who were previously considered \"qualified aliens\".15 This marks a major retrenchment in the nation\'s commitment to providing a basic safety net for those fleeing persecution.

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), formerly known as food stamps, is also targeted for significant changes. The bill expands work requirements for SNAP recipients and, critically, establishes a new state cost-sharing requirement that begins in fiscal year 2028. Under this provision, states will be forced to pay a portion of SNAP benefit costs---up to 15%---if their payment error rate exceeds certain thresholds.15 This shifts the financial burden of the program onto states, creating a powerful incentive for them to tighten eligibility and reduce their caseloads to avoid financial penalties.

These actions represent a clear and concerted effort to weaken the social safety net that mitigates poverty and social unrest, particularly during times of economic distress. By cutting these programs, the legislation exacerbates economic inequality and reduces the state\'s capacity to maintain social cohesion, aligning perfectly with the Minimiser goal of fostering a weaker, more divided, and less resilient society.1

### 1.3 Undermining Future National Competitiveness

Beyond its immediate fiscal impact, the OBBBA contains provisions that amount to a deliberate, long-term attack on the primary engines of America\'s future economic, scientific, and technological competitiveness: higher education and the green energy sector. These are not simply budget cuts driven by fiscal conservatism; they are strategic decisions to defund and dismantle the infrastructure of the 21st-century knowledge economy.

#### 1.3.1 The Overhaul of Higher Education Financing

The most significant of these provisions is the complete overhaul of the federal student loan system for postgraduate education. Effective July 1, 2026, the OBBBA eliminates the Federal Direct PLUS Loan program for graduate and professional students (\"Grad PLUS\" loans) for all new borrowers.18 For decades, the Grad PLUS program allowed students to borrow up to the full cost of attendance for advanced degrees, a critical financing mechanism for those without personal or family wealth to pay for law school, medical school, or Ph.D. programs.

In its place, the legislation imposes strict new borrowing caps that will dramatically curtail access to postgraduate education. The new annual and aggregate limits are\$\^{18}\$:

- **Graduate Students** (e.g., Master\'s, Ph.D.): \$20,500 annual limit; \$100,000 aggregate lifetime limit.

- **Professional Students** (e.g., J.D., M.D.): \$50,000 annual limit; \$200,000 aggregate lifetime limit.

These limits are far below the actual cost of attendance at most competitive graduate and professional schools, which can often exceed \$70,000-\$80,000 per year. The practical effect of this change will be to make advanced degrees inaccessible for a large swath of lower- and middle-income students, effectively turning postgraduate education into a privilege reserved for the wealthy. The bill also introduces new accountability rules that tie a university\'s eligibility for federal loan programs to the median earnings of its graduates, placing further financial pressure on institutions.\$\^{19}\$

#### 1.3.2 Reversing the Green Energy Transition

In parallel with its attack on higher education, the OBBBA systematically dismantles the federal government\'s support for the transition to a green energy economy. The bill phases out or repeals the clean energy tax credits that were the cornerstone of the previous administration\'s Inflation Reduction Act. This reversal of policy is estimated to reduce federal spending by 543billionovertenyears,a\"saving\"thatisthenusedtohelpfinancethebill′sotherpriorities.\^{11}\$ Simultaneously, the legislation includes provisions to unlock and promote oil and gas development on federal lands, explicitly favoring the fossil fuel industry over renewable energy.13

Viewed through the strategic lens of the Minimisation Plan, these are not accidental or secondary policy outcomes. A nation\'s long-term power is derived from its human capital and its technological and industrial base. By severely limiting access to advanced education, the OBBBA deliberately weakens the country\'s future pipeline of scientists, engineers, doctors, and innovators. By abandoning leadership in the multi-trillion-dollar global green energy industry and doubling down on legacy fossil fuels, it cedes the economic and technological high ground of the 21st century to global competitors. This is a strategy of long-term self-sabotage, designed to accelerate the nation\'s relative decline and diminish its capacity to project power or lead the international order, a core objective of Minimiser actors.1

The legislation appropriates \$150 billion in new defense spending and an additional \$150 billion for border enforcement and deportation operations. The funding for immigration and border security is particularly vast and detailed, amounting to a complete transformation of the scale and scope of federal immigration enforcement. Over a four-year period, the bill allocates over \$70 billion in supplemental funding for U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) and over \$75 billion for U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE).\$\^{17}\$

This massive investment transforms ICE into the single most-funded law enforcement agency in the federal government. The stark contrast between these spending increases and the deep cuts to domestic programs like Medicaid and SNAP reveals the bill\'s true priorities. It represents a direct transfer of wealth and resources from programs that support social cohesion, public health, and poverty reduction to programs that enforce national boundaries and remove noncitizens.

This budgetary realignment is a core element of the Minimiser strategy of manufacturing justification and exacerbating internal divisions.1 By legislatively defining the nation\'s primary challenge as an external threat at the border---rather than internal issues of healthcare access, economic inequality, or educational attainment---the OBBBA serves to deepen societal fissures. ## Section II: Executive Power as a Battering Ram --- The Campaign of Anomalous Domestic Interventions

Concurrent with the legislative capture achieved through the OBBBA, the administration executed a sustained campaign of anomalous domestic interventions from January through September 2025. ### 2.1 Unilateralism in a Polarized Era

The administration\'s heavy reliance on executive power must be understood within the broader context of modern American politics. A significant body of academic analysis has established a direct link between rising political polarization and the expansion of presidential unilateralism.24 As ideological divides in Congress have widened, legislative gridlock has become the norm, making it increasingly difficult to pass major legislation through traditional, bipartisan means.24 This congressional dysfunction creates a policy vacuum that presidents are increasingly tempted to fill through unilateral executive actions, such as executive orders, proclamations, and regulatory changes.24 This dynamic has contributed to a steady shift in the balance of power from the legislative branch to an increasingly imperial executive, a trend observed across administrations of both parties.24

The campaign of interventions in 2025 represents a radical escalation of this trend. Here, executive power was used not merely to bypass a deadlocked Congress on matters of policy, but as an offensive weapon to actively attack and punish the perceived institutional and cultural enemies of the administration. This shift from policy-making to political retribution fundamentally alters the nature of executive authority.

The sheer volume, rapid tempo, and often shocking nature of the executive orders served a strategic purpose: to normalize the use of autocratic tools in the political arena. Each successive \"unprecedented\" order lowered the bar for what is considered an acceptable use of executive power, conditioning the public, the media, and other institutions to a new reality with fewer checks and balances. This deliberate erosion of democratic norms is a foundational tactic of the Minimisation Plan, which seeks to dismantle the unwritten rules and traditions that constrain raw power in a liberal democracy.1

### 2.2 A Multi-Front Assault on Institutional Pillars

The campaign of interventions was characterized by its strategic selection of targets. The administration did not launch a diffuse, random assault but instead focused its fire on the symbolic pillars of the liberal democratic order: the institutions responsible for producing knowledge, upholding the law, and defining social reality. By making high-profile examples of key entities within these sectors, the administration aimed to create a powerful chilling effect that would radiate outward, intimidating potential opponents into silence and inaction.

#### 2.2.1 Targeting the Knowledge Sector (Academia and Media)

A primary focus of the campaign was the nation\'s educational and cultural institutions. On March 20, 2025, the President signed an executive order titled \"Improving Education Outcomes by Empowering Parents, States, and Communities\".28 While framed as a move to reduce bureaucracy and return control to local communities, the order\'s core directive was for the Secretary of Education to \"take all necessary steps to facilitate the closure of the Department of Education\".28 This was a direct assault on the federal government\'s role in enforcing civil rights in education, administering student aid, and supporting disadvantaged students.29

The attack on academia was made more personal and punitive with the proclamation of June 4, 2025, which suspended for six months the entry into the U.S. of any foreign national seeking to study at Harvard University.32 The order cited vague and unspecified \"national security risks\" as its justification, while also directing a review of the visas of current Harvard students.32 This precision-strike against America\'s most prominent university sent a clear message to the entire higher education sector: institutional criticism of the administration could result in severe and disruptive consequences. Public media was also targeted. The administration launched investigations into public broadcasters PBS and NPR and supported legislation that rescinded future funding for the Corporation for Public Broadcasting, aiming to cripple a key source of independent, non-commercial journalism.

#### 2.2.2 Targeting the Legal System and the Rule of Law

The legal profession and the justice system were subjected to a parallel assault. On March 6, 2025, an executive order was issued that barred all federal agencies from using the legal services of Perkins Coie, a prominent law firm with deep ties to the Democratic Party.30 The order went further, suspending the security clearances of the firm\'s attorneys and restricting their access to federal buildings, based on accusations of partisan activity and racial discrimination.34 This unprecedented action was widely condemned as an attack on the constitutional right to counsel and an attempt to punish a firm for its political associations and its representation of clients challenging the administration.35 The order was swiftly challenged in court and ultimately blocked by a federal judge as an unconstitutional retaliation against protected First Amendment speech.35

The administration also asserted direct control over law enforcement by declaring a state of emergency in Washington, D.C., and seizing operational control of the city\'s police force from its democratically elected mayor. In a move that directly challenged the legitimacy of the justice system, the President also issued pardons for all individuals who had been convicted of offenses related to the January 6, 2021, riot at the U.S. Capitol. This action served to retroactively legitimize political violence and signal to supporters that they could act with impunity.

Perhaps the most ambitious aspect of the campaign was its attempt to use executive power to redefine fundamental social and scientific concepts. An executive order issued on January 20, 2025, titled \"Defending Women From Gender Ideology Extremism and Restoring Biological Truth to the Federal Government,\" sought to erase the concept of gender identity from all federal law and policy.37 The order mandated that, for all federal purposes, \"sex\" must be defined as a strictly binary (male or female), immutable biological classification determined at conception.37 It directed all federal agencies to remove references to \"gender identity\" from their policies, forms, and communications; to ensure all government-issued IDs reflect only this binary definition of sex; and to effectively ban transgender individuals from military service.

This was part of a broader flurry of executive orders aimed at imposing a specific cultural and ideological vision on the country by fiat. These actions represented an attempt to use the power of the state to settle cultural grievances and impose a singular, state-sanctioned definition of reality.

### 2.3 The Doctrine of Domestic Strategic Exhaustion

This relentless, multi-front assault creates what has been described as a \"hum\" of chaos, fear, and illogicality throughout the political system. It normalizes a state of perpetual crisis, in which the executive branch can act with increasing impunity because the system\'s immune response has been exhausted and paralyzed. The ultimate objective is to create a chilling effect across all of civil society, forcing institutions into a defensive, risk-averse crouch. This paralysis clears the political battlespace, allowing for deeper and more permanent forms of institutional capture, such as that achieved through the OBBBA, to proceed with minimal effective resistance. This is how a determined minority faction can dominate a disorganized majority: by controlling the tempo and the terms of the conflict, ensuring the opposition is always one step behind, perpetually reacting to the last crisis while the next is already underway.
