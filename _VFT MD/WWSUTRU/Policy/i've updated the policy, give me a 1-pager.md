# **National Energy Network (NEN) Policy: One-Page Summary**

### **Our Vision: Free, Clean, & Reliable Power for Every Australian**

To create a future where every Australian has access to free, clean, and reliable electricity as a basic right, powered by a nationalised, decentralised, and intelligent energy grid that drives economic prosperity, social equity, and environmental sustainability.

### **The Project: A Nation-Building Infrastructure Upgrade**

- **What it is:** The mass installation of government-owned solar systems on \~10 million Australian homes, creating the world's largest distributed power station.

- **How it works:** The network is stabilised by \~50,000 community-level batteries located at upgraded local transformers ("NEN Nodes") and managed by a sophisticated AI-driven smart grid.

- **The Timeline:** A pragmatic and de-risked **9-year implementation timeline (2026-2034)** that balances speed with logistical and industrial reality.

### **Key Policy Innovations**

- **Transitional Subsidy for Universal Relief:** To ensure no one is left behind, a subsidy for all remaining unconnected households will be activated in **2030** (when the NEN meets 75% of residential demand) and run until parity is met in **2031**. This delivers universal relief from power bills **four years ahead of schedule**.

- **Self-Funding & Fiscally Positive Model:**

  - **Total Cost:** **\$70.1 billion** Capital Expenditure.

  - **Funding Source:** **Zero new taxes.** The project is initially funded by an accelerating redirection of direct fossil fuel subsidies (starting at \$3.5B/year and capped at \$14B/year).

  - **Self-Sufficiency:** The NEN begins generating its own revenue from selling grid services in 2030. This revenue stream supplements and eventually **replaces the need for subsidies**, with the project becoming **fully self-funding in its final year**.

- **NEN Infrastructure Trust Fund (NEN ITF):**

  - The project is projected to generate a **\$24 billion national surplus**.

  - This surplus will be managed by the legislated, ring-fenced **NEN ITF**.

  - The NEN ITF has a dual mandate: to provide a contingency buffer for the project and to act as a strategic co-investor to accelerate sovereign manufacturing, creating a powerful synergy with the *Future Made in Australia* Act.

### **National Benefits**

- **Economic:**

  - Permanently **eliminates residential electricity bills**.

  - Creates **150,000 direct and 300,000 indirect jobs** in manufacturing, installation, and technology.

  - Establishes a sovereign Australian manufacturing industry for clean energy technology.

  - Becomes a revenue-generating asset for the nation through grid services and energy exports.

- **Social & Community:**

  - Delivers cost-of-living relief to every Australian household simultaneously via the Transitional Subsidy.

  - Dramatically increases grid resilience and reduces blackouts through its decentralised design.

- **Environmental:**

  - The single most powerful climate policy available, directly eliminating nearly 8% of national emissions.

  - Enables the broader decarbonisation of transport and industry by providing abundant, cheap clean power.

**The National Energy Network is not just an energy policy; it is a comprehensive economic, social, and environmental strategy to secure Australia's prosperity and sovereignty for the 21st century.**
