### **Policy Proposal: The National Energy Network (NEN)**

**For consideration by The Australian Greens**

#### **1. Aim**

To create a future where every Australian has access to free, clean, and reliable electricity as a basic right. The National Energy Network (NEN) is a transformative infrastructure project designed to permanently eliminate residential electricity costs, establish Australia as a global leader in renewable technology, and serve as the foundational pillar for achieving and exceeding our climate targets.

#### **2. Core Principles**

This policy is a direct application of the Greens' Four Pillars:

- **Ecological Sustainability:** The NEN is the single most powerful climate policy available to Australia, eliminating the entire carbon footprint of the residential electricity sector and enabling the decarbonisation of transport and industry.

- **Social Justice:** The policy provides universal access to free electricity, treating it as an essential service. It delivers permanent cost-of-living relief to every Australian, regardless of income or location, via a universal rollout and a transitional subsidy mechanism.

- **Grassroots Democracy:** The NEN empowers every household as a producer in a decentralised network, breaking the dependency on centralised, corporate-owned power generation.

- **Peace and Non-violence:** It builds genuine national security by delivering energy independence, ending our reliance on volatile global markets and securing our critical infrastructure.

#### **3. The Proposal**

The NEN will create the world's largest distributed power station through the mass installation of government-owned solar systems on approximately 10 million Australian households. This network will be stabilised by approximately 50,000 community-level batteries integrated with local transformers ("NEN Nodes"), all managed by a sophisticated, AI-driven smart grid to provide unparalleled reliability. The project will be implemented over a pragmatic and de-risked 9-year timeline.

#### **4. Funding Model: An Investment, Not a Cost**

The total estimated capital cost of the National Energy Network is \~**\$94-110 billion** over the nine-year implementation period.

This nation-building investment is significantly **less than the \$135 billion** projected to be given in taxpayer-funded subsidies to fossil fuel companies over the same timeframe.

The NEN funding model is designed for fiscal responsibility:

- It requires **zero new taxes** on Australians.

- It is funded by the legislated, phased redirection of existing federal fossil fuel subsidies into a permanent national asset.

- The project is so financially robust that it becomes **self-funding in its final year** and generates a **net surplus in funding**, which will be held in a legislated public infrastructure and development support fund.

#### **5. Key Policy Commitments**

Adoption of this policy commits The Australian Greens to the following core actions:

1.  **Establish the NEN:** Legislate the creation of the National Energy Network as a public utility with the mandate to provide free baseline electricity to all Australian households.

2.  **Implement the 9-Year Plan:** Formally adopt the 9-year phased rollout as the primary path to completion, ensuring a sustainable and de-risked implementation.

3.  **Fund via Subsidy Redirection:** Mandate the redirection of federal fossil fuel subsidies to the NEN Infrastructure Trust Fund as the primary funding source.

4.  **Guarantee Social Equity:** Enshrine the Transitional Subsidy Mechanism in legislation, ensuring universal bill relief is delivered to all households from year five, four years ahead of project completion.

5.  **Build Sovereign Capability:** Utilise the NEN's demand and financial surplus to catalyse a domestic manufacturing industry for key renewable components, including Sodium-Ion batteries, as a core part of the 'Future Made in Australia' policy.

6.  **Enable Future Growth:** Establish the NEN Commercial Arm to leverage the network's energy surplus, creating new public revenue streams and driving investment in green industries like green steel and hydrogen.

#### **6. Conclusion**

The National Energy Network is a comprehensive, integrated strategy for national renewal. It is a meticulously planned and fiscally responsible investment that turns a national liability—our expensive, polluting, and fragile grid—into a permanent, productive, and profitable public asset. By adopting the NEN, Australia can secure its energy independence, re-industrialise its economy, and take its place as a true green energy superpower with a prosperous and sustainable future for all its citizens.
