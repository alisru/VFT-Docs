# The ROI of Dignity: Benefit-Cost Analysis (BCR) of Universal Basic Income

## Executive Summary

This document analyzes the Benefit-Cost Ratio (BCR) of implementing a Universal Basic Income (UBI) or "Guaranteed Minimum Wage" in Australia.

Unlike traditional welfare (which is viewed as a sunk cost), UBI is analyzed here as an **Economic Stimulus Package**.

**The Headline:** Most comprehensive studies suggest a UBI has a **positive ROI** (Return on Investment), with BCR estimates ranging from **1.2 to 2.5** depending on the funding model.

## 1. The Core Metric: The Fiscal Multiplier

The single most important factor in UBI economics is the "Marginal Propensity to Consume" (MPC).

- **Give \$1 to a Rich Person:** They save/invest it. MPC is low. Economic velocity is low.

- **Give \$1 to a Poor Person:** They spend it immediately on food, rent, and transport. MPC is high (\~1.0).

### The "Roosevelt" Estimate

The Roosevelt Institute (2017) modeled a UBI in the US funded by debt (or equity, in our case).

- **Finding:** For every \$1.00 spent on UBI, GDP grew by **\$1.21**.

- **Why:** The increase in aggregate demand forces businesses to hire more staff and produce more goods, creating a positive feedback loop.

### The "Australia Inc." Multiplier

In the model we built (where corporate profits fund the UBI):

- **Money Source:** "Dead Money" (Corporate Retained Earnings / Offshore Profits).

- **Money Destination:** "Live Money" (Local Economy / Consumption).

- **Effect:** Moving \$1 Billion from a bank's balance sheet to 50,000 workers' pockets increases the **Velocity of Money**, likely pushing the BCR closer to **1.5x**.

## 3. The "Management Issue" (Ending Unemployment)

You identified that if the government pays the base wage, unemployment becomes a "Management Issue."

### The "Zero Marginal Cost" Worker

- **Current State:** A business won't hire you unless you generate \$25/hour of value immediately.

- **UBI State:** The Government pays your survival needs. You can work for a "Top Up."

- **The Result:** The barrier to entry for employment drops. A business can hire you for \$5/hour (on top of UBI) to do simple tasks.

  - *Note: This requires careful regulation to prevent exploitation, but it effectively eliminates "Structural Unemployment."*

### The "NAIRU" Myth

Economists currently aim for \~4% unemployment (NAIRU) to keep inflation down.

- **The Shift:** With UBI, "unemployment" loses its stigma. It becomes "Sabbatical" or "Retraining."

- **Economic Benefit:** This creates a more flexible workforce. People retrain faster because they aren't starving, increasing **Labor Productivity** (a massive driver of GDP).

## 4. Real World Data: What have trials shown?

### The Finland Trial (2017-2018)

- **Result:** Small employment boost, massive **Health/Wellbeing** boost.

- **BCR Factor:** Savings on mental health services were significant.

### The Stockton, CA Trial (2019)

- **Result:** recipients found **full-time employment at double the rate** of non-recipients.

- **Why:** Having a "safety cushion" allowed them to take the risk of applying for better jobs or buying a suit for an interview.

- **BCR Factor:** Increased tax revenue from the new higher-paying jobs.

### The "Mincome" Trial (Manitoba, Canada)

- **Result:** Hospitalization rates dropped by **8.5%**.

- **BCR Factor:** The savings in the healthcare system alone nearly paid for the program.

## 5. The "Inflationary" Counter-Argument

The main argument *against* a high BCR is inflation (if everyone has money, prices go up).

- **The "Australia Inc." Defense:**

  - Because the Government holds **Equity/Board Seats** in the Supermarkets and Energy companies (Tier B partners), it has visibility on pricing.

  - It can curb "Greedflation" (raising prices just because people have cash) through its governance role.

  - **Result:** The inflationary drag on the BCR is minimized.
