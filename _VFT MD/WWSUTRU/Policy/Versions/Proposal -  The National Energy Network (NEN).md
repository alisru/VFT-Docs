## Proposal: The National Energy Network (NEN)

### Learning from the Past, Building for the Future

### 1. A New Standard in Public Project Planning

Australia has a history of nation-building projects plagued by mismanagement from the get-go. Projects like the National Broadband Network (NBN) were launched with a single, rigid, and overly optimistic plan. When faced with real-world challenges, the lack of a pre-planned contingency led to reactive policy changes, cost blowouts, and delays, damaging public trust.

The National Energy Network (NEN) is designed to break this cycle. It is built on a more sophisticated and robust multi-trajectory framework that learns from the lessons of the past. This business-like approach de-risks the project from the outset by having pre-planned fallback and acceleration scenarios, ensuring its success is not dependent on a single best-case outcome.

- **The Baseline Plan (Proof of Concept):** The core proposal is built on a **"realistic worst-case" financial model**. It uses conservative, current-day costs to guarantee success, projecting a **\$70.1 billion** investment will deliver a **\~\$23 billion national surplus**. This is the benchmark for success.

- **The Fallback Plan (Contingency):** The project has pre-analysed slower timelines (e.g., 10 years) to manage unforeseen systemic delays without project failure. This built-in contingency is a lesson learned from the rigid planning of past projects.

- **The Acceleration Plan (Opportunity):** The project also allows a best-case scenario where efficiencies and economies of scale drive down costs, potentially doubling the national surplus to **\~\$45 billion**. This allows for the acceleration of Stage 2 initiatives, like the National Electrification Scheme.

This transparent, multi-trajectory planning ensures the NEN is a responsible, credible, and achievable investment in Australia's future.

### 2. Vision & Objectives

The NEN is a transformative infrastructure project designed to permanently eliminate residential electricity costs and establish Australia as a global leader in renewable energy and green industry.

**Key Objectives:**

- **Eliminate Household Electricity Bills:** Install government-owned solar systems to provide free electricity to all Australian households.

- **Create a Smarter, Stronger Grid:** Build the world's largest distributed power station, using AI and community-level batteries to ensure grid stability.

- **Drive Economic Growth:** Create an estimated 150,000 direct and 300,000 indirect jobs in future-proof industries.

- **Guarantee Fiscal Success:** Implement a de-risked financial plan based on conservative cost projections to ensure the project is self-funding and generates a significant national surplus.

- **Foster Sovereign Capability:** Use the project's guaranteed success to underwrite the creation of a domestic manufacturing industry for key renewable technologies.

### 3. The Proposed Solution

The NEN involves the mass installation of government-owned solar systems on approximately 10 million households, stabilised by 50,000 community-level batteries.

- **Implementation Timeline:** The baseline plan uses a pragmatic 9-year timeline (2026-2034) to manage logistics, workforce scaling, and procurement effectively.

- **Transitional Subsidy Mechanism:** To deliver cost-of-living relief universally and early, a fully-costed subsidy will activate in 2030, paid for by the project's baseline surplus.

### 4. Funding Model & Economic Impact

The project is designed to be self-funding by redirecting existing fossil fuel subsidies, requiring **zero new taxes**.

- **Baseline National Surplus:** Even under the conservative "worst-case" cost model, the NEN is projected to cover all capital costs, fund the \$10.52 billion transitional subsidy, and still generate a **baseline national surplus of approximately \$23.13 billion**.

- **NEN Infrastructure Trust Fund (NEN ITF):** This baseline surplus will be held in a legislated trust fund, with 50% allocated as a contingency buffer and 50% to co-invest in building sovereign manufacturing capabilities. The actual surplus is expected to be significantly larger.

### 5. Industrial Strategy: A Future Underwritten by Success

The NEN's guaranteed financial success is the engine for a new wave of sovereign manufacturing. The project's massive, certain demand provides the catalyst to build a domestic industry for key components like Sodium-Ion batteries and solar panels.

### 6. Stage 2: National Electrification

The proposal includes a fully-costed Stage 2 initiative, a **National Electrification Subsidy Scheme**, to help 5 million households get off fossil gas. The **\$37.5 billion** program will be funded entirely by the NEN's own financial returns. The larger-than-projected surplus from the acceleration plan could allow this program to be expanded or implemented faster.

### 7. Conclusion

The NEN is not just a vision; it's a meticulously planned and de-risked investment. By building its financial case on a prudent baseline and having pre-planned contingencies and opportunities, it sets a new standard for responsible nation-building. It turns a national liability into a permanent public asset that will pay a dividend to every Australian—in the form of free electricity and a share in a surplus that is poised to grow far beyond its conservative baseline.
