**📄 Policy Proposal: Emergency Housing Acceleration Package (EHAP)**

**Prepared for the Australian Greens – June 2025**

*Author: Jarrod Hamilton - Probationary Member*

**🟢 Purpose**

Australia is in the midst of a **housing emergency**. Yet current federal policy — including the Housing Australia Future Fund (HAFF) — remains **underperforming, slow-moving, and capital-hoarding**, with limited measurable impact. The following policy suite proposes a **scalable, immediately actionable** Greens-backed alternative to deliver **fast, affordable, climate-aligned housing**, without sacrificing quality or fairness.

**🧩 Policy Overview – “EHAP” Framework**

A multi-part strategy combining **overnight regulatory reform**, **developer fee reprioritisation**, and **targeted construction subsidies**, funded primarily from **existing HAFF returns** and **state co-investment**.

**🔧 1. Planning Reform – Immediate Measures**

**✅ A. 60-Day DA Approval Mandate**

- All DAs for residential projects (public or private) to be assessed within 60 days.

- If no decision is made → automatic deemed approval.

- Applies only where developments meet sustainability and affordability targets.

**Rationale:** Current planning timelines stretch to 12–18 months. Speed alone can halve delivery time.

**✅ B. Affordability-Linked Fee & Levy Waivers**

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Type of Project**           **Council/State Fees**   **Conditions**
  ----------------------------- ------------------------ ----------------------------------------
  Market-rate housing           Unchanged                –

  50%+ affordable housing mix   75% discount             20-year affordability deed

  100% affordable housing       **100% waived**          Rental/purchase cap + deed enforcement
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Rationale:** Removes up to \$70,000 in costs per unit. Currently, fees are often used by councils to pad general budgets — disincentivising fast development of affordable supply.

**✅ C. Council Subsidy – Sliding-Scale Backfill**

- Councils reimbursed 35%–50% of forgone fees via federal fund.

- \% scales based on housing crisis intensity (e.g. rental inflation, homelessness rate).

- Sunset clause after 3 years.

**Rationale:** Ensures local governments aren’t penalised for approving affordable housing faster.

**🏗️ 2. Construction Acceleration – RACI Package**

**✅ D. Modern Methods Subsidy**

Subsidies for developers/builders using pre-approved fast, climate-conscious build systems **in affordable housing only**:

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Method**                                   **Subsidy/unit**
  -------------------------------------------- --------------------------
  Modular / prefab                             \$20,000

  Passive House certified                      \$15,000

  3D Printed pilot housing                     \$25,000

  Flat-pack panel builds                       \$10,000
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**✅ E. Materials Rebates**

Rebates on low-carbon, efficient construction materials for affordable builds only:

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Material Type**                    **Rebate %**      **Max/unit**
  ------------------------------------ ----------------- -----------------
  Timber/steel                         20%               \$8,000

  High-efficiency glazing              25%               \$5,000

  Concrete (low-carbon)                15%               \$10,000

  Fixtures/appliances                  30%               \$6,000
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------

**✅ F. Fast-Track Bonus**

- Builds commencing within 60 days of DA, and completed within 6 months, receive a \$5,000 bonus/unit.

- Must use qualifying construction method.

**💰 Financing: Reallocating HAFF**

- HAFF currently returns \~\$500M/year from its \$10B base.

- Instead of trickling funds to private developers or bond aggregators, redirect returns:

  - **To fee/levy waivers**

  - **To fast-build construction grants**

  - **To councils enabling fast affordable approvals**

**Optional:** Allocate **2.5B from principal** to emergency 3-year fund, with public transparency and sunset clause.

**🌱 Climate Alignment**

- All subsidies tied to climate-conscious build standards (NatHERS 7+, embodied carbon reduction).

- RACI supports domestic sustainable construction supply chain.

- Energy-efficient housing cuts long-term costs for low-income residents.

**🌏 Adapting EHAP for Regional vs Metro Areas**

**Australia’s housing crisis varies greatly by location. Effective policy must reflect distinct cost structures, supply bottlenecks, and market dynamics between metropolitan and regional areas.**

**🏙️ Metro Areas (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, etc.)**

- Challenges:

  - High land values (\$150k+ per lot)

  - Complex, overlapping planning zones and stricter environmental regulations

  - Higher labour and materials costs due to demand pressure

  - Greater traffic and site constraints limiting build methods

- EHAP Adaptation:

  - Emphasis on fee waivers & DA time limits to cut red tape in congested LGAs

  - Subsidies to encourage high-density modular/prefab builds (e.g., townhouses, walk-ups)

  - Incentives for brownfield and infill developments to maximise land use efficiency

  - Robust sustainability requirements (NatHERS 7+, embodied carbon targets)

**🏞️ Regional & Rural Areas**

- Challenges:

  - Lower land prices (\$20k–\$80k per lot) but less infrastructure and services

  - Higher logistics and transport costs for materials and labour

  - Smaller local construction markets, less specialised prefab supply chains

  - Greater reliance on smaller builders with less access to capital

- EHAP Adaptation:

  - Increased materials subsidies, particularly for transport-heavy items like steel and concrete

  - Support for mobile modular build units that can be deployed to multiple sites flexibly

  - Simplified planning overlays to speed approvals where ecological sensitivity is lower

  - Funding for local training programs to grow regional construction workforce capacity

**🔑 Key Takeaways**

- Metro solutions prioritise maximising land use via density and strict approval deadlines.

- Regional solutions focus on logistical support and modular flexibility to overcome workforce and supply chain gaps.

- Subsidies and fee waivers must be scaled appropriately to reflect these cost and operational differences, ensuring equity and efficiency.

- This approach allows EHAP to close the housing gap nationwide, not just in capital cities.

**🎯 Summary**

The Greens can lead a pivot away from the HAFF-as-hedge-fund model and toward **immediate delivery of affordable, fast, sustainable housing**. This package:

- Makes affordability **the condition** for receiving public subsidies

- Cuts red tape **without cutting safety or sustainability**

- Leverages the HAFF **as a public housing engine**, not a finance product

- Offers state and federal cooperation **without developer giveaways**
