### Policy Proposal: Emergency Housing Acceleration Package (EHAP)

**An Alternative for Fast, Affordable, Climate-Aligned Housing**

### 🟢 Part 1: Purpose & Overview

#### A. The Housing Emergency

Australia is in the midst of a housing emergency. Current federal policy, including the Housing Australia Future Fund (HAFF), remains underperforming, slow-moving, and capital-hoarding, with limited measurable impact. This policy suite proposes a scalable, immediately actionable alternative to deliver fast, affordable, climate-aligned housing, without sacrificing quality or fairness.

#### B. The EHAP Framework

The Emergency Housing Acceleration Package (EHAP) is a multi-part strategy combining **overnight regulatory reform**, **developer fee reprioritisation**, and **targeted construction subsidies**, funded primarily from existing HAFF returns and state co-investment. Its core philosophy is to change market behaviour by making the delivery of affordable housing the **path of least resistance**.

### 🔧 Part 2: Planning Reform – The 60-Day Digital Pathway

The central goal of the EHAP planning reforms is to cut the Development Application (DA) assessment time from the current 12–24 months down to a mandated 60 days for compliant affordable housing projects.

[[Policy Deep Dive: Redefining Council vs. Applicant Responsibilities]{.underline}](https://docs.google.com/document/d/15yiVwPRkN24Ro5RehB5dWoZKRTneeSZbabv5xuINID4/edit?usp=sharing)

#### A. The Enabling Mechanism: The "Council Surveyance Subsidy"

This reform is made possible through a targeted federal funding stream to local councils.

- **Purpose:** To cover the full cost for councils to commission upfront, precinct-wide master studies and digital certifications for areas zoned for high-density housing.

- **Political Framing:** The offer is positioned as a partnership. Councils can adopt a best-practice, efficient model at no cost, or choose to remain with their slow and expensive legacy systems, making them accountable for their own performance.

#### B. Reformed Roles & Responsibilities

The EHAP model fundamentally redefines the roles of the key actors by shifting the burden of strategic assessment from the applicant to the council.

- **The Council (The Proactive Certifier):** The council's role shifts from a reactive gatekeeper to a proactive enabler. Using the subsidy, they create binding **Precinct-Level Certification Plans**. These are not vague strategic documents; they establish clear, objective, and non-negotiable rules for an entire zone, with detailed controls specified **zone-by-zone, block-by-block, and street-by-street**. This includes:

  - **Traffic & Mobility Plan:** Conducting a master study to pre-determine acceptable traffic impacts, parking rates, and site access solutions.

  - **Integrated Water Management Plan:** Designing a coordinated drainage strategy and specifying the exact engineering requirements for all stormwater connection points.

  - **Precinct Acoustic & Amenity Standards:** Establishing objective standards for noise levels and building materials (e.g., glazing requirements).

  - **3D "Digital Twin" Heritage Model:** Creating a public 3D model that clearly defines protected views and pre-approved building envelopes near heritage items.

  - **Geotechnical & Contamination Database:** Collating existing data to create a "risk map" for the precinct and commissioning investigations on high-risk sites directly.

- **The Applicant (The Compliant Builder):** The applicant's role is simplified to designing a building that complies with the council's public, pre-approved standards and lodging it via a digital portal. Their focus shifts from paperwork to construction. Their responsibility becomes demonstrating compliance through simplified, often automated, means.

- **Specialist Consultants:** The need for most applicant-side consultants (traffic, acoustic, heritage, etc.) is eliminated for compliant projects, as their function is replaced by the council's upfront precinct-level work.

#### C. The New Approval Process

The EHAP model replaces the current manual process with a streamlined, digitally-native workflow.

#### D. Affordability-Linked Fee & Levy Waivers

To further incentivise affordable housing, council and state fees are discounted based on the project's composition, with the federal government partially reimbursing councils via the "Surveyance Subsidy" fund.

### 🏗️ Part 3: Construction Acceleration & Industry Reform

This section focuses on speeding up the physical build time and professionalising the workforce.

#### A. The National Construction Network (NCN)

[[Policy Deep Dive: The National Construction Network (NCN)]{.underline}](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1YU1Tt5LD13DKDiaY2vej__k-4aeFjb58OOdr58rILJ4/edit?usp=sharing)

The NCN is a federally funded digital platform that acts as the central nervous system for EHAP.

- **Logistical Coordination:** It efficiently matches skilled workers with EHAP-approved projects, overcoming regional labour shortages.

- **Worker Empowerment:** It provides workers with a verified professional profile (qualifications, skills, history) and a platform to leave confidential, public reviews of companies based on safety, management, and timely payments.

- **Industry Improvement:** This transparency creates a powerful market-based incentive for companies to improve standards. To be eligible for any EHAP subsidies, companies **must** use the NCN for all project recruitment.

#### B. Construction Subsidies (RACI Package)

Targeted subsidies are available for developers using pre-approved fast, climate-conscious build systems **in affordable housing only**:

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Method**                                   **Subsidy/unit**
  -------------------------------------------- --------------------------
  Modular / prefab                             "\$20,000"

  Passive House certified                      "\$15,000"

  3D Printed pilot housing                     "\$25,000"

  Flat-pack panel builds                       "\$10,000"
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### C. Materials Rebates & Bonuses

- **Rebates:** Percentage-based rebates on specified low-carbon, high-efficiency materials (timber, steel, glazing, etc.) for affordable builds.

- **Fast-Track Bonus:** Builds commencing within 60 days of DA and completed within 6 months receive a **\$5,000 bonus per unit**.

### 💰 Part 4: Funding, Costs & Adaptation

#### A. Financing: Reallocating the HAFF

The HAFF currently returns \~\$500M/year. Instead of trickling these funds through complex financial mechanisms, EHAP redirects these returns directly:

- To fund the **"Council Surveyance Subsidy."**

- To fund the **RACI construction grants and rebates.**

- *Optional:* Allocate **\$2.5B from the HAFF principal** to an emergency 3-year fund for rapid scaling, with full public transparency.

#### B. Climate Alignment

All subsidies are tied to projects meeting high climate-conscious standards (e.g., NatHERS 7+), supporting a domestic sustainable construction supply chain and cutting long-term energy costs for low-income residents.

#### C. Projected Housing Costs & Adaptation

The EHAP model is projected to deliver a net reduction of **\~\$100,000–\$150,000 per affordable dwelling**. The policy framework is designed to be adaptable, with different subsidy levels and logistical supports for high-cost metro areas versus regional and rural areas to ensure equitable outcomes nationwide.

### 🎯 Part 5: Summary & Legislative Path

#### A. Summary

The Greens can lead a pivot away from the HAFF-as-hedge-fund model and toward the immediate delivery of affordable, fast, sustainable housing. This package:

- Makes affordability **the condition** for receiving public subsidies.

- Cuts red tape **without cutting safety or sustainability.**

- Leverages the HAFF **as a public housing engine**, not a finance product.

- Offers state and federal cooperation **without developer giveaways.**

- Introduce as a motion in federal and state parliaments.
