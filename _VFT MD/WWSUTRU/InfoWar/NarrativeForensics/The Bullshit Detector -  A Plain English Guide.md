# The Bullshit Detector: How to Spot a Lie

**A Plain English Guide to Navigating Information**

## The Core Rule: Emotion vs. Logic

The easiest way to trick someone is to make them feel something so strongly that they forget to think. This is the "Hum."

**If a piece of information makes you instantly angry, afraid, or smugly satisfied, stop.**

You are likely being played. Truth is usually boring, complicated, or specific. Lies are exciting, simple, and vague.

## The 3 Warning Signs

### 1. The "Missing Middle" (The Void)

Deception relies on you not knowing how things actually work. It gives you a scary starting point and a disastrous ending point, but deletes the middle.

- **The Lie:** "They are going to destroy the economy!"

- **The Check:** "How? Step by step. What is the specific mechanism?"

- **The Red Flag:** If the explanation skips from step A to step Z without explaining B, C, and D, it is emotional manipulation, not a prediction.

### 2. The "Buzzword Salad" (The Fog)

Smart people use simple words to explain complex things. Liars use complex words to hide simple things.

- **The Lie:** "We are leveraging synergistic paradigms to optimize freedom."

- **The Check:** "Say that again using words a 10-year-old would understand."

- **The Red Flag:** If the sentence collapses into nothingness when you take out the big words, there was never anything there.

### 3. The "Us vs. Them" Trap (The Tribe)

Lies often work by flattering you. They tell you that *you* are smart and good, and *they* are stupid and bad.

- **The Lie:** "Only people like us understand the truth about \[Topic\]."

- **The Check:** "Does this make me feel special? Does this confirm what I already wanted to believe?"

- **The Red Flag:** If the information feels like a warm blanket for your ego, it is probably sedating you, not informing you.

## The 5 Boring Questions That Kill Lies

When you read something in a Canvas (or anywhere else) that feels "off," hit it with these five questions. Lies cannot survive them.

1.  **WHERE is it?**

    - Don't tell me about a "threat." Show me where it is happening physically. Give me a location. If it's "everywhere," it's likely nowhere.

2.  **WHO said it?**

    - Not just the name, but the motive. Does the person telling me this make money if I get angry? Do they get power if I get scared?

3.  **HOW does it work?**

    - Mechanics. Physics. Money. How does the bad thing actually happen? If they can't explain the gears, the machine doesn't exist.

4.  **WHAT is the counter-evidence?**

    - If this were a lie, what would it look like? If the author ignores 50% of reality to make their point work, they are lying by omission.

5.  **WHY now?**

    - Why am I seeing this specific information at this specific moment? Is it to distract me from something else?

## Summary

- **Emotion = Warning.**

- **Vagueness = Trap.**

- **Specifics = Safety.**

Real knowledge is heavy. It has weight (Where), an owner (Who), and a shape (How). If the information feels weightless and makes your heart race, throw it out. It's noise.
