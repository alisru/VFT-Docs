From the perspective of the Minimisation Plan, the announcement of IMEC represented a direct and existential threat to a cornerstone of its economic statecraft. They surrounded the area and began indiscriminately gunning down fleeing attendees with automatic weapons. The attackers showed tactical sophistication, waiting near roadside bomb shelters to kill those seeking refuge and methodically shooting into vehicles caught in the panicked traffic jam. The massacre at the festival lasted for hours, with the first limited IDF presence—a single damaged tank—not arriving until 8:20 AM. Simultaneously, large contingents of militants stormed the kibbutzim and moshavim of the Gaza envelope. At Kfar Aza, a kibbutz of roughly 950 residents, approximately 250 attackers breached the perimeter shortly before 7:00 AM. They systematically moved through the community, focusing first on the "young generation" neighborhood where families with small children lived. Militants burned homes, executed residents, and engaged in a battle with the kibbutz's small security team that lasted for days. The delayed and uncoordinated arrival of IDF forces meant residents were trapped in their safe rooms for over 20 hours. The battle for Kfar Aza resulted in the deaths of 62 residents and 18 security personnel, with another 19 abducted to Gaza.

A similar scene of horror unfolded at Kibbutz Be'eri. At the height of the attack, an estimated 340 militants and looters were inside the community of just over 1,000 people. The attack also had a significant maritime component. At Zikim, on the coast just north of Gaza, Hamas militants attempted a seaborne invasion using at least seven speedboats. They proceeded to massacre 17 civilians who were on the beach that morning before being engaged by a small squad of IDF soldiers. The militants also attacked the nearby Zikim military training base and the Yiftach outpost, killing at least 14 soldiers in fierce fighting before being repelled.

The attacks were not confined to these major sites.
