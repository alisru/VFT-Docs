# OPINION: The Class War You Aren't Seeing (It’s Not About the Boats)

**By Alethekanon** \| *Finance & Strategy*

**SYDNEY** — They want you looking at the boats. They want you looking at the queues. They want you angry at the family who just arrived, desperate for a start, competing with you for a mouldy rental.

As long as you are punching down, you aren't looking up. And up there, in the glass towers of Martin Place, the real architects of your misery are counting their bonuses.

The housing crisis isn't a mistake. It isn't incompetence. It is a **wealth transfer**. It is the largest siphoning of wealth from the working class to the banking class in Australian history.

### The Parasite Economy

We don't have banks that build businesses anymore. We have ticks.

Look at the ledger. Westpac isn't investing in Australian manufacturing. They aren't funding the next CSL or Atlassian. Their latest report shows **\$496.5 billion** of their money is just sitting in residential mortgages.

They are a one-trick pony. They take money from workers (depositors) and lend it to speculators (landlords) to buy houses that workers can no longer afford.

They have turned the Australian dream into a financial product. And like any good parasite, they need the host to stay alive—just barely.

### The 5% Gamble

Here is the part that should make your blood boil. These banks are gambling with *your* future on a 20-to-1 bet.

Their own reports show they only hold about \$5 of equity for every \$100 they lend out. They are leveraged to the hilt. If house prices drop by even 10%—which would make life affordable for you—it wipes them out.

So, the government steps in. Not to help you. To help *them*.

The "Implicit Guarantee" is a fancy economist term for a simple truth: **The government will burn the social contract before they let a bank lose money.**

### Why They Import Desperation

This is why the borders are open. It’s not because Labor loves diversity. It’s not because the Liberals love multiculturalism.

It’s because the Ponzi scheme needs fresh blood.

To keep house prices high (so the banks stay solvent), they need constant, crushing demand. They need to import 500,000 people a year—not as citizens, but as **economic battering rams**.

The migrant is a victim here too. They are sold a lie of Australian prosperity, only to be turned into a "yield unit" for a landlord. They are imported to ensure that vacancy rates stay near zero, so rents stay high, so investors can pay their mortgages, so the banks don't collapse.

It is a machine that eats people—local and migrant alike—and spits out bank profits.

### The Corporate Landlord

And now they have a new plan. Since you can’t afford to buy, they will make sure you rent forever.

Enter "Build-to-Rent." This is the corporate takeover of the Australian home. Blackrock, Super Funds, Macquarie Bank. They want to be your landlord.

Why? Because a corporation doesn't lose its job. A corporation pays on time. It is the perfect serfdom. They get the yield; you get a lifetime subscription to a roof over your head, with annual price hikes built into the contract.

### The Verdict

Don't let them divide us. The struggle isn't between the Aussie battler and the new arrival. We are both standing in the same queue.

The struggle is between the people who work for a living and the institutions that bet on our shelter for a profit. They have taken the economy hostage. It’s time we stopped paying the ransom.
