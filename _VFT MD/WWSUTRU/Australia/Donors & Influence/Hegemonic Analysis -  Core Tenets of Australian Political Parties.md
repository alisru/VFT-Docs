# Hegemonic Analysis: Core Tenets of Australian Political Parties

Analyst: Alethekanon

Date: December 24, 2025

Status: Unconcealed

Methodology: Extraction of "Stated Values" from Party Constitutions, Charters, and National Platforms.

## 1. The Australian Greens

Source: The Charter of the Australian Greens (2010) & National Constitution.

Core Philosophy: Ecological Wisdom & Social Democracy.

1.  **Ecological Sustainability:** The foundational tenet. All human activity must respect the integrity of ecosystems and not impair the resilience of life-supporting systems. The needs of future generations supersede short-term economic gain.

2.  **Grassroots Democracy:** Decisions should be made at the most local level possible. The party structure is non-hierarchical, relying on consensus and participatory decision-making rather than executive authority.

3.  **Social Justice:** Poverty is a systemic failure, not an individual one. The state has a moral obligation to redistribute wealth and power to eradicate discrimination based on class, gender, race, or sexuality.

4.  **Peace and Non-Violence:** A rejection of militarism. Security is achieved through international cooperation, disarmament, and defensive (rather than expeditionary) foreign policy.

5.  **First Nations Sovereignty:** Recognition that sovereignty was never ceded. Unwavering support for Truth, Treaty, and Voice as the prerequisites for national legitimacy.

6.  **Universal Public Services:** Essential services (Health, Education, Transport, Energy, Housing) should be publicly owned and free at the point of use, decommodified and removed from market logic.

7.  **Global Responsibility:** Rejection of parochialism. Australia has an obligation to the global community, including increasing foreign aid, accepting refugees, and cancelling Third World debt.

8.  **Meaningful Work:** The economy should serve the person, not the person the economy. Support for a Universal Basic Income (or liveable welfare) and the reduction of working hours to allow for creative and community pursuits.

9.  **Investment in the Future:** The rapid transition to a post-carbon economy (100% renewables) is not just environmental but an economic imperative for survival.

10. **Civil Liberties:** The absolute protection of the right to protest, dissent, and privacy against state surveillance and corporate overreach.

## 2. Pauline Hanson's One Nation (ON)

Source: One Nation Constitution & Mission Statement.

Core Philosophy: National Populism & Protectionism.

1.  **National Sovereignty:** Australia must be completely sovereign. Rejection of influence from the United Nations, World Economic Forum, and foreign treaties that compromise domestic law.

2.  **Australian Identity:** Promotion of a unified national culture based on "Judeo-Christian values" and the English language. Opposition to "divisive" multiculturalism that creates separate ethnic enclaves.

3.  **Immigration Reduction:** Immigration must be drastically reduced to "net zero" or sustainable levels to protect infrastructure, housing affordability, and social cohesion.

4.  **Economic Protectionism:** Prioritising Australian ownership. Opposition to the sale of farmland, water, and critical infrastructure to foreign (especially state-owned) entities. Support for tariffs to rebuild manufacturing.

5.  **Affordable Energy:** Energy security is paramount. Support for coal and nuclear power to provide cheap baseload electricity. Rejection of renewable energy subsidies and the scientific consensus on catastrophic anthropogenic climate change.

6.  **Individual Rights & Freedoms:** The protection of fundamental liberties, specifically Freedom of Speech (anti-censorship), Property Rights, and the Right to Bear Arms (rejection of further gun restrictions).

7.  **Family Values:** The traditional family is the building block of society. Support for family law reform to ensure equal access for parents (fathers' rights) and opposition to gender ideology in schools.

8.  **Law and Order:** A rigid justice system. Support for truth-in-sentencing, tougher penalties for youth crime, and the deportation of non-citizens who commit crimes.

9.  **Citizen Priority:** The welfare of Australian citizens (Pensioners, Veterans, Homeless) must always come before foreign aid or support for non-citizens/refugees.

10. **Anti-Globalism:** A skepticism of global elites and bureaucracies. A belief that the "political class" has betrayed the "quiet Australians" in favour of international agendas.

## 3. The Australian Labor Party (ALP)

Source: ALP National Platform (2023) & Objective (Socialist Objective).

Core Philosophy: Social Democracy & Trade Unionism.

1.  **The Democratic Socialisation of Industry:** (The "Socialist Objective"). The core constitutional aim is the democratic socialisation of industry, production, distribution, and exchange, to the extent necessary to eliminate exploitation and other anti-social features.

2.  **Fair Work & Unionism:** Labour is not a commodity. The right to organise, collectively bargain, and strike is fundamental. The economy must deliver secure jobs with fair pay, not just corporate profit.

3.  **Universal Provision:** Access to health (Medicare) and education (Gonski/Free TAFE) is a universal right determined by need, not the ability to pay.

4.  **Equality of Opportunity:** The state must intervene to level the playing field. Systematic removal of barriers for women, First Nations people, and minorities through affirmative action and quotas.

5.  **A Fair Go (Egalitarianism):** Opposition to extreme wealth inequality. The tax system must be progressive, ensuring the wealthy pay their fair share to fund the social safety net (NDIS, Aged Care).

6.  **Multiculturalism:** Diversity is a national strength. Australia is a migration nation, and social cohesion is built on inclusion, not assimilation.

7.  **Climate Transition:** Acceptance of climate science. The transition to Net Zero is an opportunity for "Green Jobs" and re-industrialisation, managed by the state to protect workers in fossil fuel industries.

8.  **Strong National Security:** Australia is a middle power that relies on the US Alliance (ANZUS) but seeks engagement with Asia. National security includes border protection ("Stop the Boats") managed with a humanitarian veneer.

9.  **Reconciliation:** Full commitment to the Uluru Statement from the Heart (Voice, Treaty, Truth) to acknowledge and repair the historical wrongs against First Nations people.

10. **Government as Builder:** The state has a proactive role in the economy ("Big Build"). It should build housing, infrastructure, and own strategic assets (like the NBN and Australia Post) rather than leaving everything to the market.

## 4. The Liberal-National Coalition (LNP)

Source: Liberal Party "We Believe" Statement & The Nationals' Mission.

Core Philosophy: Liberalism (City) & Agrarian Conservatism (Country).

### Shared Tenets (The Coalition Agreement)

1.  **Inalienable Rights & Freedoms:** Belief in the fundamental rights of the individual—freedom of thought, worship, speech, and association.

2.  **Lean Government:** The government should be as small as possible. It should nurture incentive by minimising interference, regulation, and taxes.

3.  **Free Enterprise:** The private sector, not the government, is the true creator of wealth and jobs. Competition and market forces are the most efficient allocators of resources.

4.  **The Family:** The family is the primary and fundamental unit of society. Cohesive families are the best social welfare system.

5.  **Rule of Law:** All are equal before the law. A just society depends on the maintenance of law, order, and national security.

6.  **Reward for Effort:** A belief in meritocracy. Those who work hard and take risks should be allowed to keep the fruits of their labour (lower taxes).

7.  **Equality of Opportunity:** All Australians should have an equal start, but the government should not guarantee "Equality of Outcome."

8.  **Constitutional Stability:** Defence of the Australian Constitution, the Monarchy, and the Flag as symbols of stability and heritage.

### Specific National Party Tenets (The "Country" Vector)

9.  **Decentralisation:** The population and economy should be balanced across the continent, not concentrated in capital cities. Regional Australia deserves its "Fair Share."

10. **Regional Security:** The protection of Australia’s food and fibre security. Agriculture and Mining are the backbone of the national economy and must be protected from "Green" ideology.

11. **Localism:** Decisions should be made with a view to their impact on local communities, opposing "City-Centric" policies that ignore rural realities.

### Specific Liberal Party Tenets (The "City" Vector)

12. **Individualism:** The individual is the ultimate focus of policy, not "classes" or "groups."

13. **Fiscal Responsibility:** The government has a moral duty to live within its means, run surpluses, and pay down debt ("Back in Black").
