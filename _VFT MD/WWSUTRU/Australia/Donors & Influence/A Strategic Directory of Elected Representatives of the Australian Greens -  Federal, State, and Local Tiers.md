A Strategic Directory of Elected Representatives of the Australian Greens: Federal, State, and Local Tiers

Introduction: Engaging a Key Political Influencer

This report furnishes a comprehensive and actionable directory of publicly available contact information for all elected representatives of the Australian Greens across federal, state, and local tiers of government. The primary objective is to provide a verified, multi-jurisdictional list formatted for immediate use in large-scale communication and engagement campaigns.

The Australian Greens have firmly established themselves as a consistent force in the nation's political landscape. Their influence extends far beyond their numerical representation in any single chamber. In the Federal Senate and various state upper houses, the party frequently holds the balance of power, making its members pivotal to the passage, amendment, or rejection of critical legislation.

This report is structured to provide not only the contact data but also the crucial jurisdictional context required to develop and execute a tailored, effective, and impactful engagement strategy.

Part I: The Australian Greens in the Federal Parliament

At the federal level, the Australian Greens' influence is primarily concentrated in the Senate, where they maintain a significant voting bloc. Following the 2025 Federal Election, their presence in the House of Representatives has reduced to a single seat, making the Senate the critical forum for legislative negotiation.

Section 1.1: Greens Representatives in the House of Representatives

Following the 2025 election, the Greens' representation in the Lower House is held by a single member in Queensland. While the party no longer holds the seat of Melbourne, this representative remains a vital conduit for the party's policy announcements and parliamentary questions.

Section 1.2: Greens Senators in the Australian Senate

The Australian Senate is the epicentre of the Greens' federal political power. The party maintains a significant bloc of senators, often holding the balance of power. This position allows the Greens to act as a decisive voting bloc on contentious government legislation. Consequently, any federal-level lobbying campaign must prioritize Greens Senators.

Greens senators are highly active in the Senate's extensive committee system. An effective engagement strategy involves communicating with senators about upcoming votes and providing them with well-researched submissions for their committee work. **Senator Larissa Waters** now leads the federal party, with **Senator Mehreen Faruqi** serving as Deputy Leader.
