### B. The "Latent Trigger" vs. "The Kinetic Campaign"

A Double Dissolution requires a specific bill to be rejected twice with a 3-month gap.

- **Scenario A (The Fast Track):** They rush the Security Bills in Feb, demand immediate passage, get rejected, wait 3 months, reject again, election in August 2026.

- **Scenario B (The Dirty Trick):** They *already* have a trigger in their pocket (e.g., a Housing or Industrial Relations bill rejected in 2025). They introduce the Security Bills in Feb 2026. When the Senate "delays" them for inquiry, the PM calls the DD using the *old* Housing Trigger, but fights the *entire campaign* on the "Greens blocking Security Laws."
