- **The Technique:** The process almost always begins with an induction phase, such as progressive relaxation. The hypnotist guides the subject to focus on their breathing, relax their muscles one by one, and enter a state of deep calm.

#### 2. Pacing and Leading: The "Path to Redemption" in Practice

- **The Technique:** The hypnotist first **paces** the subject by validating their immediate, undeniable reality (e.g., "You can feel the chair supporting you... you can hear the sound of my voice... you can feel your eyelids getting heavy..."). - **Attraction Tensor Analysis:**

It is a "truthful lie"—it feels true because it follows logically from what the subject is already experiencing. The **Attraction** is maintained because the suggestion doesn't create a repulsive force; it feels like a natural and inevitable extension of their current state.

#### 3. Guided Imagery & Metaphor: Building a New Worldview

- **The Technique:** The hypnotist creates a compelling, detailed internal world for the subject to inhabit (e.g., "Imagine you are walking down a set of ten steps into a peaceful garden..."). Suggestions are then made within the context of this imagined world.
