## Summary: Plane 3 Moral Vectors

### Highest Positive Vectors (Greater Good):
1. **The Moon Landing** (+1.0, +1.0) — Absolute apex of expansive possibility.
2. **The Homesteader / Property** (+1.0, +0.8) — Ownership of land as the bedrock of freedom.
3. **Mobility** (+0.9, +0.9) — The physical manifestation of liberty through the automobile.
4. **The Railroad** (+0.9, +0.9) — Binding the continent into a singular empire.

### Highest Tension & Negative Vectors (Greatest Lies):
1. **Climate Change** (-0.9, +0.8) — Devastating planetary cost of high-velocity industrial infrastructure.
2. **The First Nations / Manifest Destiny** (-0.8, +0.9 / -0.6, +1.0) — The immense moral contradiction of conquering a continent under the divine banner of freedom.
3. **Gerrymandering** (-0.8, +0.9) — Weaponization of the map to subvert the political will.
4. **The Ruin** (-0.5, -0.8) — Total geographic exhaustion where extraction hollows out meaning (Greater Evil).

---

> **Plane 3 Totality Judgment:**
> Plane 3 is remarkably high in Will (+ψ) but structurally troubled in Morality (+υ). The American Land is not a passive backdrop; it is heavily engineered through infrastructure (Railroad, Interstate, Dam) and commodified as pure Property. This leads to the ultimate triumphs of the **Moon Landing** and total **Mobility**, but exacts a terrible moral cost along the way (Displacement of First Nations, Imperial Expansion, Climate Change). The "Where" is frequently conquered through the "Greatest Lie" vectors—justified by the belief that expanding American territory inherently expands American liberty.
