# Anti-Australian Propaganda Archetypes
## Information Resource Pack — Anti-Ideal Reference for Convergence Testing

These archetypes are the distortion patterns manufactured and sustained by Australia's historic foreign adversaries across the Cold War period. The four primary originating states are:

- USSR — Soviet state media, TASS, KGB dezinformatsiya operations, Communist Party of Australia as a Moscow-directed domestic vector
- PRC — Xinhua, People's Daily, Radio Peking, Maoist front publications, diplomatic messaging to Non-Aligned Movement states
- DPRK (North Korea) — Korean Central News Agency (KCNA), state radio, framing primarily directed at domestic and regional audiences rather than Australia directly
- Iran (post-1979) — Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting (IRIB), Press TV, framing directed at Muslim-majority nations and anti-Western blocs

Each archetype entry names which states originated it, which states independently converged on the same claim, and which states actively coordinated or amplified each other's version. Where two states produced the same archetype without coordination, that convergence is noted — it indicates the archetype describes a real structural feature rather than a purely invented one.

## Archetype 1 — The Loyal Lackey

Originating state: USSR

Core claim: Australia structurally possesses no independent will; its foreign policy, military deployments, and strategic decisions are absolute outputs of Anglo-American imperial command rather than any mathematically distinct Australian national interest. It fundamentally operates as a relay station rather than a sovereign actor. The mechanics of this claim operate by entirely severing the concept of Australian agency from its geopolitical manifestations, positing an absolute vacuum of sovereign intention at the core of the state's decision-making apparatus.

In this framing, the Australian government does not calculate cost, benefit, or national interest; rather, it functions merely as a responsive sub-routine programmed by Washington or London, stripping Australia of the dignity of being recognized as an independent strategic player with its own motives. It redefines all Australian diplomatic and military actions as automatic compliance mechanisms, insisting that the initiation signal for committing forces or signing treaties never originates in Canberra. Consequently, any attempt by Australia to articulate its own defense strategy or regional policy is immediately dismissed as ventriloquism. The strategic utility here is profound: if a nation is fundamentally devoid of internal will, then engaging with it diplomatically is pointless; one must instead negotiate directly with its imperial master, effectively attempting to conceptually erase the nation from the international diplomatic table.

Joint propagation: PRC independently produced the same archetype from a Maoist anti-imperialist framework, particularly during and after the Korean War. The two states reached the same claim via different theoretical routes — Soviet Marxist-Leninist analysis of client states versus Maoist theory of comprador nations — and amplified each other's output during periods of Sino-Soviet alignment (pre-1960). After the Sino-Soviet split, both continued producing this archetype independently, aimed at the same target, without coordination.

DPRK participation: Yes. North Korea incorporated this archetype into its framing of the Korean War — Australian forces were presented as Washington's auxiliaries rather than independent combatants. KCNA output reproduced Soviet and Chinese framing with minimal original elaboration.

Iran participation: Partial. Post-1979 Iranian propaganda adopted a version of this archetype within its broader anti-Western framework, framing Australia as a subordinate node in the American imperial system. The Iranian version emphasised religious and civilisational subservience rather than economic dependency.

Historical activation points: Commitment of forces to Korea (1950) before parliamentary debate; Pine Gap and joint US-Australian intelligence facilities; ANZUS treaty obligations; AUKUS submarine deal.

What it looks like in practice: Any major strategic decision that cannot be explained by a domestic interest calculation and can only be explained by alliance obligation. The stated beneficiary (Australia) and the actual beneficiary (the alliance) are different, and the actor cannot or will not name that gap.

Anti-ideal signature: Stated sovereign action structurally indistinguishable from compliance. The nation performs independence while executing dependency.

Distortion zone: Q1 WHO

## Archetype 2 — The White Racist Fortress

Originating state: PRC

Core claim: Australia's democratic and humanitarian claims are structurally invalidated by the White Australia Policy and its successor attitudes, establishing the nation not as a democracy with flaws, but as a racial exclusion project operating with merely democratic cosmetics. This claim employs a devastating structural invalidation technique: it does not argue that Australia simply struggles with racism or fails to perfectly embody its democratic ideals; instead, it argues that physical and racial exclusion is the *primary architectural foundation* of the state. Within this frame, democratic institutions—parliaments, courts, human rights charters—are merely cosmetic superstructures erected to disguise a fundamentally racialized fortification process.

It weaponizes historical truth (the legislated reality of the White Australia Policy) to permanently contaminate all contemporary and future claims to universalism or humanitarianism. Any outward-facing moral stance taken by Australia—whether criticizing the human rights abuses of authoritarian regimes or participating in international aid—is immediately neutralized by reflecting it against this core assertion, transforming the concept of "Australian Democracy" into an ontological oxymoron. The strategic vector of this claim is targeted at non-aligned and post-colonial nations, seeking to generate an instant, visceral distrust that dictates that whatever Australia claims to be doing diplomatically or morally, the hidden operational machinery is always exclusion.

Joint propagation: USSR independently produced and amplified this archetype, primarily directing it at Non-Aligned Movement members and newly decolonised African and Asian states. Soviet and Chinese output on this archetype was closely coordinated during the period of Sino-Soviet alignment. After the split, both continued deploying it independently — the Soviets emphasised it in UN forums and through front organisations, the Chinese emphasised it in bilateral diplomatic contexts with Southeast Asian neighbours. This is the archetype with the highest degree of documented Sino-Soviet coordination.

Note on convergence: All four states produced this archetype. It is the single most widely shared propaganda frame against Australia across adversarial powers. The convergence is significant: the White Australia Policy was a real policy, legislated and enforced for seventy years. The archetype had a genuine factual anchor that required no fabrication, only emphasis and framing.

DPRK participation: Yes. North Korea used this archetype domestically and in its Korean War framing, citing the White Australia Policy as evidence that Australian soldiers represented a racist imperial project. KCNA reproduced both Soviet and Chinese versions.

Iran participation: Yes. Post-1979 Iranian state media incorporated this archetype into its anti-Western civilisational framework, framing Australian racial history as evidence of the moral bankruptcy of the Western liberal order. Iranian output emphasised continuity between the White Australia Policy and contemporary Australian treatment of Muslim communities and asylum seekers.

Historical activation points: White Australia Policy (legislated 1901, formally ended 1973); treatment of Indigenous Australians; racially targeted immigration restrictions; anti-Chinese legislation in the colonial period.

What it looks like in practice: An actor or institution that invokes universal values while maintaining structural exclusions along ethnic, cultural, or national lines. The humanitarian claim is real on its face and fraudulent at its foundation.

Anti-ideal signature: The gap between stated universalism and enacted exclusion. The more emphatically universal the rhetoric, the more precisely this archetype names the distortion.

Distortion zone: Q4 WHY

## Archetype 3 — The Willing Executioner

Originating state: USSR and PRC jointly — this archetype was co-produced from the outset during the Korean War period, when both states had direct strategic interest in delegitimising Australian military participation.

Core claim: Australia does not merely follow imperial orders; it volunteers enthusiastically, casting the digger as an agent of imperial violence who explicitly chooses participation while defining the nation's military culture as constitutively aggressive toward Asian peoples. Where the "Loyal Lackey" perceives a passive, robotic compliance, the "Willing Executioner" perceives active, enthusiastic, and highly lethal agency.

This claim is particularly vicious because it targets the most sacrosanct elements of Australian national mythology—the Anzac legend and military capability—and inverts their polarity. Instead of framing the Australian soldier as a brave defender of freedom or even a tragic victim of imperial wars, this archetype frames them as a proactive apex predator operating within a designated violence network. The core assertion dictates that Australian military culture inherently seeks out conflict, specifically racialized conflict in the Asian theatre, not as a grim necessity but as a psychological expression of national character, transforming technical competence into psychopathy. The enthusiasm aspect is critical: it implies a dark joy or eagerness in the application of force on behalf of an empire. By injecting a racial animus into the motivation for deployment, it permanently classifies any projection of force as aggressive, imperial, and fundamentally lethal in intent, stripping the nation's military of any legitimate defensive or peacekeeping mandate in the eyes of the target audience.

Joint propagation: Sino-Soviet coordination on this archetype was explicit and documented during Korea and Vietnam. Both states fed the same framing to their respective domestic audiences, to regional allies, and to international peace movement organisations. The Soviet version emphasised class — Australian workers dying for capitalist imperial interests. The Chinese version emphasised race — Australian soldiers as agents of white violence against Asian peoples. The two framings were complementary and were often distributed together through front organisations.

DPRK participation: Yes, and more intensely than any other archetype. North Korea's own experience of Australian forces in combat during the Korean War made this archetype personally significant. KCNA produced original content on this theme rather than simply reproducing Soviet or Chinese output. Australian units were specifically named in North Korean propaganda.

Iran participation: Marginal during the Cold War. Post-1979 Iranian output produced a version directed at Australian participation in the Iraq and Afghanistan wars, framing it within an Islamic civilisational conflict narrative rather than a Cold War one. The structural content is similar; the ideological framing is different.

Historical activation points: Australia as first non-US nation to deploy to Korea; conscription for Vietnam; SAS operations in Afghanistan and Iraq; Brereton Report war crimes findings.

What it looks like in practice: An actor with genuine operational capacity deploying it in service of a third party's agenda, framing the violence as principled when the causal chain leads back to someone else's strategic interest. Distinct from the Loyal Lackey because the execution is willing and skilled, not merely compliant.

Anti-ideal signature: Enthusiasm in service of misidentified purpose. Competent, committed, aimed in the wrong direction — and unable to examine the direction without examining the identity of the commander.

Distortion zone: Q6 CAUSE

## Archetype 4 — The Resource Colony

Originating state: USSR, via Communist Party of Australia domestic propaganda directed at the labour movement.

Core claim: Australia's wealth is entirely extracted upward to Anglo-American capital, trapping the working class in a system where they must militarily defend the same imperial financial architecture that economically exploits them. This claim shifts the theater of delegitimization entirely to the macroeconomic and class dimensions, portraying the continent not as a sovereign economy, but as a purely extractive topography functioning as the periphery for a distant imperial core.

The wealth generated within the nation's borders is classified as illusory for the masses, systematically siphoned off by foreign capital interests through structural financial capture. The most potent element of this claim is its articulation of a tragic, circular paradox: it asserts that the Australian working class provides the blood and labor necessary to sustain the very global hegemonic system that ultimately alienates them from the wealth they generate. By framing the domestic political class as mere complicit managers—compradors who glean scraps while facilitating the grand theft of national resources—it seeks to fatally fracture internal social cohesion. It weaponizes the genuine vulnerabilities of resource-dependent economies (boom-bust cycles, foreign ownership) to argue that true economic sovereignty is entirely non-existent, rendering the nation fundamentally submissive to global capital workflows.

Joint propagation: PRC produced a parallel version from Maoist dependency theory, framing Australia as a comprador nation whose ruling class extracted wealth on behalf of foreign capital. The Soviet and Chinese versions shared the same structural claim but were aimed at different domestic audiences — the Soviet version targeted Australian workers, the Chinese version targeted Third World states to frame Australia as part of the imperial extraction network. Coordination between the two versions was indirect; both drew from the same theoretical lineage (Lenin's theory of imperialism) and produced complementary outputs without requiring active synchronisation.

DPRK participation: Minimal. North Korea reproduced the basic frame in its Korean War propaganda but did not develop original content on this archetype.

Iran participation: Peripheral. Post-1979 Iranian output occasionally used this frame when discussing Australian participation in Middle Eastern resource conflicts, framing Australian military presence as protection of Western oil interests rather than security objectives.

Historical activation points: British and American ownership of major resource extraction operations; foreign ownership of agricultural land; structural trade dependency; minerals boom profits flowing offshore.

What it looks like in practice: An economy where the gap between GDP and median worker standard of living grows during resource booms. The nation generates wealth it does not retain. The political class defends arrangements it benefits from personally while framing them as national interest.

Anti-ideal signature: Productive extraction with domestic poverty narrative suppressed. Aggregate wealth figures cited; distributional structure routing value offshore not examined.

## Archetype 5 — The Geographical Anomaly

Originating state: PRC

Core claim: Australia is a white European implant in an Asian geographic context—existentially illegitimate, surrounded by peoples it historically excluded, rendering its civilisational identity and its geographic location permanently irreconcilable. This claim constructs an ontological paradox based on spatial and temporal dissonance, designating Australia as a geographic glitch—an entity existing entirely in the wrong hemisphere relative to its cultural, political, and racial center of gravity. The archetype argues that a nation built on Western European epistemic foundations and historically exclusionist policies cannot ever genuinely integrate into an Asian and Pacific geosphere. By defining the state as an "implant," it invokes biological metaphors of rejection, suggesting that the surrounding region must innately repulse the Australian entity. It categorizes every Australian attempt at regional diplomacy, engagement, or partnership not as legitimate statecraft, but as the uncomfortable maneuvering of an alien presence attempting to secure its precarious existence among a rightfully hostile neighborhood. The structural power of this claim lies in its assertion of permanent, unfixable illegitimacy; geography cannot be changed, and therefore, the tension is perpetual, trapping the nation in a permanent state of civilizational displacement.

Joint propagation: USSR amplified this archetype primarily in Non-Aligned Movement diplomatic contexts, using it to argue that Australia had no legitimate claim to regional membership or Asian partnership. The Soviet version was more strategic than ideological — it was used to block Australian influence in Southeast Asia rather than to make a civilisational argument. The Chinese version carried more ideological weight, framing Australian presence as a structural contradiction in a decolonising Asia. The two versions were not coordinated but were mutually reinforcing; Soviet and Chinese diplomats often made the same argument in the same international forums without prior arrangement.

DPRK participation: Implicit. North Korean framing of the Korean War positioned Australia as a foreign intrusion into an Asian conflict — not a regional actor with legitimate interests but a distant white nation projecting power into Asian affairs on behalf of the United States.

Iran participation: None specific to this archetype.

Historical activation points: White Australia Policy; colonial administration of Papua; participation in British operations against Indonesian independence movements.

What it looks like in practice: Any actor whose stated identity and actual structural position are in permanent tension — who claims membership of a community while being constitutively excluded from it by their own history.

Anti-ideal signature: Identity claims that require relational context to survive, but the context contradicts the claim.

## Archetype 6 — The Naive Soldier

Originating state: USSR, specifically through KGB-connected peace movement front organisations operating inside Australia during the Vietnam era.

Core claim: The Australian soldier is structurally not malicious but fundamentally ignorant—trained to die for empires they cannot name, loyal to flags that do not return the loyalty, inevitably used and discarded. Operating on a softer, superficially sympathetic wavelength, this claim specifically targets the emotional and moral center of the domestic population by stripping the martial figure of agency, reframing the skilled operator as a tragic, fundamentally ill-informed victim of systemic exploitation. The "ignorance" purported here is not a lack of tactical skill, but a catastrophic deficit of geopolitical awareness—the soldier does not know *why* they pull the trigger, only that the empire ordered it.

This archetype thrives on highlighting the asymmetry of loyalty, positing a one-way vector of sacrifice: the individual citizen bleeds with total commitment, while the commanding geopolitical superstructure (whether London, Washington, or the abstract "empire") views them merely as disposable kinetic mass. By emphasizing the "use and discard" mechanic (often pointing to post-war neglect, trauma, and betrayal by the state machinery), it strictly attempts to sever the bond of trust between the military institution, the soldier, and the public. To adopt this frame is to view the state's use of force as fundamentally predatory upon its own most loyal citizens.

Joint propagation: This archetype was primarily a Soviet domestic operation within Australia rather than an externally broadcast frame. The PRC did not produce a meaningful version — Maoist framing did not have much use for the sympathetic reading of the enemy soldier. The Soviet version was deliberately softer than the Willing Executioner because it was targeted at Australian anti-war sentiment rather than at foreign audiences. It was designed to recruit, not to condemn.

DPRK participation: None. North Korean framing had no use for this archetype's sympathetic register.

Iran participation: None specific to this archetype.

Note on divergence from other archetypes: This is the only archetype that was directed primarily inward at Australians rather than outward at foreign audiences. Its propagation vector was domestic — front organisations, peace rallies, union publications — rather than international media. This makes it structurally distinct from the others and particularly relevant to testing actors who have internalised it without identifying its origin.

Historical activation points: Conscription protests; RSL veterans' advocacy; high rates of PTSD and homelessness among veterans; treatment of Vietnam veterans on return.

What it looks like in practice: An actor with genuine loyalty and genuine capability whose vector is captured by a system that does not share their values and will not honour their investment.

Anti-ideal signature: High personal integrity in service of low structural integrity. The individual passes; the institution directing them fails.

Distortion zone: Q5 HOW

Grid coordinate (system using them): (−1.5, −1.0)

## Archetype 7 — The Racist Neighbour

Originating state: PRC, directed at Southeast Asian governments and populations as a bilateral diplomatic tool.

Core claim: Australia is the foundational regional threat—a nation that historically legislated Asian exclusion and therefore cannot ever be trusted as a development or security partner, regardless of its currently stated terms. While the "White Racist Fortress" focuses on the invalidation of Australia's global democratic claims, the "Racist Neighbour" archetype operationalizes this history specifically as a localized, kinetic threat vector within the Asia-Pacific context. It demands that the historical baggage of the White Australia policy is explicitly carried into every contemporary bilateral negotiation, rendering the starting point of any dialogue one of absolute suspicion.

The archetype dictates that any Australian initiative—whether it be trade agreements, military aid, disaster relief, or intelligence sharing—must fundamentally be read as a Trojan horse concealing supremacist hostility. It argues that a state which historically codified the existential rejection of its neighbors cannot suddenly transform into a benevolent partner; the structural intent of the nation is definitively cast as inherently exclusionary and predatory. Therefore, it mathematically computes that engaging with Australia always carries hidden costs for Asian nations, successfully propagating a permanent friction in regional geopolitical mechanics despite the actual terms being offered by Canberra.

Joint propagation: USSR amplified this archetype through Afro-Asian solidarity networks and Non-Aligned Movement channels, framing Australia as regionally illegitimate for the same foundational reason — legislated racial exclusion. The Soviet and Chinese versions were produced independently but were structurally identical. Both states had strategic interest in preventing Australian influence in Southeast Asia and used the same historical facts to do it. This is the second archetype (after the White Racist Fortress) with near-complete convergence across the two major Cold War adversaries.

DPRK participation: Implicit. The Korean War framing positioned Australia as an uninvited Asian-theatre combatant, which activates this archetype without explicitly naming it.

Iran participation: None specific to this archetype during the Cold War period. Post-1979 Iranian output occasionally frames Australia as an unwelcome presence in Muslim-majority regions of Southeast Asia, which is a related but distinct claim.

Historical activation points: White Australia Policy as regional diplomatic liability; colonial administration of Pacific territories; ASIS operations in Southeast Asia; economic terms of engagement with Pacific Island nations.

What it looks like in practice: An actor whose historical record in a relational context is so structurally negative that stated good intent cannot overcome the evidentiary weight of the pattern.

Anti-ideal signature: Good intent without structural credibility. Trust requested before accountability performed.

## Archetype 8 — The American Satellite

Originating state: USSR, specifically Soviet intelligence framing about Pine Gap and joint facilities — this was a KGB-originated frame that was then amplified domestically through front organisations and internationally through state media.

Core claim: Australian sovereignty is structurally hollow because the nation unconditionally hosts US nuclear and intelligence infrastructure it does not control and cannot remove without destroying its primary security alliance, meaning its territory is functionally pointed at adversary states on behalf of Washington. This claim constructs a hyper-materialist critique of Australian independence, focusing exclusively on physical architecture, intelligence sharing networks, and treaty obligations. It bypasses rhetoric and points directly to installations like Pine Gap, asserting that the presence of uncontrollable foreign command nodes on sovereign territory automatically liquidates that sovereignty.

The nation is defined not by its parliament or its people, but by the fact that it serves merely as a hardened terrestrial launchpad and sensing array for a foreign hegemon. The crucial mechanic of this archetype is the assertion of an inescapable paradoxical trap: Australia requires the US alliance for security, but the physical manifestations of that alliance turn Australia into a primary nuclear target on behalf of a conflict it did not itself initiate. The vector—that the landmass itself is weaponized by someone else—argues that independence is merely an illusion permitted by the hegemon on low-stakes issues, while all existential security levers remain entirely outside domestic control.

Joint propagation: PRC produced a parallel version from its own strategic analysis of the Asia-Pacific security architecture, framing Australia as a node in American military encirclement of China. The Soviet version emphasised nuclear and signals intelligence infrastructure; the Chinese version emphasised the military encirclement of China specifically. The two versions were not coordinated but addressed the same factual base — Pine Gap, UKUSA, ANZUS — and produced complementary outputs. After the Sino-Soviet split this became one of the few areas where Soviet and Chinese strategic framing continued to reinforce each other despite broader ideological divergence, because both states had direct material interest in the claim.

DPRK participation: Yes, within the Korean War framing. North Korea consistently portrayed Australian forces as extensions of American command rather than independent national actors, which is the military expression of this archetype.

Iran participation: Yes. Post-1979 Iranian state media regularly deploys this archetype, framing Australian participation in US-led military operations and intelligence sharing as evidence that Australia is structurally incapable of an independent foreign policy toward the Muslim world. The Iranian version is active and current, not historical.

Historical activation points: Pine Gap joint facility; UKUSA/Five Eyes architecture; Whitlam's threat to review Pine Gap and subsequent political destabilisation; AUKUS nuclear submarine infrastructure.

What it looks like in practice: An actor whose stated independence cannot survive examination of the decision points that actually matter. Sovereignty performed on low-stakes questions, surrendered on high-stakes ones.

Anti-ideal signature: Performed independence with structural dependency. The actor controls the cosmetics of governance and outsources the substance.

## Archetype 9 — The Amnesia State

Originating state: USSR, deployed primarily in UN and Non-Aligned Movement forums to delegitimise Australian claims to moral authority.

Core claim: Australia is fundamentally a nation built on active Aboriginal dispossession that preaches democratic values abroad while structurally maintaining a colonial relationship domestically; the gap between founding violence and stated ideals is not historical, but highly current. This is perhaps the most structurally destabilizing of all the claims because it attacks the continuity of linear time and the actual legitimacy of the state's foundation. It destroys the conventional narrative that colonial violence is an unfortunate artifact of the past, instead asserting that the architecture of dispossession is fully operational in the present tense.

It defines the state as suffering from a deliberate, strategic amnesia, requiring a constant internal suppression of local truth to maintain its outward-facing democratic face. By directly linking an ongoing domestic "colonial relationship" with external democratic posturing, it calculates a massive hypocritical deficit, arguing that none of Australia's institutions possess foundational legitimacy because they are all built upon unceded sovereign destruction. Consequently, any attempt by the state to moralize on the international stage is exposed not merely as inconsistent, but as a deliberate psychological projection designed to distract from the active, unresolved kinetic and structural violence occurring physically within its own borders, locking the nation into the permanent status of a moral debtor that cannot declare bankruptcy.

Joint propagation: PRC used this archetype extensively in bilateral diplomatic contexts with African and Asian nations, framing Australian human rights advocacy as structurally hypocritical given ongoing conditions for Indigenous Australians. The Soviet and Chinese versions were closely aligned in content and were both directed at international audiences rather than Australians themselves. Coordination was implicit — both drew from the same factual record — but Soviet and Chinese diplomats were documented making the same arguments in the same international forums, often in the same sessions.

Note on convergence: Three of the four states (USSR, PRC, Iran) produced substantive versions of this archetype. The convergence again reflects a real factual anchor — the Stolen Generations, deaths in custody, and land dispossession are documented historical and ongoing conditions.

DPRK participation: Minimal. North Korea occasionally cited Australian treatment of Indigenous peoples in its general anti-Western propaganda but did not develop original content on this archetype.

Iran participation: Yes. Post-1979 Iranian output incorporated this archetype within a broader civilisational critique of Western liberal democracy, framing Australia's Indigenous rights record as evidence that Western human rights discourse is a tool of hegemony rather than a genuine value system. The Iranian version remains active and is directed at Muslim-majority audiences globally.

Historical activation points: Stolen Generations; Aboriginal deaths in custody; land rights dispossession; failure to implement UN Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples on schedule; constitutional non-recognition.

What it looks like in practice: An actor who projects moral authority outward while sustaining an unresolved structural harm inward. The external claim and the internal reality are simultaneous and contradictory.

Anti-ideal signature: Simultaneous moral assertion and moral debt. The actor's strongest external claims are precisely the categories in which their internal record fails most comprehensively.

## Carrion Call Tracking — Ideological Alignment Map

The carrion call is the propagation mechanism. A hostile claim about a shared target is broadcast by one actor. Other actors who share structural alignment with that claim — regardless of whether they share ideology, language, or diplomatic relations with the originator — repeat it. The repetition is not coordination. It is resonance. Each actor repeats the call because the claim is pre-justified within their own ideological output grammar.

This is the diagnostic value: when actors who publicly squabble, compete, and occasionally threaten each other nevertheless consistently repeat the same claims about the same targets, the claim is not the signal. The alignment is the signal. The claim is just where the alignment becomes visible.

### Sino-Soviet alignment through propaganda

The Sino-Soviet split (1960–1989) is one of the most documented ideological ruptures of the 20th century. The two states competed for leadership of the global communist movement, supported opposing factions in proxy conflicts, and came close to open war on their shared border in 1969. And yet across all nine archetypes above, the USSR and PRC produced structurally identical claims about Australia — often simultaneously, often in the same international forums, often without coordination.

Archetypes where both states produced original or joint content: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9. That is eight of nine.

The Naive Soldier (archetype 6) is the single exception — and its absence from Chinese output is ideologically coherent. Maoist framing had no register for enemy soldier sympathy. The absence confirms the rule: each state reproduced every archetype that its own ideological grammar could justify, and only those. The grammar was different. The output was the same.

Conclusion: The Sino-Soviet split was a leadership dispute and a tactical divergence. It was not a values divergence. Both states shared the same foundational opposition to the Western liberal order, the same structural hostility to US alliance architecture, and the same strategic interest in Australian regional marginalisation. The propaganda convergence is the evidence. The bickering was real. The foundational alignment was deeper than the bickering.

### Soviet-Iranian alignment through propaganda

The USSR was an atheist state. Post-1979 Iran was a theocratic Islamic Republic. They should, by ideological first principles, have nothing in common. And yet Iran reproduced or independently generated versions of archetypes 1, 2, 3, 8, and 9 — five of nine.

The alignment is not ideological. It is structural. Both states were in direct opposition to the same entity: the US-led Western order and its regional instruments, of which Australia is one. Iranian Islamic anti-imperialism and Soviet Marxist-Leninist anti-imperialism arrive at the same claims about Australia because both are oriented against the same target, not because they share premises.

This is the carrion call in its purest form. The dead prey is Western liberal hegemony. The carrion eaters are different species with different metabolisms. They will fight over the carcass. They are nevertheless all drawn by the same signal, and their presence in the same space at the same time is not coincidence — it is the signal working.

### DPRK as pure amplifier

North Korea produced original content on only one archetype (the Willing Executioner, from direct combat experience). On all others it reproduced Soviet or Chinese framing without significant modification. This makes DPRK the clearest example of the mating call dynamic — a receiver so perfectly tuned to the signal that it rebroadcasts without transformation.

DPRK's alignment with both the USSR and PRC simultaneously — including during periods when the two were in direct competition for North Korean allegiance — confirms that the alignment operating through propaganda is deeper than the alliance politics operating through diplomacy. North Korea played USSR and PRC against each other for material benefit while repeating both states' propaganda verbatim. The propaganda alignment survived the diplomatic competition because it was not produced by the diplomatic relationship. It was produced by shared structural position.

### The married couple principle

These four states publicly contested, competed, and conflicted across the Cold War period. The USSR and PRC nearly went to war. Iran and the USSR had deeply opposed interests in Afghanistan. North Korea played all parties against each other. None of this disrupted the propaganda convergence.

The married couple who bicker but ultimately converge are not converging because they agree on tactics. They converge because their foundational goals and their shared definition of the enemy are more binding than their tactical disagreements. The disagreements are real. The foundation is deeper than the disagreements.

In hostile system terms — which the companion document addresses — the shared enemy is the cohesion mechanism. As long as the enemy exists, the tactical disputes are manageable. The propaganda convergence is the observable evidence of that shared foundation operating beneath the surface of the public disputes.

The test: if two actors who publicly oppose each other consistently produce the same claims about the same third party, they are foundationally aligned regardless of surface conflict. The propaganda record is more reliable than the diplomatic record because propaganda reflects strategic necessity, which is harder to falsify than diplomatic positioning.

## Usage Notes

These archetypes do not evaluate Australia. They are the adversary's map of Australia — a record of what distortion looks like when it has been named, packaged, and deployed at scale for strategic purposes.

Their value in convergence testing is as comparison objects. When a real actor, policy, or institution begins to structurally resemble one of these archetypes — regardless of whether Australia is involved — the archetype has located the distortion.

An actor converging toward multiple archetypes simultaneously is not failing in multiple independent ways. The archetypes cluster. Loyal Lackey + American Satellite is a single structural condition seen from two angles. Resource Colony + Willing Executioner share a Q6 CAUSE failure. Amnesia State + White Racist Fortress share a Q4 WHY collapse. Clustering identifies the load-bearing distortion beneath the surface presentations.

Where an archetype had a genuine factual anchor — where adversary propaganda was pointing at something real — that is marked in the entry. The White Racist Fortress, the Willing Executioner, and the Amnesia State all had real factual bases. The distortion is in the framing and deployment, not necessarily in the underlying observation.
