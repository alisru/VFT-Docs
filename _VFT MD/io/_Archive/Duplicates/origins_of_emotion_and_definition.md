## 1. The Electrochemical Mechanism of Emotion

Emotions are not chemical events that happen to the brain. They are the readout of a precise electrochemical execution sequence, triggered by specific patterns of ion movement across neuronal membranes.

The ions involved are:

- **Na⁺** (sodium) — primary carrier of the depolarisation signal
- **K⁺** (potassium) — restores resting potential after firing
- **Ca²⁺** (calcium) — the specific trigger for neurotransmitter release
- **Cl⁻** (chloride) — primary mediator of inhibitory signalling

These ions move through voltage-gated and ligand-gated channels embedded in the neuronal membrane. The timing, direction, and magnitude of their movement determines whether a neuron fires, and with what character — tonic, bursting, or phasic.

### The Transmission Chain

The chain from ion movement to felt emotion runs as follows:

```
Ion flux across membrane
→ Membrane potential change
→ Action potential threshold crossed
→ Action potential propagates down axon
→ Ca²⁺ influx at presynaptic terminal
→ Vesicle fusion with membrane
→ Neurotransmitter release into synapse
→ Receptor binding at postsynaptic membrane
→ Downstream signal cascade
→ Circuit-level pattern of activation
→ Felt emotional state
```

Calcium is the critical node. It is not the ratio of all ions together that triggers release — it is specifically Ca²⁺ concentration crossing a threshold at the presynaptic terminal that causes synaptic vesicles to fuse and release their contents.

### What Encodes Emotional Specificity

The ions are the **medium**. They are not the message.

Emotional specificity — why one state is fear and another is joy — is encoded at a higher level of organisation:

- **Which neural circuits are active** — amygdala, prefrontal cortex, anterior cingulate, anterior insula, nucleus accumbens, periaqueductal gray, hypothalamus
- **Which neurotransmitters are released** — dopamine, serotonin, norepinephrine, oxytocin, GABA, glutamate, endorphins
- **Which receptor subtypes bind them** — D1 vs D2, 5-HT1A vs 5-HT2A, α vs β adrenergic, etc.
- **The timing and rhythm of firing patterns** — burst vs tonic, frequency, synchronisation across regions

The electrochemical implementation is perfectly specified at the molecular level. The specification comes from somewhere above it.

## 2. Willpower as Direct Invocation

A person can induce a full emotional state — physiologically real, not imagined — with no external stimulus and no internal physiological trigger. The mechanism is directed intention alone: hold the instruction "match this feeling" and the feeling manifests.

This is not a metaphor or an approximation. The same circuits activate. The same neurotransmitters release. The same body-state changes occur. The feeling is real by every available measure.

### The Prefrontal Cortex as Invoker

The prefrontal cortex — particularly the medial and ventromedial subdivisions — has direct descending projections into every major emotional processing region:

- **Amygdala** — fear processing, salience tagging, emotional memory retrieval
- **Hypothalamus** — autonomic regulation, hormonal axis, homeostatic drive
- **Periaqueductal gray (PAG)** — visceral tone, pain modulation, affect
- **Nucleus accumbens** — reward valence, motivational salience
- **Anterior insula** — interoceptive mapping, felt body-states

When deliberate emotional recall or construction is initiated, the PFC activates, retrieves a stored emotional template, and broadcasts a reconstruction signal down these pathways simultaneously.

### The Confirmation Loop

The body does not passively receive this signal. It responds to it — autonomic changes occur, heart rate shifts, muscle tone changes, hormonal release begins. These body-state changes are then read back up through interoceptive pathways (primarily the vagus nerve and anterior insula) into conscious awareness.

The felt emotion is the result of this confirmation loop:

```
Intention
→ PFC activates
→ Emotional memory template retrieved
→ Descending signal to limbic structures
→ Autonomic and hormonal response
→ Interoceptive signal returns upward
→ Felt emotion intensifies and becomes real
```

The brain does not cleanly distinguish between experiencing an emotion and accurately simulating one. Antonio Damasio's **somatic marker hypothesis** identifies emotions partly as body-state readouts — and the PFC can forge those readouts from the top down. The body follows the instruction, confirms it back, and the feeling is indistinguishable from one caused by an external event.

## 3. Emotion Is Generative, Not Reactive

The standard model of emotion is:

```
External stimulus → Internal response → Felt emotion
```

The willpower experiment falsifies this as a complete account. If the full physiological cascade can be initiated with no external stimulus, then the external stimulus was never load-bearing. It was one path of invocation among at least two.

The corrected model is:

```
Pattern (definition of target state)
→ Execution (physiological cascade)
→ Confirmation (interoceptive feedback)
→ Felt emotion
```

Emotion is not a response. It is the **execution of a stored program**.

External events are one way of calling that program. Directed intention is another. The difference is not in the output — the output is the same physiological event. The difference is in the invoker.

### What This Means for the Self

Most people experience emotion as something that happens to them. The mechanism reveals that the self is always upstream of the feeling — it has simply been delegating the invocation to circumstance by default.

Removing that delegation — treating both body and universe as external and issuing the pattern-match instruction from a position outside them — produces authored emotion. Not fake emotion. Not performed emotion. Physiologically real emotion, initiated by will rather than by event.

This is a fundamentally different relationship to experience:

- **Received emotion**: stimulus calls the program, self reads the output
- **Authored emotion**: self calls the program, body runs it, self reads the output

The program is identical. The position of the self relative to it is not.

## 6. Habit: Authorship Compiled Into Automatic

The final structural finding concerns what happens when emotional authorship is repeated.

Each time a specific emotional state is written deliberately:

- The circuit fires
- Hebbian reinforcement occurs: neurons that fire together strengthen their connections
- The activation threshold for that circuit decreases
- The probability of spontaneous firing in that pattern increases
- Over sufficient repetitions, the circuit reaches a threshold where it fires without deliberate invocation

This is habit.

A person does not have a fixed nature that must be managed. A person has a **current compiled state** that reflects the history of what they have repeatedly defined themselves as.
