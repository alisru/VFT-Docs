# The p·t Moral Primitive

## The Fundamental Quantity

The base moral metric is integrated possibility space over time.

    good  =  Δ(p·t) > 0    →    net expansion of available futures
    bad   =  Δ(p·t) < 0    →    net contraction of available futures
    evil  =  Δ(p·t) < 0 before natural end    →    closing possibilities before their time
    saintly  =  Δ(p·t) > 0 under Δ(p·t) < 0 pressure    →    keeping good possibilities open in the face of evil

No reference to harm, flourishing, dignity, or any culturally loaded term. Those are downstream approximations of this single quantity.

## The Chain Rule

Any sequence of actions forms a causal chain. If any node in that chain produces Δ(p·t) < 0 and is not cancelled, the chain is bad regardless of the stated intent of any other node.

This is not an average — it is a minimum. A chain that builds a hospital but requires a genocide to fund it does not net to positive.

## The Double Negation Property

bad + bad = good is only true under one condition: the second bad act is specifically directed at eliminating the first bad act's p·t contraction.

    If A  →  Δ(p·t) = −δ
    and B eliminates A  →  Δ(p·t) = +δ
    then B is good, regardless of B's surface appearance

Destruction of a tyranny is a p·t expansion event. The label applied to the mechanism is irrelevant. The direction of the p·t vector is what matters.

## The Confusion of Sainthood and Good

Good is the baseline. Δ(p·t) > 0 is what a functioning person executing their role correctly produces automatically. A doctor who treats patients is producing good. A builder who builds soundly is producing good. That is not praiseworthy — it is the definition of the role being executed at specification.

Saintly is specifically the case where the system is actively producing Δ(p·t) < 0 and the actor maintains expansion anyway at personal cost. It requires absorbing the pressure that would normally close the possibility and neutralising it with will.

What the vocabulary collapse produced:

    Good   →   mislabelled as saintly when performed visibly
    Saintly   →   invisible because it looks quiet, or mislabelled as foolishness because it's costly

Praising someone for doing their prescribed job to the prescribed level is category error. It inflates the currency of moral praise and simultaneously makes actual saintly action unreadable — because the vocabulary no longer distinguishes between them.

## Cross-Tradition Confirmation

This distinction is not culturally local. Every major tradition that developed a serious moral vocabulary arrived at the same separation.

**Biblical**

Luke 17:10 is explicit — "when you have done all that you were commanded, say: we are unworthy servants, we have only done what was our duty." Doing the job is not grounds for praise. The martyrs and prophets are saintly not because they did good but because they maintained expansion under active lethal opposition. Isaiah 42 Servant — does not break the bruised reed, does not quench the smouldering wick — is the precise image of saintly: holding what is almost closed open, at cost, against pressure. Job is the clearest case — saintly is explicitly tested by the application of evil and defined by survival of that test with will intact.

**Taoism**

Tao Te Ching Chapter 17 — the best leader, when the work is done, the people say "we did it ourselves." The sage produces good without claiming the production. Chapter 8 — highest good is like water, benefits all without striving. Wu wei is not passivity — it is the absence of the ego claim on the outcome. The Taoist saintly figure is invisible precisely because they maintained without noise. The confusion Jarrod identifies — praising visible performance — is what the Tao explicitly warns against. Acclaim is a signal of misattribution, not virtue.

**Hinduism**

Bhagavad Gita — nishkama karma, action without attachment to fruit. Krishna's instruction to Arjuna is direct: do your dharma without expectation of praise or reward. The act of correct duty execution is not grounds for pride — it is simply the function of the role. Saintly in the Hindu framework is the yogi or the karma yogi who maintains correct action under conditions specifically designed to produce failure — the battlefield being the literal image of evil pressure applied to force closure.

**Buddhism**

The Bodhisattva vow is the most structurally precise encoding of saintly in any tradition. The Bodhisattva delays their own liberation specifically to hold the door open for all beings still inside the closing system. That is the exact formal definition — Δ(p·t) > 0 maintained under maximum Δ(p·t) < 0 pressure, at the cost of the actor's own exit. Mahayana explicitly distinguishes between merit accumulation (good, passive) and the Bodhisattva path (active maintenance under pressure). The confusion of the two is what produces the lesser vehicle critique — mistaking personal good accumulation for the full moral task.
