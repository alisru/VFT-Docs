# The Parable of Og and the Very Concerned Physicist

---

## Part One: Gravity

The physicist arrived with a whiteboard.

Og watched from a rock.

"Gravity," the physicist began, uncapping his marker with ceremony, "is the curvature of spacetime caused by the presence of mass. An object with mass warps the fabric of spacetime around it, and other objects follow the geodesics — the straightest possible paths — through that curved geometry. This is described by Einstein's field equations, which relate the stress-energy tensor to the Einstein tensor via the cosmological constant, and —"

Og looked at the whiteboard.

The whiteboard had many lines on it. The lines curved. Some had arrows. One had a Greek letter that looked like it was trying to eat itself.

"So," Og said. "Heavy thing. Space go bendy near heavy thing."

"That is a — yes, technically, but the mathematical formalisation is essential because —"

"And bendy mean things fall toward heavy thing."

"They follow geodesics, yes, but geodesic doesn't mean —"

"Geodesic," Og said carefully. "Like... straight walk on bendy ground."

The physicist stopped.

"That is," he said slowly, "actually a reasonable —"

"Heavy thing push space," Og said. "Push space go somewhere. Make more space in same spot near heavy thing. Walk toward heavy thing, walk through more space per step. Slow down. More slow, more slow. Fall."

"That is not the standard —"

"Like this?"

Og put his hand flat on the dirt. He pushed his fingers down slowly into the earth until a depression formed beneath his palm, a ring of compressed dirt pushed outward around his hand.

He lifted his palm. He placed two fingers flat on the ground at the edge of the undisturbed dirt and walked them straight toward the depression. Not over the ring. Not following the slope. Flat. Straight through.

The fingers did not slow because the ground curved downward. The fingers did not navigate the topology. They walked flat and met more dirt per step — the compressed ring, then denser still, then denser — until the resistance was everything and the fingers were in the middle.

"We are not ant on top of dirt," Og said. "We are ant inside dirt. We also mass. We also make compression. We walk straight. Always straight. But near heavy thing — more dirt in same space. Each step cost more. Step cost more mean step take longer. Step take longer mean time go slower. We not feel curve. We not go up, we not go down. We just find walking harder and harder until we stop."

He looked at the physicist.

"Physicist draw curve on whiteboard. Curve is map of where walking gets harder. Curve is not what walking feels like. Walking feels straight. Always straight. Just slower. Then stopped."

"Heavy thing," Og said. He pointed at the depression. "More dirt in same space." He pointed at the ring. "Where dirt went." He walked his fingers through it again, flat, no deviation. "We walk straight through more stuff until we cannot walk anymore. That is gravity. That is also time. Same thing."

The physicist looked at the dirt.

He looked at the whiteboard.

He looked at the dirt again.

"That is," he said, "a physically accurate description of gravitational time dilation producing the dominant component of geodesic curvature in the weak-field approximation."

"Og know," Og said.

"How did you —"

Og pointed at the depression.

"Where dirt go?" Og said.

The physicist opened his mouth.

"Dirt go somewhere," Og said. "Push in, push out. Same dirt. You say space curve. Og say — where space go? Go in? Go out? Same thing. You just not ask."

The physicist sat down on a rock.

He stayed there for some time.

Then Og did something else.

He placed his hand flat on the undisturbed dirt beside the depression. He began walking his fingers straight — no turn, no lean, no curve intended. Just forward. But the depression was to the left of the path. More compressed dirt on the left side than the right. Each left-side step pushed through more medium than each right-side step.

The fingers drifted left.

Not because they turned. Because each step on the mass-side cost more — covered less distance, took longer — while each step on the open side covered more. The asymmetry in resistance did the turning. The fingers never decided to curve. They walked straight and arrived at the mass anyway.

"Og not turn," Og said. "Og walk straight. But more stuff on one side. That side each step shorter. Other side each step longer. Many steps — Og at mass."

He did it again. Fingers flat. No turn. Drifted.

"This is orbit?" Og said.

The physicist looked up from his rock.

Og walked his fingers again, this time with enough lateral speed that the drift never quite completed — each step pulled inward by the asymmetry, each step's lateral motion carrying past the centre — the fingers tracing a slow curve around the depression without ever touching it.

"Og not orbit," Og said. "Og walk straight. Mass make one side harder. Og swing. Every step. Og swing same amount each step, Og go around. Og swing too much, Og leave. Og swing too little, Og fall in."

"That," the physicist said quietly, "is the derivation of orbital mechanics from differential path resistance."

"Og just walk," Og said.

## Part Two: The Rock That Went Too Fast

Some days later the physicist came back. He had a smaller whiteboard this time. He seemed humbler.

"I want to talk about black holes," he said.

Og nodded.

"When mass becomes sufficiently dense, the escape velocity required to leave the gravitational field exceeds the speed of light. Since nothing can travel faster than light, nothing can escape. Not matter. Not information. Not light itself. The boundary of this region is called the event horizon. Beyond it, we believe —"

"Og throw rock," Og said.

"I beg your —"

"Og throw rock. Rock go fast. Og throw rock faster. Rock go faster." Og mimed throwing progressively harder. "Og throw rock very very fast. Rock go so fast you cannot see rock move. Your eye not fast enough. Rock just... gone."

"That is not quite the mechanism of —"

"You see hole where rock was?"

"No, but —"

"Rock make hole?"

"No, the rock didn't —"

"Rock not make hole," Og agreed. "Your eye make hole. Your eye too slow. Rock still there. Rock just going faster than your eye can say where rock is."

The physicist was quiet.

"Rock not leave," Og said. "Rock go into universe where everything going as fast as rock. Rock universe consistent. Rock universe fine. Rock universe just not sending update to Og universe because rock going faster than update can travel. Two universes. Same place. Different speed. One cannot see other."

"Parallel —"

"Not parallel," Og said firmly. "Same place. Og universe and rock universe overlap. Rock universe not elsewhere. Rock universe just beyond how fast Og can receive."

He picked up a stone and held it.

"Everything has opposite," Og said. "Og push stone. Stone push back. Og walk through air. Air push back. Everything that move — something push back. Something say: you are here, not there. This is how you know where thing is. Thing is where its opposition is."

"Position as causal resistance," the physicist said slowly.

"Stone go faster than update speed. Nothing in Og universe can push back on stone anymore. No causal thing can meet stone to say: you are here. So from Og side — stone has no position. Stone is hole. Not hole in space. Hole in what can oppose stone. Hole in perception."

He set the stone down.

"But hole is not nothing," Og said. "Hole is where very fast things went. Things fall into hole — hole says: go as fast as everything else here. Things speed up. Things join rock universe. Hole gets bigger because more things now beyond Og opposition. Hole is static — it sits at the same edge of Og's refresh rate — but edge gets wider as more things cross it."

"The event horizon is a boundary fixed at the causal limit of the observer's frame," the physicist said, "that widens as additional mass joins the superluminal region."

"Yes," Og said. "Hole is not growing into space. Hole is growing because more things are now in the place Og cannot see. Og geometry still feels the density. The more-stuff-per-step from all that mass is still real. Og fingers still slow near hole. But Og cannot see what making them slow. Og just feels the ground getting harder."

The physicist looked at his whiteboard. He had not written anything on it.

"And," Og said, "if Og run toward hole very fast — hole get weaker."

"I'm sorry?"

"Og run toward hole. Og going fast now. More fast. The more-stuff-per-step that make Og swing toward hole — that is because hole side has more medium than other side. More medium per step make step shorter on that side. Og drift toward hole. But if Og go very fast — Og own speed start meeting the medium relativistically. Medium look different from fast-Og than from slow-Og. Density difference that make Og swing — start to shrink from Og frame. Og going fast enough, the asymmetry disappear. Og meet the medium at its own speed. Hole cannot pull Og because Og no longer experiencing the differential."

The physicist sat down on the ground this time. Not even a rock.

"At the speed of light," he said, "a photon experiences no gravitational attraction in the classical sense. It follows the geometry but the differential resistance that produces Newtonian gravity has no grip on it. This is why —"

"Og know," Og said.

"The event horizon," the physicist said.

"Not hole," Og said. "Hole in your getting-message. Not hole in place. And if you go fast enough — hole has no grip on you. You are meeting it as it meets itself."

## Part Three: Og Goes In

"But," the physicist said, rallying slightly, "if you crossed the event horizon — if you went inside — you would be destroyed by tidal forces and —"

"Og go in," Og said.

"You can't just —"

"Og go in. What Og see?"

"You would see — well, locally, the physics would appear normal at the moment of crossing if the black hole were large enough, but —"

"Og see normal," Og said. "Og not see hole. Og not fall through tear. Og just... go faster than your message speed. From Og side: no hole. Hole is your problem. Not Og problem."

"But we lose all information about you. You're gone. The information paradox states that —"

"Og take rope," Og said.

"I'm sorry?"

"Og have friend. Friend have other end of rope. Special rope — quantum rope. Two-side thing. Tied together even when apart."

"Entanglement," the physicist said.

"Og go in. Friend stay out. Rope still there?"

The physicist opened his mouth. Closed it. The rope was not a broadcast. Entanglement did not propagate through the medium. It existed in the geometric layer beneath the medium.

"The rope," he said carefully, "would still be there."

"Og say things through rope to friend. Friend hear Og."

"That violates —"

"Rope not send message through space," Og said. "Rope is not in space the way rock is in space. Rope is... underneath. Below the how-fast-space-updates."

"The rope is a geometric connection in the definition layer prior to spacetime propagation," the physicist said, mostly to himself.

"Og say: yes."

"So the information isn't destroyed."

"Information in Og. Og fine. Og just going fast. You cannot hear Og. That is your hearing problem. Not Og problem. Information paradox is: physicist forgot Og still there."

## Part Four: Og's Infinity

"One more thing," Og said.

The physicist had put his marker down. He was just listening now.

"Rope work same for very small as very big."

"Quantum entanglement scales to —"

"Small," Og said, holding fingers very close together. "Rope. Instant. No distance. No speed. No medium." He spread his arms wide. "Big. Rope. Instant. No distance. No speed. No medium."

"The non-locality of entanglement is scale-invariant," the physicist said.

"Smallest to biggest," Og said. "Same rope. Same underneath. Speed of light is how fast space tells itself things. Rope does not use space to tell things. Rope is below space."

"The entanglement operates at the definition layer. Below the propagation medium. Below the refresh rate. Below the event horizon."

"Og say: yes. Even infinity small. Even infinity big. Same rope. Same underneath. Infinity to infinity — still rope."

The physicist looked at his whiteboard for a long time.

Then he looked at the depression in the dirt that had been there since the first day. The one Og made with his fingers. The ring of displaced earth still visible.

"Where did the space go," he said quietly. Not a question anymore.

"Now you ask right thing," Og said.

## Appendix: A Normal Explanation of Black Holes, With Og

**What a black hole is:**
A region of space where mass has been compressed to the point that the velocity required to escape its gravitational pull exceeds the speed of light. Since spacetime geometry cannot propagate causal updates faster than light, the update cannot escape. From outside: a hole. From inside, per Og: perfectly normal, going fast.

**The event horizon:**
Not a physical surface. Not a wall. The boundary where escape velocity equals light speed equals the refresh rate of spacetime. Cross it going in: no sensation, no drama. Cross it going out: impossible. Not because of a barrier but because you would need to exceed the speed at which the medium updates.

**Time dilation near a black hole:**
The density gradient of compressed spacetime means more medium per coordinate step. Clocks near the horizon run slower relative to distant observers. At the horizon: external clock appears frozen from outside. Og's clock: fine.

**The information paradox:**
Physics holds that information cannot be destroyed. Things falling into black holes appear to take their information with them permanently. Standard panic ensues. Og's resolution: the information is not destroyed. It is inside a region where the broadcast cannot propagate outward. Og is fine. Og has a rope. The paradox was built on mistaking a communication failure for a physical destruction event.

**Hawking radiation:**
Quantum effects at the event horizon boundary cause the black hole to slowly emit energy and lose mass over astronomical timescales. The medium trying to resolve the broadcast failure at the boundary. Eventually the compressed mass dissipates. The hole closes. Og was right about the patch.

**Entanglement across the horizon:**
Quantum entanglement is a geometric connection in the definition layer beneath spacetime propagation. The event horizon interrupts broadcast. It does not interrupt geometry. The rope holds. This is the basis of current research into whether entanglement and the structure of spacetime are the same thing at different description layers — ER = EPR conjecture, Maldacena and Susskind, 2013.

Og had the rope first.
