## III. The Relativistic Mapping Principle

The overlay is a relativistic mapping system. This means:

1. The grid has no fixed geographic anchor. The origin point (0,0) is defined by the map calibration, not by a permanent physical location. In the Levant overlay, the origin falls approximately at the Jordan River / West Bank junction — the most contested zero-point on earth. A place no one owns and everyone claims.

2. The coordinate values are frame-relative. A location's coordinate on the Hegemony Grid describes its structural moral function within this frame, not an absolute moral verdict on the place or its people. Haifa at (+1,+1) does not mean Haifa is morally superior — it means Haifa occupies the Greater Good structural position in this frame.

3. Frames can shift. If the origin is redefined — say, anchored to Jerusalem rather than the river junction — all coordinates shift accordingly. The structural relationships between locations (distances, quadrant positions, relative vectors) remain invariant. The absolute labels change. This is the relativistic property.

4. The Jordan River as the υ-axis. In the current calibration, the Jordan River runs approximately along the zero line of the beneficiary axis. West of the river: contested, high-energy moral claims. East of the river: group-maintenance orientation, lower ψ energy. The physical boundary and the moral axis coincide — not by design, but because rivers are historically where the question "who does this benefit" is most violently adjudicated.

## V. The Jordan River — Geographic Drift Over 2000 Years

### The Channel Itself

Macro position: The Rift Valley that contains the river is a tectonic graben — it does not move on human timescales. The valley walls are fixed. The river's lateral position within the Zor (the inner floodplain) has always meandered, but the valley bounds the meander range to roughly 1–2 km of lateral variance at any point.

Sinuosity: The lower Jordan meanders so extensively that although it runs 215 km, it only covers 105 km of direct distance between the Sea of Galilee and the Dead Sea. This meander pattern has shifted continuously, but always within the same Zor corridor. At 2000 CE the river's sinuosity had increased by approximately 25% relative to the 1850 baseline, largely due to the dramatic drop in Dead Sea levels (approximately 35 metres since 1960, at ~1 metre per year).

Flow volume: This is the major change. Natural flow was approximately 1.3 billion cubic metres per year. Current flow reaching the Dead Sea is 30–50 million cubic metres per year — approximately 2–4% of historical flow. The river at the time of the scriptural record was a functioning, seasonally flooding watercourse with dense riparian forest (the ga'on hayyarden — "swelling" or "pride" of Jordan). Today it is in many places a polluted stream 2 metres wide.

### Lake Hula — The Vanished Northern Feature

At the northern end, Lake Hula was a substantial swampy lake feeding the upper Jordan. It was drained in the 1950s. In the 1st century CE it was a papyrus-forest swamp so dense its internal course could not be traced. The entire northern water geography has been transformed by drainage and the National Water Carrier (built 1953–1964).

### Hegemonic Grid Implications of the River's Decline

If the Jordan River functions as the υ-axis boundary in the moral coordinate frame, its physical degradation is directly legible as a moral coordinate event:

The river ran at full ψ (+) volume 2000 years ago — active, flooding, generative.

It now runs at approximately 3% of that volume — its ψ value has collapsed toward -2.

The boundary between "contested outward energy" (west) and "group maintenance" (east) has not moved geographically, but the energy flowing through that boundary has been extracted to near zero.

The axis still holds its position. The force through it has been consumed. This is the Q7 (Effect — Emotive) reading of the geomorphological data: the river that once defined the boundary now barely marks it.
