## The Hunt as the Pure Price Model

Before any abstraction layer existed — before currency, property, institution, or credential — the price of food was air.

Not metaphorically. Literally. The metabolic cost of the hunt is measured in breath, in exertion, in stored energy expended to acquire new energy. The hunter pays in oxygen consumed and glucose burned. The price of the meat is exactly three variables and nothing else:

- Energy expenditure — the metabolic cost of the pursuit
- Scarcity gradient — how many options, how far, how fast
- Urgency — how hungry, how close to death

No overhead. No solving class. The transaction closes the moment the animal is eaten. This is the price equation at zero abstraction. Everything examined in this document is overhead layered on top of it.

**Scarcity of Options Determines Ferocity**

When game is abundant, the hunt is casual. The scarcity gradient is shallow — options are everywhere, urgency is low, the hunter can be selective. The price of meat in air is minimal.

When game is scarce, the hunt becomes ferocious. The gradient is steep — few options, high urgency, every failed attempt increases cost because air was spent and nothing was returned. The price climbs. If the gradient becomes steep enough the hunter spends more air pursuing the game than the game returns in calories. Starvation through unsuccessful hunting is paying more than the item is worth. This is the market crash in metabolic form — acquisition cost exceeds acquisition value, the system bankrupts itself.

The same gradient drives every extraction economy: mining, logging, fishing, farming. The scarcity of available options determines the ferocity of the extraction. The ferocity of the extraction determines the rate of depletion. The rate of depletion steepens the scarcity gradient. The steepened gradient increases ferocity. The loop runs to collapse unless a stopping condition is built into the system.

The metabolic model has a natural stopping condition built in: satiation. You stop hunting when you're fed. The loop terminates at sufficiency.

**The Information Space Toggle**

The structure changes the moment the hunt is for selling rather than eating. Two prices now exist simultaneously on the same meat.

The first price is still air. The metabolic cost is unchanged. The deer doesn't care why you're hunting it.

The second price is the information-space encoding of the first — what is this meat worth in the exchange medium? This second price is not determined by the hunt. It is determined by the scarcity of options available to the buyer, the urgency of the buyer's hunger, and the air cost the buyer would incur getting the meat themselves if they didn't buy it.

The seller's price floor is their air cost. The buyer's price ceiling is their own air cost — what they would spend hunting it themselves. The transaction price lands somewhere between those two points. This is the complete price equation derived from first principles. No abstraction required.

**The $+n Potential Value**

When the hunt is for exchange rather than use, what accumulates is $+n — stored information-space value representing actual metabolic cost encoded into an exchangeable medium. It is not fictitious. It represents real air spent. But it has one critical property: it is potential rather than actual. It sits in the information space as a claim on future exchange. It is not food. It is the promise of food.

The moment $+n becomes the goal of the hunt rather than the food, the hunt's optimisation target changes fundamentally.

Optimising for calories: stop when you have enough food. The hunt ends at sufficiency. The natural stopping condition holds.

Optimising for $+n: never stop. $+n has no satiation point. There is no such thing as enough $+n. The hunt continues past sufficiency, past sustainability, past the point where the game can reproduce faster than it is taken. The scarcity gradient steepens permanently. The price of air goes to infinity. The resource collapses.

This is the structural origin of overhunting, overfishing, deforestation, and soil depletion — not greed as a moral failing but as the predictable output of switching the optimisation target from calories to $+n. The metabolic model terminates at sufficiency. The information-space model has no termination condition. It runs until the resource is gone.

**The Tether**

The correction is not to abolish $+n. Exchange enables specialisation. Specialisation produces more total output than every person hunting for themselves. $+n is the mechanism that makes this possible and it is a genuine good at the appropriate scale.

The correction is to keep $+n tethered to the air price — to ensure the information-space price never fully decouples from the metabolic reality that produced it. When the tether holds, information-space prices track real scarcity, extraction rates self-regulate, and the resource persists. When the tether breaks — when $+n can be inflated independently of air cost through financialisation, speculation, or monopoly — extraction runs to collapse because the price signal no longer reflects the actual depletion of the underlying resource.

Every financial instrument that separates the price of a resource from the cost of extracting it is a mechanism for breaking the tether. Every broken tether is a countdown to the collapse of the resource it was broken on.

The hunt is the model because in the hunt the tether cannot break. The hunter cannot speculate on future game prices. The hunter cannot financialise their calorie deficit. The air price and the food price are the same price paid in the same currency at the same moment. The system is honest because it has no information layer thick enough to hide the truth of the gradient.
