Within classical theology (Divine Impassibility), God in His divine nature is perfect and immutable; suffering implies a vulnerability incompatible with infinite perfection. Because the historical incarnation is finished, the deity is not currently in a state of active physical agony requiring alleviation. However, the mystical tradition of \"Reparation\" instructs believers to actively seek to alleviate the mystical suffering caused by human rejection through acts of extreme charity. The concept of \"Redemptive Suffering\" maps a system where human suffering is transmuted by willfully uniting it to the completed historical suffering, aligning human pain with divine purpose rather than extracting divine energy.

The absolute core of this doctrine commands reciprocal death, defined by *kenosis* (self-emptying). Failure to execute this reciprocal death is considered a profound theological failure.
