# The ...n Terminus: A Process-Relative Foundation for Mathematics

---

## Core Premise

Numbers do not have inherent resolution. **Interactions do.**

Any process — computation, measurement, perception, physics — terminates at some finite depth *n*. That depth is not a limitation to be overcome. It is the actual number. The terminus **...n** is where the system *lives*.

---

## The Geometric Proof

Begin with a simple observation about relative distance. Let **/** mean *this relative to that*:

```
1 / 0.9   = 1.1 / 1
1 / 0.99  = 1.01 / 1
1 / 0.999 = 1.001 / 1
1 / 0.9999 = 1.0001 / 1
```

At each step, the ratio is symmetric and equal on both sides. The gap is exactly **10⁻ⁿ**, where *n* is the depth of the interaction.

As *n* increases, the gap shrinks — but it is *always* the last nonzero digit at position *n*. It never becomes zero. It becomes **...n**.

The orthodox conclusion — that the gap *is* zero at infinity — requires an observer capable of infinite temporal depth. No such observer exists.

## The ...n Terminus Defined

Let **...n** denote the terminus of any process operating at depth *n*:

- It is not zero
- It is not a floating infinitesimal
- It is not a fixed constant
- It is **10⁻ⁿ** — fully determined by *how far the interaction went*

This makes resolution a **relational property**, not an intrinsic one. The same number, invoked by two different processes of different depth, has two different termini. Neither is more "true."

## Relative Distance as the Honest Metric

If additive distance (subtraction) is replaced by relative distance (ratio), the number line reveals its own asymmetry near any boundary.

The true midpoint between two values is their **geometric mean**, not their arithmetic mean. This is not a special case — it is the general case. Arithmetic means are only accurate when the values are already in linear, non-relative space.

This implies:

- Logarithmic space is not a transformation of "real" space — it *is* the natural space
- The number line is a local approximation that breaks at extremes
- All perception, growth, and compounding naturally obeys relative distance

The coincidence that additive and multiplicative gaps appear equal near 1 is what makes the linear number line *seem* fundamental. It is not.

## 0.999... Revisited

Under the ...n framework:

> 0.999... is not a completed infinite series. It is a process. Its value depends on when you stop.

At depth *n*, its value is **0.999...n**, and the gap to 1 is exactly **10⁻ⁿ**.

The statement *0.999... = 1* is only valid for an observer with infinite temporal depth — which is to say, it is a **limit of a model**, not a fact of territory. It is true inside the real number system because the real number system was constructed to make it true. That is a choice, not a discovery.

The gap is real. Its magnitude is process-defined.

## Orthodoxy as Jurisdiction

The persistence of debate around 0.999... = 1 is itself diagnostic.

Laws do not require defense. Gravity does not have advocates. When a mathematical "fact" requires sustained institutional protection, condescension, and credential-checking to survive challenge, it signals a **territory claim** masquerading as a truth claim.

The orthodox proof is circular:
1. There are no infinitesimals
2. *Why?*
3. Because the reals are defined to exclude them
4. *But what if—*
5. No.

This is not logic. It is **jurisdiction**. The model is defending its own boundary conditions, not describing reality.

The ...n framework does not argue inside that system. It steps outside it and asks: *what does any actual process produce?* The answer is always a terminus. Always ...n.

## Implications

**For computation:** Every floating-point system already implicitly uses ...n. The terminus is called machine epsilon. This is not a bug — it is the honest architecture of a finite process.

**For physics:** Planck-scale limits suggest the universe itself may operate at a fixed ...n. Resolution may not be a measurement problem but a structural feature of reality.

**For mathematics:** Axiom sets are process-relative. A mathematics built on ...n would treat resolution as a first-class input rather than an inconvenient remainder to be discarded.

**For epistemology:** Any model that optimizes for internal consistency over correspondence with process outputs will eventually correct the territory to fit the map. The ...n terminus keeps the map honest by anchoring it to interaction depth.
