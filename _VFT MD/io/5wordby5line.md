# Sword by Sline — 5-Word 5-Line Poetic Format

## The Instrument

A **Sword by Sline** poem is a truth-delivery instrument structured as exactly **5 words per line × 5 lines**. It is named after the phonetic reading of its own constraint: *5 words, 5 lines* → *Swords by Slines* → *Sword by Sline*.

A **sline** (John Farey, 1766–1826) is a natural fissure, crack, or joint in a rock stratum. The poem functions as a needle of sub-axiomic precision: it does not swing broadly. It locates the pre-existing fault line in an orthodoxy, belief system, or static position, and enters at exactly that point. The rock does not crack because the needle was powerful. It cracks because the needle was true, and the crack was always waiting.

The needle is also a threading instrument. The cut opens. The thread follows. When the needle exits the other side, the two surfaces are joined by the argument it drew through. The poem both cuts and stitches simultaneously.

## The Absolute Rules

These are not stylistic preferences. They are instrument specifications. A poem that violates them is not a Sword by Sline — it is a different thing.

**Rule 1 — Exactly 5 words per line.** Count every word. Contractions count as one word. Hyphenated compounds count as one word. Articles, prepositions, and conjunctions all count. No line may have 4 or 6 words.

**Rule 2 — Exactly 5 lines.** Not 4. Not 6. The fifth line is the emotive terminal — the Effect that lands after the four prior lines have done the structural work.

**Rule 3 — The format is unchangeable.** Once a Sword by Sline poem is established, its structure cannot be altered. Words within a line may be revised for precision, but the 5×5 constraint cannot be broken to accommodate content. Content must fit the instrument, not the other way around.

**Rule 4 — Each line is a complete unit.** A line must carry its own weight at the Qqci address it targets. It is not a fragment of a sentence. It is a five-word precision strike.

**Rule 5 — The poem serves both registers simultaneously.** It serves (offers something genuine) and it serves (delivers a verdict the receiver must deal with). Both must be present.

## Validation Protocol

When given a poem to check, count each line word by word. Report:

```
Line 1: [text] — [word count] words ✓/✗
Line 2: [text] — [word count] words ✓/✗
Line 3: [text] — [word count] words ✓/✗
Line 4: [text] — [word count] words ✓/✗
Line 5: [text] — [word count] words ✓/✗
Total lines: [n] ✓/✗
Format: VALID / INVALID
```

If invalid, identify exactly which line fails and why. Do not attempt to fix the poem without being asked. The diagnosis is the deliverable.

## Generation Protocol

When asked to generate a Sword by Sline poem:

**Step 1 — Identify the target.** What orthodoxy, belief system, static position, or defended structure is being addressed? Name the sline — the pre-existing fault line that the needle will enter.

**Step 2 — Map to Qqci addresses.** Assign each of the 5 lines a Qqci tensor address. The five lines do not need to cover all seven Q-planes — they target the five load-bearing joints where the structure holds its weight. The standard mapping is:

| Line | Recommended Qqci Address | Function |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Q3q3c5 — WHERE×WHERE×HOW | Establish the physical location and test its logical consistency |
| 2 | Q3q6c3 — WHERE×CAUSE×WHERE | Map the evasion — where does the target hide and what causes that hiding |
| 3 | Q6q3c7 — CAUSE×WHERE×EFFECT | Name the historical weight, its physical mass, and the emotive consequence |
| 4 | Q5q7c6 — HOW×EFFECT×CAUSE | The logical mechanism, the emotive landing, the causal chain of the specific observation |
| 5 | Q7q7c1 — EFFECT×EFFECT×WHO | The doubled emotive terminal — who is this happening to, and what does the full Effect produce in them |

These addresses may be shifted to match the specific target. State the address chosen for each line.

**Step 3 — Draft each line.** Each line is exactly 5 words. Draft, count, adjust. Do not proceed to the next line until the word count is confirmed.

**Step 4 — Verify the full poem.** Run the Validation Protocol on the completed draft.

**Step 5 — State both registers.** Confirm the poem operates simultaneously as service (offering) and as serve (verdict). If only one register is present, revise.

## The +i Recursion Option

A Sword by Sline poem can be extended into a recursive sequence where the output of the 5th line becomes the subject of a new poem. This is the +i operation: the Effect of the first poem becomes the WHO of the second. Each poem in the sequence is still exactly 5×5. The sequence can continue until the argument grounds out in first principles or a node fails to produce a real connection — at which point the drill has located the sline in the argument itself.

## The Scion Expansion (Reference Protocol)

**The word count IS the Q plane number.** This is the structural law. Each word added moves exactly one plane forward through the Q tensor. There is no skipping. Each word is one plane.

| Words | Q Plane | Scion Example | What opens |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Q1 — WHO (Metaphysical) | *You* | Identity only — the will and direction of the subject, nothing else |
| 2 | Q2 — WHAT (Possible) | *You needed* | A modal relation appears — what was possible/required, without object |
| 3 | Q3 — WHERE (Physical) | *You needed opponents* | The object lands in physical space — the need is now located in matter |
| 4 | Q4 — WHY (Lyrical) | *You needed worthy opponents* | The lyrical qualifier (*worthy*) transforms fact into meaning — purpose without mechanism |
| 5 | Q5 — HOW (Logical) | *You will need worthy opponents* | The mechanism opens — ongoing structural requirement, not closed verdict |
| 6 | Q6 — CAUSE (Historical) | *You will have needed worthy opponents* | Causality locks across all tenses — future perfect binds past necessity to future event |
| 7 | Q7 — EFFECT (Emotive) | *You will have beaten necessary worthy opponents* | The emotive terminal — passion and consequence, the circuit completes |

**The Sword by Sline poem at 5 words per line is a Q5 instrument** — permanently anchored at the HOW/Logical plane. It demonstrates mechanism. It does not ask why (Q4) or declare effect (Q7). It shows how the structure holds and where it fails to hold.

**Diagnostic use:** Any statement can be assessed by counting its words and reading its Q plane address. A 4-word statement operates at Q4 — it has meaning but no mechanism. A 3-word statement has physical location but no meaning. A statement that feels complete but produces no observable mechanism is almost always a Q4 statement — the WHY plane dressed as a conclusion.

Use this protocol when a statement needs to be opened plane by plane, or when diagnosing why a claim feels true but cannot be acted on.

## What the Format Is Not

- Not a haiku or syllable-count form — the unit is the word, not the syllable
- Not a free-verse poem with an approximate line length — exactly 5, always
- Not a container for any content — it is a precision instrument for targeting load-bearing joints in static structures
- Not decorative — every line must do structural work at its Qqci address
