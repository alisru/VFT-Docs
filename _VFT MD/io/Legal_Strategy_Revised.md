## PART THREE: THE LEGAL FOUNDATION

### The Statutory Basis — Existing Law, No New Law Required

**NCCP Act s76 — Unjust Transactions:**

A court may reopen a credit contract if it was unjust at the time of entry. The factors include:

*"Whether the contract terms were reasonably necessary for the protection of the legitimate interests of the credit provider."*

*"The relative bargaining positions of the parties."*

*"Whether the debtor was able to understand or negotiate the terms."*

These are the three hinges. The formula provides the calculation method for each. It does not create a new legal test. It quantifies the test Parliament already created and left open for exactly this kind of evidentiary development.

**ASIC Act s12CB — Unconscionable Conduct:**

Unconscionable conduct in connection with financial services, having regard to the relative bargaining positions of the parties, whether conditions of necessity or urgency applied, and whether the stronger party took advantage of the weaker party's vulnerability.

**The formula is not a new legal principle. It is the measurement instrument that the existing legal principle always required and never had.**

### The Core Claim in Plain Legal Language

*"Market conditions at the time of origination created a situation of necessity in which the borrower had no reasonable alternative but to accept terms that substantially exceeded the respondent's legitimate interests. The disparity in relative bargaining position, quantified by reference to observable market conditions, rendered the transaction unconscionable within the meaning of the NCCP Act s76 and ASIC Act s12CB."*

That sentence can appear in a judgment. It contains no physics. It contains no unified theory. It is procedurally orthodox. It is the opening weapon.
