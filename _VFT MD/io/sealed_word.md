# **I. THE HISTORICAL RECORD**

**The Vulgate Declaration --- Council of Trent, 1546**

Jerome translated the biblical text into Latin between 382--405 CE, working from the Hebrew and Greek originals. His Vulgate was intended as a correction of earlier corrupted Latin versions. Jerome himself was a Greek and Hebrew scholar who understood the precision required. His intent was fidelity to the source.

In 1546, the Council of Trent declared the Latin Vulgate the sole authoritative text for doctrine, discipline, and public reading. This is the pivot point. The translation --- which Jerome produced as a faithful rendering of the originals --- was elevated above the originals it was translating. Any reading of the Greek that diverged from the Vulgate was now, institutionally, suspect.

> **Deuteronomy 4:2** *You shall not add to the word that I command you, nor take from it.*
>
> **Revelation 22:18--19** *If anyone adds to them, God will add to him the plagues described in this book. If anyone takes away from the words\... God will take away his share in the tree of life.*

Jerome translated. Trent declared the translation sacred. These are different operations. The first is faithful transmission. The second is the substitution of a transmission layer for the signal it was transmitting.

**The Codex Vaticanus --- Restricted for 400 Years**

The Codex Vaticanus --- one of the oldest complete Greek New Testament manuscripts, dated to the 4th century --- was catalogued in the Vatican Library in 1481. Access was restricted for nearly four centuries. Erasmus, building the first printed Greek New Testament in the early 16th century, was given only selective, mediated access. A reliable full edition was not published until Tischendorf in 1867. The institution held the oldest known copy of the source text and declined to make it available to the scholars attempting to reconstruct that source text.

> **Luke 11:52** *Woe to you lawyers! For you have taken away the key of knowledge. You did not enter yourselves, and you hindered those who were entering.*

The key of knowledge here is not doctrinal interpretation. It is access to the primary text. The Koine Greek of the New Testament is a precise, technical language. Its words carry specific freight. When access to the language is restricted, interpretation migrates from philology to authority. The institution becomes the interpreter, and the text becomes what the institution says it is.

**The Romans 13 Chain --- A Documented Mistranslation**

Romans 13:1 opens with ἐξουσία --- from ἐξ (out of, from the essence of) + οὐσία (being, substance). The compound means: authority flowing from the nature of being itself. It describes structural law --- the operating rules of reality --- not political governance.

The mistranslation chain is documented in four steps:

Step 1: Jerome rendered ἐξουσία as potestatibus --- powers, magistrates. The political framing is introduced at the first translation layer.

Step 2: The Council of Trent (1546) declared the Vulgate authoritative. The Greek ἐξουσία is now subordinate to Jerome\'s potestatibus in any doctrinal context. The translation layer is sealed over the source.

Step 3: Medieval Latin literacy collapsed to the clergy. The Greek text became inaccessible to virtually all interpreters. No correction mechanism existed because the source language was unavailable.

Step 4: The Reformation translators --- Luther, Tyndale, the KJV committee --- rendered potestatibus as \'governing authorities,\' \'higher powers,\' \'rulers.\' Five layers from the Greek. Each layer added confidence and subtracted information.
