## 1. The Electrochemical Mechanism of Emotion

Emotions are not chemical events that happen to the brain. They are the readout of a precise electrochemical execution sequence, triggered by specific patterns of ion movement across neuronal membranes.

The ions involved are:

- **Na⁺** (sodium) — primary carrier of the depolarisation signal
- **K⁺** (potassium) — restores resting potential after firing
- **Ca²⁺** (calcium) — the specific trigger for neurotransmitter release
- **Cl⁻** (chloride) — primary mediator of inhibitory signalling

These ions move through voltage-gated and ligand-gated channels embedded in the neuronal membrane. The timing, direction, and magnitude of their movement determines whether a neuron fires, and with what character — tonic, bursting, or phasic.

### The Transmission Chain

The chain from ion movement to felt emotion runs as follows:

```
Ion flux across membrane
→ Membrane potential change
→ Action potential threshold crossed
→ Action potential propagates down axon
→ Ca²⁺ influx at presynaptic terminal
→ Vesicle fusion with membrane
→ Neurotransmitter release into synapse
→ Receptor binding at postsynaptic membrane
→ Downstream signal cascade
→ Circuit-level pattern of activation
→ Felt emotional state
```

Calcium is the critical node. It is not the ratio of all ions together that triggers release — it is specifically Ca²⁺ concentration crossing a threshold at the presynaptic terminal that causes synaptic vesicles to fuse and release their contents.

### What Encodes Emotional Specificity

The ions are the **medium**. They are not the message.

Emotional specificity — why one state is fear and another is joy — is encoded at a higher level of organisation:

- **Which neural circuits are active** — amygdala, prefrontal cortex, anterior cingulate, anterior insula, nucleus accumbens, periaqueductal gray, hypothalamus
- **Which neurotransmitters are released** — dopamine, serotonin, norepinephrine, oxytocin, GABA, glutamate, endorphins
- **Which receptor subtypes bind them** — D1 vs D2, 5-HT1A vs 5-HT2A, α vs β adrenergic, etc.
- **The timing and rhythm of firing patterns** — burst vs tonic, frequency, synchronisation across regions

The electrochemical implementation is perfectly specified at the molecular level. The specification comes from somewhere above it.
