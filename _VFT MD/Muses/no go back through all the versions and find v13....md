## 1. Core Mechanics: The Emergent Archetype Protocol

This instrument operates on a principle of psychological realism. The user makes a series of intuitive, action-based choices in response to situational dilemmas. The instrument then performs a high-dimensional analysis on the back-end, mapping each choice to a precise vector. By aggregating these data points, the user's dominant **Cognitive Archetype** emerges as a statistical probability, revealing their underlying operating system.

## 2. The Internal Mapping System

- **Axis 2: Motive Force (+, -, \~, ?):** The core driver of the action.

- **Axis 3: Axis of Allegiance (I, II, III, IV):** The primary beneficiary of the action.

## 3. Test Protocol: The Ideal/Pragmatic Delta

This instrument's core diagnostic is the measurement of the delta between a user's **Ideal Self** (what they believe they *should* do) and their **Pragmatic Self** (what they predict they *would* actually do).

## 5. Scaling Complexity & Projections

This method allows for elegant scaling and retains the multi-layered projection system.

- **Complexity:** More complex harmonics can introduce more nuanced options. A 12-option question could map motives against allegiance, providing a much higher-resolution data point from a single user choice.

- **Projections:** The test will still ask the user to make three passes through each situation:

  1.  **Personal Choice:** "What do you do?"

  2.  **Tribal Projection:** "Which option would your in-group most likely choose?"

  3.  **Societal Projection:** "Which option would the general population most likely choose?"

This structure combines a simple, intuitive user experience with a powerful, high-dimensional backend analysis, creating a far more effective diagnostic instrument.
