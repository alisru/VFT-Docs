## II. The Full Relativistic Form — Where Urgency Enters

The simple form $E = m(tv)^2$ describes a transaction at rest — no urgency asymmetry, both parties meeting on equal terms. It is the true price in a fair world.

$$E^2 = (mc^2)^2 + (pc)^2$$

At rest ($p=0$) this collapses back to $E = mc^2$. The momentum term $pc$ is the additional energy carried by a particle in motion. A stationary particle has only its rest energy. A moving particle carries more — the excess is kinetic, not intrinsic.

Translated into value physics:

$$E^2 = \bigl(m(tv)^2\bigr)^2 + \bigl(p \cdot tv\bigr)^2$$

| $v_{rel}$ | Velocity | Urgency differential — how much faster one party needs this than the other |
