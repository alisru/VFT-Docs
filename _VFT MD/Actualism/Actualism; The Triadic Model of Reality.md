## 5. Defining the Foundational Virtues & Vices

### 5.1 Defining the 7 Classical Virtues (The "Good" Base Code)

These are the 7 foundational energies *from which* the Virtue Matrix is generated. They are the "base code" of "Good."

**The 3 Theological Virtues (The "Mind" / Conceptual Plane)**

1.  **Faith:** Belief in the unseen; the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen.

2.  **Hope:** The intellectual and spiritual conviction in a positive, achievable, and *True* future outcome.

3.  **Charity (Love):** The *agape* or divine, ordering love; the selfless, unconditional connection to the "Other" and the divine order.

**The 4 Cardinal Virtues (The "Body" / Physical & Social Planes)**

4.  **Prudence:** Practical wisdom; the ability to discern the correct path of action ("right reason in action").

5.  **Justice:** Fairness, righteousness, and the internal compass for determining what is "due" to oneself and others.

6.  **Fortitude (Courage):** The strength to face objective facts, withstand adversity, and act correctly even when "The World" makes it difficult.

7.  **Temperance:** Self-control, moderation, and the mastery of one's own internal state and passions.

### 5.2 Defining the 7 Deadly Sins (The "Bad" Base Code)

These are the 7 foundational energies *from which* the Vice Matrix is generated. They are the "base code" of "Bad."

**The 3 "Spiritual" Sins (The "Fallen Mind")**

1.  **Pride (Perversion of Faith/Prudence):** The original sin. The belief that one's *internal abstraction* is superior to the *external, objective Truth*.

2.  **Envy (Perversion of Charity/Justice):** The sorrow for another's good, based on the belief that it diminishes one's own.

3.  **Wrath (Perversion of Patience/Temperance):** The desire for vengeance or uncontrolled anger, rejecting Justice in favor of emotional excess.

**The 4 "Carnal" Sins (The "Corrupted Body")**

4.  **Sloth (Perversion of Fortitude):** Apathy and the refusal to *act* or face the difficulty of objective reality; a failure of courage.

5.  **Greed (Perversion of Justice):** The desire to acquire or possess more than one needs, disrupting moral balance.

6.  **Gluttony (Perversion of Temperance):** The over-consumption of anything to the point of waste; a failure of self-control.

7.  **Lust (Perversion of Charity/Love):** The disordered desire for or inordinate enjoyment of sexual pleasure, reducing a person (the "Other") to an object.
