### The Inability to Change: A VFT Perspective

#### The Spiritual Observation

Scripture and spiritual texts often observe that some people will not change their beliefs, even when presented with overwhelming evidence or during times of great tribulation. This is not a moral failing, but a predictable, mechanical process.

#### The VFT Translation: The Running Narrative

The inability to change, even when faced with verifiable facts, comes from a worldview so distorted that the person literally cannot believe the truth—even when they're being beaten to death with it.

The reason for this is that people, as natural "question answerers," form a continuous **running narrative** to make sense of reality. This internal story is their **Worldview**, and every new idea is judged not on its own merits, but by how well it fits into this pre-existing narrative.

This is not a conscious choice; it's a fundamental mechanism of the mind, a process governed by the **Answer Equation**: Answer = Idea to Consider × Resistance to my Worldview. The "Resistance" is a direct measure of how much a new idea contradicts the established running narrative.

When a new idea creates too much cognitive strain—when it threatens to break the coherence of the story—Free Will allows a person to choose the more comfortable path: rejecting the idea. It requires far less effort to maintain your existing narrative than it does to endure the chaotic and painful process of a complete rewrite.

The real danger is that there are people who understand this psychological mechanism and use it to their advantage, crafting their manipulations to fit perfectly within their target's running narrative.

#### The Three Archetypes

Within any system, individuals tend to fall into one of three archetypes:

- **Maximisers:** Those who seek the best outcome for everyone (+υ).

- **Minimisers:** Those who seek the best outcome for only themselves or their group (-υ).

- **The Compliant:** Those who align with whichever narrative is loudest or creates the least immediate strain.

Some individuals will never change because their running narrative is fundamentally rooted in the "Give to me" or "Take to give to me" quadrants. This is because people are a natural mix of greedy and altruistic; you must consider yourself to ensure your own survival.
