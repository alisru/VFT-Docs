Title: SMARTS Cognitive Processor: A Continuous Intelligence Architecture

Abstract:
This research report presents the SMARTS model, a novel cognitive architecture mapping human intelligence as a series of semantic processors integrated with resource management principles analogous to computer hardware (bandwidth, RAM, cache, and indexed long-term memory), physics analogs, and multidimensional representations of meaning. SMARTS conceptualizes intelligence as a continuous, self-correcting process rather than a static snapshot, modeling learning processes as probabilistic inference over high-dimensional structures such as 6D hyperobjects representing meaning.

1.  Introduction
    Traditional intelligence models often focus on static assessments, such as IQ scores, procedural memory, or domain-specific expertise. The SMARTS model extends these paradigms by representing human cognition as dynamic semantic processors, with explicit stages for knowledge management, application, adaptation, compression, and sustainability. It incorporates external knowledge sources, internal experiential processing, computer hardware and physics analogs, and multidimensional representations of meaning. Learning the geometry of objects and meanings is modeled as a Gaussian process in multi-dimensional space, with meanings represented as 6D hyperobjects.

2.  SMARTS Framework

3.  Learning Shapes, Angles, and Meanings

- Learning orientations and geometries of objects is modeled as a **Gaussian process**, updating mean and variance over observed angles and shapes.

- Meanings are represented as **6D hyperobjects**, allowing multi-faceted, high-dimensional interpretation.

- **Objective:** External observations provide data for updating shapes, angles, and semantic context.

- **Subjective:** Internal processors update Gaussian distributions and 6D hyperobject representations, allowing predictive generalization and cross-domain transfer.

4.  Emergent Properties

- **Intelligence:** Emergent from high efficiency in transfer, compression, adaptation, probabilistic learning, and multidimensional meaning integration.

- **Incapability / Stupidity:** Emergent from low cross-domain transfer, weak compression, poor probabilistic updating, or inefficient resource management.

- **Hardware Optimization:** Learning dynamically improves cognitive resources (bandwidth, RAM, cache, IAM).

- **Physics Alignment:** External states provide the potential energy landscape; internal processing performs probabilistic inference analogous to particle settling.

- **Semantic Depth:** Representing meanings as 6D hyperobjects allows robust multi-dimensional reasoning and abstraction.

5.  Comparison with Existing Models

- Overlaps with ACT-R, SOAR, Transformer architectures, and probabilistic cognitive models.

- Diverges by integrating continuous self-correction, hardware analogs, physics analogs, Gaussian process learning, and 6D hyperobject meanings.

6.  Discussion
    SMARTS conceptualizes intelligence as **continuous semantic processing**, cross-domain transfer, adaptive refinement, sustainable self-correction, probabilistic inference, and high-dimensional meaning representation. The model unifies human cognition, hardware and physics analogs, Gaussian learning, and multidimensional semantic spaces into a single framework.

7.  Conclusion
    SMARTS provides a framework for understanding human cognition as a dynamic, resource-bound, probabilistic, multi-dimensional intelligence system. Internal processors, external knowledge, probabilistic inference, and 6D meaning representations converge to continuously improve general intelligence.
