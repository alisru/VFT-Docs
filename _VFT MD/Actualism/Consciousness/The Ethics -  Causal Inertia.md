# The Ethics: Causal Inertia

## The Argument Against Fatalism

- **The Proposition:** "In a clockwork, causally determined universe, ethics has no ground because everything is automatic."

- **The Rebuttal:** A non-causal universe is the only place where ethics *cannot* exist.

Ethics is the study of Causal Inertia.

It is the understanding that "If I do X, Y will happen."

- If the universe were random, we could not be judged for our actions because we could not predict the result.

- Because the universe is rigid (clockwork), we are fully responsible for the projectile once we fire it.

## The Rejection of "Intent"

In a causally dominated universe, the universe does not care *why* the bridge collapsed (Incompetence vs. Malice). It only cares that it *did* collapse.

- **The Intent Loophole:** "I didn't mean to hurt them."

- **The Causal Reality:** The action resulted in destruction. The action was unethical because the result was a net negative causal ripple.

## Judgment by Debris Field

Unethical behavior is identified by looking at the debris field left behind a person or entity.

- **Narrative View:** Listening to apologies or justifications.

- **Causal View:** Observing the trail of emotional, financial, or physical wreckage.

## Trajectory vs. Accident

An accident is a single data point. A **Trajectory** is a line drawn through multiple data points over time.

- **Unethical Negligence:** If a vector is pointing toward a cliff (destruction), and the actor refuses to change course, they are unethical.

- It does not matter if the crash hasn't happened yet. The physics dictates the crash is inevitable.

- **Conclusion:** We judge the mechanism by its output. If a machine reliably produces "suffering" as output, it is an unethical machine, regardless of its "free will" or lack thereof.
