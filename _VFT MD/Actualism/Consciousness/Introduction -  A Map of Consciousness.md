# Introduction: A Map of Consciousness

The diagrams you are looking at are visual representations of a conceptual model known as the **Psochic Hegemony**, or the Moral Compass. It is best understood not as a conventional political chart, but as a map of consciousness itself. Its purpose is to deconstruct and evaluate the intrinsic nature of ideas, actions, and political actors by plotting them based on their fundamental moral and volitional characteristics. This allows for a deeper structural understanding of a concept's true intent, its trajectory, and its impact on the world.

## How to Read the Hegemony

The Psochic Hegemony is defined by the intersection of two fundamental, non-standard axes. Its purpose is not to classify existing ideologies but to reveal the moral and volitional vectors that underpin them.

**1. The Horizontal Axis (**  \mathbf{\upsilon}   **- Upsilon): Morality**

- **Vector:** Measures *who* ultimately benefits from the idea or action.

- **Direction:** Runs from   \mathbf{+1}   (Universal Benefit, "Everyone") on the left to   \mathbf{-1}   (Self/Group Benefit, "Only Me") on the right.

**2. The Vertical Axis (**  \mathbf{\psi}   **- Psi): Will**

- **Vector:** Measures *how* the idea is executed in the world—its mode of action.

- **Direction:** Runs from   \mathbf{+1}   (Proactive Will, "Do/Build/Create") on the top to   \mathbf{-1}   (Suppressive Will, "Do Not/Prevent/Suppress") on the bottom.

3\. The Quadrants:

The four quadrants are simply containers for the resulting vector combination (e.g., proactive and universally beneficial, or suppressive and self-serving). Crucially, the inherent morality (Good or Evil) of an idea is determined only by the vector of intent that fills the container, not the container itself.

4\. The Trajectory Cycle:

The map depicts a continuous, four-stage moral cycle: Redemption (  \mathbf{BR \to BL}  ) leads to Grace (  \mathbf{BL \to TL}  ), which risks The Fall (  \mathbf{TL \to TR}  ), which risks Delusion (  \mathbf{TR \to BR}  ), bringing the idea back to the need for Redemption.

## The Core Axes: Morality and Will

Every idea, actor, or event can be mapped within this framework by answering two foundational questions that correspond to the horizontal (  \upsilon  ) and vertical (  \psi  ) axes, which together form the   (\upsilon, \psi  #0) **vector space**. The orientation of these axes is non-standard. **Crucially, the quadrants themselves are moral containers; the morality (Good or Evil) is determined only by the vector of intent that fills them.**

### 1. The Horizontal Axis (  \upsilon  ): The Moral Question

This axis answers the most important question: **"Who benefits?"** It assesses the ultimate beneficiary of an idea if it were fully realized, measuring its moral valence. This axis defines the objective Morality Vector (  \upsilon  ), running from   -1   (Extractive/Destructive) to   +1   (Universally Beneficial).

- **Far Right (**  \mathbf{-\upsilon}  **): Benefit for Self.** The right side of the axis represents pure self-interest ("Only Me," "My Group"). This is the realm of Evil, Lies, and Regression.

- **Far Left (**  \mathbf{+\upsilon}  **): Benefit for Others.** The left side of the axis represents collective benefit ("Everyone," "Other Beings"). This is the realm of Good, Truth, and Progress.

### 2. The Vertical Axis (  \psi  ): The Volitional Question

This axis answers the question: **"What is its mode of action?"** It assesses the method by which an idea operates in the world, running from bottom to top.

- **Bottom (**  \mathbf{-\psi}  **): Suppressive Will.** The bottom of the axis represents a restrictive and negative force. It seeks to **"Do Not"**—to prevent, suppress, or destroy ideas and actions.

- **Top (**  \mathbf{+\psi}  **): Proactive Will.** The top of the axis represents a creative, assertive, and action-oriented force. It seeks to **"Do"**—to build, create, and manifest new possibilities.

### Quadrant Descriptions (Modes and Purpose)

1.  **Top-Left Quadrant: The Greater Good** (  \mathbf{+1\upsilon, +1\psi}  )

    - **Mode:** **Productive**.

    - **Description:** This quadrant combines a benefit for all (  +\upsilon  ) with a proactive will (  +\psi  ). This is the ideal space where the point labeled **"Greater Good (Good) The Truth"** is located. It represents proactive, creative ideas that benefit everyone.

2.  **Top-Right Quadrant: The Lesser Evil / Greatest Lie** (  \mathbf{-1\upsilon, +1\psi}  )

    - **Mode:** **Reductive**.

    - **Description:** This quadrant combines a benefit for self (  -\upsilon  ) with a proactive will (  +\psi  ). This is the **land of myth and fantasy, of stories to confound and amuse**. The quadrant is not inherently evil; its Reductive nature is determined by the vector of intent. When used immorally, the creative energy of this space becomes deceptive, resulting in the **Greatest Lie**. The labels here, such as **"Lesser Evil / The Greatest Lie,"** signify that actions in this quadrant are **proactive but self-serving**, often deceptively framed as being for the greater good (e.g., The "Delusion" cover).

3.  **Bottom-Left Quadrant: The Lesser Good / Half Truth** (  \mathbf{+1\upsilon, -1\psi}  )

    - **Mode:** **Constructive**.

    - **Description:** This quadrant combines a benefit for all (  +\upsilon  ) with a suppressive will (  -\psi  ). The labels here, such as **"Lesser Good / The Half Truth,"** suggest a space of well-intentioned but **passive, compromised, or suppressive action** (e.g., maintaining the status quo, stopping a harmful act).

4.  **Bottom-Right Quadrant: The Greater Evil / True Lie** (  \mathbf{-1\upsilon, -1\psi}  )

    - **Mode:** **Regressive**.

    - **Description:** This quadrant combines a self-serving interest (  -\upsilon  ) with a suppressive will (  -\psi  ). This is where the point **"Greater Evil (Bad) The True Lie"** is located, representing **self-interested inaction, suppression, and deceit** (e.g., promoting nihilism, extraction, or active destruction).

## Trajectories: The Paths of Progress and Deception

The diagrams also illustrate the dynamic trajectories of ideas. The paths form a continuous cycle of moral evolution and potential decay: **Redemption** leads to **Grace**, which leads to **The Fall**, which leads to **Delusion**, which leads to **Redemption**, and so on.

- **The Path of Grace (Enlightenment):** This is the constructive path, starting in the **Bottom-Left** quadrant (  \mathbf{+1\upsilon, -1\psi}  ) and moving to the **Top-Left** quadrant (  \mathbf{+1\upsilon, +1\psi}  ). It is a vector shift from passive compromise to **active, creative universal benefit**. It is guided by the principle of **"Grace,"** which asks: "How does this benefit me, **and** other people?"

- **The Path of The Fall:** This is the trajectory of temptation, starting in the **Top-Left** quadrant (  \mathbf{+1\upsilon, +1\psi}  ) and moving to the **Top-Right** quadrant (  \mathbf{-1\upsilon, +1\psi}  ). It is a vector shift from creative universal benefit to **active, deceptive self-interest**. It is guided by **The Fall**, which asks: "How does this benefit **me** and you, **but really for me**?"

- **The Path of Delusion (Regression & Fall from Grace):** This is the trajectory of decay, starting in the **Top-Right** quadrant (  \mathbf{-1\upsilon, +1\psi}  ) and moving to the **Bottom-Right** quadrant (  \mathbf{-1\upsilon, -1\psi}  ). It is a vector shift from active, deceptive self-interest to **passive, destructive self-interest**. It is guided by the **"Delusionary Idea,"** which asks: "How does this benefit me, **but not** other people?"

- **The Path of Redemption:** This is the upward path of recovery, starting in the **Bottom-Right** quadrant (  \mathbf{-1\upsilon, -1\psi}  ) and moving to the **Bottom-Left** quadrant (  \mathbf{+1\upsilon, -1\psi}  ). It is a vector shift from destructive self-interest to **passive, compromising universal benefit**. It is guided by **Redemption**, which asks: "How does this benefit **me**, only **me**, and you take what I don't need?"
