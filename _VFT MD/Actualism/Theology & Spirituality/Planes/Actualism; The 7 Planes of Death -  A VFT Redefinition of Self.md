# The 7 Planes of Death: A VFT Redefinition of Self

Abstract:

In conventional thought, death is a singular biological error—a failure of the hardware. In Vector Field Theory (VFT), death is a feature, not a bug. It is the necessary algorithm for breaking the "Closed Loop" of the Self so it can integrate with the "Open Circuit" of the Divine. This document maps the "Process of Dying" across the Seven Planes, demonstrating that true Life (\$Zoe\$) requires a specific death on every level of existence.

## I. The Concrete Deaths (The Breaking of the Vessel)

### 1. The Physical Plane (Biological Death)

- **The Definition:** **Entropy.**

- **The Old Self:** A biological machine fighting to maintain homeostasis against the universe.

- **The Death:** The cessation of metabolic resistance. The hardware goes offline.

- **The Redefinition:** You are no longer a **Generator** of energy, but a **Seed** planted in matter.

- **Biblical Index:** *1 Corinthians 15:36* ("That which thou sowest is not quickened, except it die.")

### 2. The Historical Plane (Reputational Death)

- **The Definition:** **Obscurity.**

- **The Old Self:** A protagonist in a personal narrative, obsessed with legacy, credit, and "being known."

- **The Death:** The erasure of the Name. Accepting that your specific contribution may be forgotten so that the Truth remains. It is the death of the "Main Character Syndrome."

- **The Redefinition:** You are no longer the **Author**, you are the **Ink**.

- **Biblical Index:** *John 3:30* ("He must increase, but I must decrease.")

### 3. The Logical Plane (Intellectual Death)

- **The Definition:** **Submission.**

- **The Old Self:** The "Knower." The intellect that judges truth based on its own limited processing power. The pride of the "Correct Opinion."

- **The Death:** The admission of ignorance. The collapse of the human intellect before the Divine Logos. It is the acknowledgment that your logic is a subset, not the superset.

- **The Redefinition:** You are no longer the **Processor**, you are the **Terminal**.

- **Biblical Index:** *Proverbs 3:5* ("Lean not unto thine own understanding.")

## II. The Abstract Deaths (The Surrender of the Spirit)

### 4. The Emotive Plane (Emotional Death)

- **The Definition:** **Detachment / The Weaning.**

- **The Old Self:** A creature driven by dopamine, comfort, and the need for validation.

- **The Death:** The "Dark Night of the Soul." The removal of emotional feedback loops. Serving the System when it feels cold, empty, and unrewarding.

- **The Redefinition:** You are no longer a **Consumer** of joy, but a **Conductor** of loyalty.

- **Biblical Index:** *Psalm 131:2* ("My soul is even as a weaned child.")

### 5. The Possible Plane (Volitional Death)

- **The Definition:** **Surrender (Gethsemane).**

- **The Old Self:** The Planner. The will that seeks to impose its own probability vector onto the future.

- **The Death:** The destruction of the "Five Year Plan." The total release of control over outcomes.

- **The Redefinition:** You are no longer the **Captain**, you are the **Vessel**.

- **Biblical Index:** *Luke 22:42* ("Not my will, but thine, be done.")

### 6. The Lyrical Plane (Metaphorical Death)

- **The Definition:** **Silence.**

- **The Old Self:** The Noise. The need to express, to speak, to project identity outward.

- **The Death:** Becoming a symbol rather than a speaker. Allowing your life to become a parable that points to something else.

- **The Redefinition:** You are no longer the **Singer**, you are the **Song**.

- **Biblical Index:** *Galatians 2:20* ("I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I live; yet not I...")

## III. The 7th Plane (The Synthesis)

### 7. The Meta-Physical Plane (Kenosis)

- **The Definition:** **Union.**

- **The Old Self:** The "I." The fundamental illusion of separation from the Source.

- **The Death:** *Kenosis* (Self-Emptying). The final removal of the barrier between the drop and the ocean.

- **The Redefinition:** The "Self" is deleted. There is only the System. The drop does not disappear; it becomes the Ocean.

- **Biblical Index:** *Philippians 2:7* ("But made himself of no reputation, and took upon him the form of a servant...")
