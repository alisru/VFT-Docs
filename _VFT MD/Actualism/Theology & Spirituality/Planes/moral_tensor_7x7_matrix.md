## Identity

The moral tensor is a 2D coordinate space with υ (morality, who benefits) on the horizontal axis and ψ (will, what the energy does) on the vertical. The four corners define the semantic extremes. The 7 divisions between each corner pair give the named gradient positions. Every analogue layer is a different vocabulary reading the same underlying coordinate — the tensor doesn't change, only the lens does.

**Corner labels:**

| Corner | Direction | Morality | Will | Quadrant |
|--------|-----------|----------|------|----------|
| TL (+υ,+ψ) | up-left | altruism | active | Productive |
| TR (-υ,+ψ) | up-right | egoism | active | Reductive |
| BL (+υ,-ψ) | down-left | altruism | passive | Peaceful |
| BR (-υ,-ψ) | down-right | egoism | destructive | Regressive |

**The diagonal axes:**

| Axis | Phrase | Character | TL/BR ends |
|------|--------|-----------|------------|
| \ | "In the ideal world" | optimistic, grounded, acting | TL (+1υ,+1ψ) altruist's ideal — BR (-1υ,-1ψ) egoist's known anti-ideal |
| / | "In an ideal world..." | pessimistic, ungrounded, stated not acted | TR (-1υ,+1ψ) egoist's ideal — BL (+1υ,-1ψ) altruist's anti-ideal |

The `/` axis is where "In an ideal world..." lives. Both ends use it pessimistically — the egoist has the will but wrong direction, the altruist has the right direction but no will. The ceiling gets defined by whoever has the most will, which is usually TR. The `\` axis is grounded — both ends verified against reality, optimistic, acting. The flip between them is that altruism and egoism swap which end has the will.

**Worldview / knowledge tensor:**

The hegemony is the orthogonal interaction of two separate 2×2 tensors. Each has its own υ and ψ axes. Their interaction produces the full worldview space.

Tensor A — Spirituality & Possibility:

| | +ψ (thought-as-truth) | -ψ (known-as-truth) |
|-|-----------------------|---------------------|
| +υ (spiritual) | felt meaning, open, unverified | verified felt meaning, wisdom |

| | +ψ (known-as-truth) | -ψ (thought-as-truth) |

Thinking = feeling (the internal process). Knowing = verified (grounded against reality). The conflict between science and spirituality is a frame problem — the axis is epistemological not ontological. A spiritual claim that has been verified is wisdom. A scientific claim that hasn't been verified is hypothesis.

## Variable Definitions Grounded Against the Matrix

### c_Evil — the worst-worst anchor (-1υ,-1ψ)

c_Evil is not a fixed moral label — it is any process that simultaneously maximises extraction AND maximises destruction of generative capacity. The worst-worst example across layers:

| Layer | c_Evil BR worst-worst |
|-------|----------------------|
| morality | predatory debt — extracts all wealth while destroying the borrower's capacity to generate more. Takes the seed corn not just the harvest. |
| truth | the perfected lie — internally consistent, zero contact with reality, self-reinforcing, unfalsifiable from inside its own frame |
| good/evil | Bad Truth — true AND maximally harmful. Not a lie, worse. A true fact weaponised to destroy. |
| happy/sad | Depression Zone — active destruction of the capacity for future joy. Learned helplessness as a system. |
| right/wrong | actively wrong with full will and full resources. Not ignorance — deliberate. |
| direction | down-right — descending, self-contracting, away from everyone |
| strong/weak | takes everything, destroys the source of what it takes. Parasitism that kills the host. |
| knowledge | known anti-ideal — full information, wrong direction, moving toward it anyway |

## Attractor Archetypes — n_scale Definition

Each attractor is a scaled archetype. n_scale is set by the reader's frame — personal, institutional, or civilisational. The geometry is identical at every scale. Primary labels are the truth/lie names; examples are illustrative instances at a given n_scale.

Not setting the c_Evil floor means you cannot measure how bad things are. Not setting the c_Good ceiling means someone else sets it for you — usually from TR. Both omissions mean you're navigating inside someone else's cube with their floor and ceiling.

| Attractor | Coord | Label | Character | Example |
|-----------|-------|-------|-----------|---------|
| Greater Good | (+1υ,+1ψ) | Good Truth | max will, max beneficiary spread — the ceiling most leave undefined | save * n — pull someone from traffic / structural opposite of the mass death event |
| Lesser Evil | (-1υ,+1ψ) | Bad Lie | high will, wrong beneficiary — building hard in wrong direction | debt * n — exploitative loan / institutions that produced the mass death event at full energy |
| Lesser Good | (+1υ,-1ψ) | Good Lie | right direction, insufficient will — saves through systemic shift not direct action | forgive * n — reform the condition / structural change movements |
| Greater Evil | (-1υ,-1ψ) | Bad Truth | the floor of the interaction frame — all other positions measured relative to this | kill * n — minor cruelty / the mass death event at civilisational scale |
| Lesser of two Evils | (+1υ,0ψ) | Half Truth | right beneficiary, zero will — the correct neutral observer | witness * n — knows harm is occurring, sympathises, does nothing |
| Greater of two Evils | (-1υ,0ψ) | Half Lie | wrong beneficiary, zero will — the bystander who benefits | passive extraction * n — profits from conditions that produced the harm |

**n_scale:** set the frame at whatever scale is legible — the geometry holds. Personal scale uses minor cruelty as BR calibration. Civilisational scale uses the mass death event. Same structure, different n.

## c_Evil as Any Arbitrary Process

c_Evil is the lower bound of whatever interaction frame has been drawn — the BR corner of the relative cube for that specific interaction. It is not a fixed global point. When you define the scope of an interaction, c_Evil is whatever the worst possible outcome of that scope is. It scales to the frame.

Greater Evil (-1υ,-1ψ) is the nearest named attractor in the standard tensor — not c_Evil itself. c_Evil is kill * n_scale where n_scale is determined by the interaction frame. At personal scale it is minor cruelty with no remorse. At civilisational scale it is the mass death event. The geometry is the same. The floor moves with the frame.

This is why the floor matters structurally: if you draw a small interaction frame with a low c_Evil floor, everything above bare minimum reads as ultrapositive — "doing their job correctly" appears in the TL quadrant of that frame. The person isn't wrong within their cube. Their cube is miscalibrated because they haven't accounted for all the bad implications of the good things they missed doing. Good deeds look exceptional only because the floor wasn't set at the actual worst outcome of inaction.

Any process moving simultaneously toward negative υ and negative ψ with increasing velocity is moving toward the c_Evil floor of its interaction frame — regardless of stated purpose, scale, or what it calls itself. The attractor at (-1υ,-1ψ) is where that trajectory becomes structurally locked within the standard frame. Past that point the frame rescales and the actor can no longer self-measure without an external reference point.
