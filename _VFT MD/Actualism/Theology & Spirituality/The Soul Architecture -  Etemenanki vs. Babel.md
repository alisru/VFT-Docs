Consider a pyramid as an inverse drill. You need to build a whole diamond shell. If you want to go higher, you start by stacking one brick on the center peak, then filling the rest brick by brick.

If you try to skip steps—only building layers where you want to be "efficient"—you end up building a Tower of Babel instead of the solid Temple of Etemenanki (The Foundation of Heaven and Earth). You build a tower of ego that looks high but is hollow.

It is about fixing souls to stop building towers. We must understand the landscape and start building a temple of "You" around Understanding.

We must realize the drilling nature of the soul: to move ever higher and deeper simultaneously. We build two opposing pyramids in the shape of a diamond—grounding ourselves deep into the foundation so we can move forwards and effectively put the past behind us.
