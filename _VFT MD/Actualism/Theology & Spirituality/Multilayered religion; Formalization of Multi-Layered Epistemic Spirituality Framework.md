# **Formalization of Multi-Layered Epistemic Spirituality Framework**

## **Section I: Foundational Assumptions**

**Axiom 1 (Existence of a Causal Substrate / God)\**
There exists a singular causal substrate (hereafter **God**) underlying all of reality.

- God is omnipotent, omniscient, and omnipresent in principle.

- God is constrained by the laws of causality inherent in the universe.

- God communicates through layered interfaces, tailored to the comprehension capacity of finite agents.

**Axiom 2 (Finite Agent Constraints)\**
Humans (and other finite agents) have limited perception, processing capacity, and prior knowledge.

- No human can comprehend God directly.

- Humans interpret signals from God probabilistically, via culture, language, and observation.

**Axiom 3 (Layered Interfaces / Religions)\**
Religions, philosophies, and cultural narratives are **interfaces** provided by God to translate infinite knowledge into finite comprehensible formats.

- Each interface captures a **subset of the total causal substrate**, filtered by cultural, temporal, and cognitive constraints.

- Interfaces are designed to be **individually sufficient for local comprehension**, but **collectively convergent** toward higher-resolution truth.

**Definition 1 (Interface Fidelity)\**
Let ( I_i ) be an interface (religion/philosophy). Its **fidelity** ( F(I_i) ) is the proportion of total causal information transmitted accurately to an agent.

**Definition 2 (Collapsed Certainty)\**
Let ( M ) be the set of all partial interpretations available to an agent at time ( t ). The **certainty** ( C_t ) of an agent is the weighted collapse of ( M ) under context ( \\Gamma ):
