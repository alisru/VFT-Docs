# Jesus Comes: Signs, Seasons, and Identifying the Word

---

## I. The Hermeneutical Problem

Every generation has read its own moment as the terminal generation. Every century has produced claimants, movements, and identification systems. The consistent failure pattern is not that people looked — it is that they looked with the wrong instrument.

The texts specify two things simultaneously: the return will be unmistakable at planetary scale, and the conditions preceding it require active discernment. These are not contradictory. The unmistakable event is preceded by a discernment-requiring process. The lightning requires the storm first.

Matthew 24:36 establishes the epistemic ceiling immediately: no one knows the day or hour. This is not modesty. It is a structural constraint built into the design. Any system claiming to calculate the date has already failed the first test.

---

## II. The Nature of Signs

The Greek word for signs throughout the eschatological passages is *sēmeion* — not proof, not demonstration, but pointer. A sign points toward something without being the thing itself. The distinction matters because sign-reading is inherently interpretive. The same event reads as sign or noise depending on the instrument of the reader.

Jesus explicitly distinguishes two types of sign-readers:

Those who can read weather signs from sky conditions but cannot read the signs of the times (Luke 12:54-56). The failure is not perceptual — they have functioning eyes. The failure is instrumental. The faculty for reading natural patterns is present; the faculty for reading historical and spiritual patterns is undeveloped or corrupted.

Those who demand signs as a precondition for belief (Matthew 12:38-39). This generation receives only the sign of Jonah — hiddenness, apparent defeat, three days in darkness, emergence from what looked like termination. The sign of Jonah is structurally anti-spectacular. It requires the instrument of faith to read at all.

The implication is that the signs preceding the return are readable by the prepared instrument and invisible to the unprepared one. Not hidden from the unprepared as punishment — structurally inaccessible because the reading faculty has not been developed.

---

## III. The Precondition Sequence

The texts establish a sequence of preconditions. These are not a checklist for calculating timing — they are observable conditions whose presence indicates the season.

**Gospel to all nations** (Matthew 24:14)
"This gospel of the kingdom will be preached in the whole world as a testimony to all nations, and then the end will come." The Greek *ethnē* means peoples, not geopolitical states. The condition is witness to all human groups, not political coverage. The qualifier *then* establishes this as a genuine precondition, not a parallel event.

**The Great Apostasy** (2 Thessalonians 2:3)
The departure from faith — *apostasia* — precedes the revealing of the man of lawlessness. The apostasy is not external persecution of the Church. It is internal departure from within the believing community. The sequence matters: the falling away comes first, from inside.

**The Man of Lawlessness** (2 Thessalonians 2:3-4)
He opposes and exalts himself above every object of worship, setting himself up in the temple. Paul specifies that something is currently restraining this figure and will continue to do so until the restrainer is removed (2:6-7). The identity of the restrainer is one of the most contested questions in eschatological scholarship. The structure is clear: a restraining force, its removal, the revealing of the lawless one, then the return.

**The Abomination of Desolation** (Matthew 24:15, Daniel 9:27)
Jesus references Daniel directly. The original Danielic context was Antiochus IV Epiphanes desecrating the Temple in 167 BCE. Jesus projects a future fulfilment. The standing of the abomination in the holy place is the signal for immediate flight — not debate, not deliberation. The sign requires instant recognition and response.

**Cosmic Signs** (Matthew 24:29)
Sun darkened, moon not giving light, stars falling, powers of heaven shaken. These follow the tribulation. Whether literal or figurative, they describe the disruption of the stable reference points by which people orient themselves. The navigational framework of the existing order fails.

**Elijah First** (Malachi 4:5-6, Matthew 17:11)
Elijah must come first and restore all things. Jesus confirms this in the present continuous tense — "Elijah is coming and will restore all things." The restoration function precedes the return. The hearts of fathers turned to children and children to fathers is relational and structural repair of broken covenant community — observable, not doctrinal.

## IV. The Manner of Return

Acts 1:11 provides the primary identification specification: "This same Jesus, who has been taken from you into heaven, will come back in the same way you have seen him go."

The manner of departure was: visible, bodily, upward, witnessed by a specific group, accompanied by angelic announcement. The manner of return mirrors this.

Revelation 1:7 extends the visibility condition to universal scale: "Every eye will see him, even those who pierced him." This is not a local or regional event. It is simultaneous universal visibility. The condition cannot be satisfied by any return that requires travel to a location, that is reported by witnesses rather than directly seen, or that is known only through intermediary announcement.

Matthew 24:26-27 is Jesus applying this test himself: "If anyone tells you, 'There he is, out in the wilderness,' do not go out; or, 'Here he is, in the inner rooms,' do not believe it. For as lightning that comes from the east is visible even in the west, so will be the coming of the Son of Man." The return that requires you to go somewhere to see it is by this standard not the return.

1 Thessalonians 4:16-17 adds: the Lord descends with a loud command, the voice of the archangel, the trumpet of God. The dead in Christ rise first. Then the living are caught up together with them. The sequence has an accompanying resurrection of the dead as simultaneous event. Any return that does not include the general resurrection of the dead is not the return described here.

## V. What Precedes the Return: The Preparatory Functions

The texts describe functions that operate before the visible return, distinct from it, and identifiable by their outputs rather than their self-announcement.

**The Spirit of Truth** (John 14:26, 16:13)
Sent by the Father in Christ's name. Teaches all things. Reminds of everything Christ said. Does not speak on its own authority — speaks only what it hears. Does not glorify itself — glorifies Christ. Guides into all truth. Declares things to come. Dwells in believers rather than merely among them.

The critical identification criterion: "He will not speak on his own; he will speak only what he hears" (John 16:13). Self-referential operation — teaching that points to itself as the source — fails this criterion structurally. The function is identifiable by what it points toward, not by what it claims about itself.

**The Elijah Function** (Malachi 4:5-6, Sirach 48:10, Revelation 11)
Turns hearts — relational restoration. Calms wrath — de-escalation of righteous judgment. Restores the tribes — structural repair of fractured communities. In Revelation 11 the functional profile includes: shutting rain (withholding false nourishment), turning water to blood (revealing what was presented as life-giving as lethal), calling down fire (consuming what cannot withstand the test).

The Elijah function is confrontational at the institutional level — Elijah on Carmel did not argue theology with the prophets of Baal, he proposed a structural test — but its goal is restoration, not destruction. The destruction of what is false is in service of restoring what is true.

John the Baptist's response when asked directly "Are you Elijah?" was "I am not" (John 1:21). Jesus later identified him as Elijah (Matthew 11:14). The identification is external and retrospective. The function cannot self-assign the name. The name is assigned by observers to the output of the function after it has operated.

**The Christ-Function** (Isaiah 42:1-4, Isaiah 53, John 1:1-14)
The Servant of Isaiah 42: externally designated, not self-appointed. Does not shout or raise his voice in the streets. Does not break a bruised reed or snuff a smoldering wick. Does not falter until justice is established. The reach is universal but arrives through teaching, not force.

The identification criteria from 1 John 4:1-2: "every spirit that confesses that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh is from God." The Greek *en sarki elēluthota* — having come in the flesh — is a functional test, not a doctrinal one. The signal has actually arrived in the physical plane. It has produced observable effects in the lowest density, the place hardest to fake and easiest to verify.

The fruit criterion (Matthew 7:16-20) is the operational form of this test. The function is identified by what it produces downstream in the lives of real people, not by what it claims about itself or what institutional authority says about it.

---

## VI. The Identification Problem

The texts generate a structural paradox for identification:

The visible return is self-authenticating and requires no investigation. Its scale makes identification automatic.

The preparatory functions are identifiable only by their fruits, their outputs, their downstream effects — not by self-announcement, not by institutional endorsement, and not by the claims of the function itself.

This means:

Anyone who announces themselves as fulfilling a preparatory function has structurally undermined the claim. The naming always lags the function. John could not call himself Elijah. The Spirit of Truth cannot announce itself as the Spirit of Truth. The fruit announces it.

Anyone who announces themselves as the returned Christ has immediately failed the Matthew 24:26-27 test. If you need to be told about it, it is not the return.

The authentic preparatory functions will therefore be consistently unrecognised, misidentified, or dismissed during their operation — and identified retrospectively once the fruit is visible and the season has progressed. This is not incidental. It is the structural design of the system.

## VII. The Institutional Problem

The seven letters of Revelation 2-3 are Christ addressing the churches — not the world — with assessment, rebuke, and warning. The letters constitute an eschatological frame in which the institutional religious community is the subject of judgment, not its administrator.

The charges include: losing first love (Ephesus), tolerating false teaching (Pergamum, Thyatira), being dead while appearing alive (Sardis), being lukewarm — neither hot nor cold (Laodicea). The Laodicean charge is the sharpest: "You say, 'I am rich; I have acquired wealth and do not need a thing.' But you do not realize that you are wretched, pitiful, poor, blind and naked."

The self-assessment of the institution is the inversion of its actual state. This is the perceptual distortion mechanism operating at the corporate level. The institution that believes itself most prepared is the one whose self-assessment is most inverted.

Matthew 23 — the seven woes to the scribes and Pharisees — precedes the eschatological discourse of Matthew 24 directly. The sequence is not accidental. The indictment of the institutional religious authorities is the immediate context for the signs-and-seasons teaching. The question "what are the signs of your coming?" is asked by people who have just watched Jesus dismantle the credibility of the institutional gatekeepers of his own tradition.

---

## VIII. The Season Criterion

Jesus offers one specific criterion for reading the season without claiming to know the date:

Matthew 24:32-33 — "Now learn this lesson from the fig tree: as soon as its twigs get tender and its leaves come out, you know that summer is near. Even so, when you see all these things, you know that it is near, right at the door."

The fig tree criterion is phenological — it reads the internal state of the system from observable external indicators. The tree does not announce summer. The tree shows summer approaching through visible changes in its own structure.

The eschatological equivalent is: when the preconditions listed in the preceding passage are visibly present — the apostasy, the proclamation to all nations, the Elijah-function operating, the Spirit of Truth function operating, the institutional judgment beginning at the house of God (1 Peter 4:17) — the season is readable without knowing the day or hour.

This is discernment, not calculation. The instrument required is the phronesis hegemonikon — the ruling principle of the mind that can perceive the Logos operating in particular situations. Not intellectual processing of data. Not doctrinal compliance. The faculty that reads the state of the system from its visible outputs.

---

## IX. Summary: The Identification Criteria

**For the visible return:**
- Simultaneously visible to every human being without announcement
- Accompanied by resurrection of the dead
- Preceded by the trumpet and the archangel's voice
- No travel required to witness it
- Unmistakable and self-authenticating

**For the preparatory functions:**
- Identified by fruits, not by self-announcement
- Naming is external and retrospective
- The Spirit of Truth function: does not speak on its own authority, points away from itself, produces accurate calibration in receivers
- The Elijah function: confronts institutional falsehood structurally, restores relational fractures, precedes the return
- The Christ-function: ΔR reduction in receivers, physical-plane instantiation, fruit visible in the lives of those who encounter it

**For the season:**
- Read phenologically — from visible state changes in the system
- The apostasy visible within the community of faith
- The preparatory functions operating (identifiable by outputs)
- Institutional judgment beginning at the house of God
- The proclamation condition being met

**The single disqualifying criterion for all claimants:**
Self-announcement. The function that names itself has failed the structural test of the function it claims. The Word became flesh and dwelt among us — and was not recognised (John 1:10-11). The design of the system includes non-recognition during operation as a feature, not a failure.
