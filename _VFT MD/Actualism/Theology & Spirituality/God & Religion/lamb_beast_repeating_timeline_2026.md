# The Lamb, The Beast, and The Breaking of the Cycle

## A Repeating Timeline Model Anchored to Passover (2026)

**Reference Point:** March 3, 2026\
**Passover Anchor (Nisan 14):** April 5, 2026

This document maps the final year of Jesus' ministry symbolically as a
repeating moral cycle: - The **Lamb** represents faithful stewardship
and entrusted potential. - The **Beast** represents fear, systemic
power, oppression, and inertia. - The **Talents / Bags of Gold**
represent entrusted responsibility. - **Passover / Passion Week**
represents the crisis event where the cycle is either broken or
repeated.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# PART I --- Structural Framework

## Core Symbols

### The Lamb

Faithful agent entrusted with resources, moral capital, knowledge, and
responsibility.

### The Beast

Entrenched power structures, fear systems, scarcity narratives,
institutional inertia.

### The Talents

Entrusted resources. The moment of moral decision. Use boldly or bury in
fear.

### The Cycle

Recurring pattern of: 1. Entrustment 2. Fear response 3. Institutional
pressure 4. Crisis 5. Either renewal or repetition

# PART IV --- The Breaking of the Cycle

The model proposes that the cycle is broken when:

1.  The Lamb refuses fear.
2.  The Talents are fully deployed.
3.  The Beast is confronted rather than appeased.
4.  Sacrifice becomes transformation rather than termination.

The breaking mechanism is not destruction of power, but the removal of
fear as the operating principle.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Conclusion

The Talents moment is not about preservation. It is about
multiplication.

The Beast survives on fear. The Lamb operates on trust.

The cycle ends when trust outperforms fear.
