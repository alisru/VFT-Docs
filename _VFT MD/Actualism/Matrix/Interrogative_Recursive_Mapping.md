# The Master Canonical Narrative of Recursive Inquiry

## The Simple Story of the Seven Questions
*(Non-Systems Interpretation)*

Imagine you are trying to understand an event, like a person building a house. To get the full truth, you don't just ask one question; you ask seven, and then you ask them again *inside* the first answers.

1.  **Who** is building? (The Architect)
2.  **What** are they building? (The House)
3.  **Why** are they doing it? (For Shelter)
4.  **Where** is it being built? (On the Hill)
5.  **How** are they building it? (With Tools)
6.  **Cause**: What started this? (The need for a home)
7.  **Effect**: What is the result? (A warm family)

As we go deeper, we ask: "**What** is the Architect's **Skill**? (**What of Who**)" or "**Why** does the **House** need to be **Strong**? (**Why of What of What**)". This is the 3-layer mapping. It is the art of asking "Why?" until there are no more secrets left.

## The Master Recursive Mapping

In the Vector Field Theory (VFT), we explore the seven interrogatives across three nested layers:
- **Layer 1 (Q)**: The Primary Plane (The Core Question)
- **Layer 2 (q)**: The Sub-Plane (The Question *of* the Plane)
- **Layer 3 (c)**: The Sub-Sub-Plane (The Deep Logic)

The narrative order below follows the canonical sequence: **Who, What, Why, Where, How, Cause, Effect**.

In this narrative, each "Who" is an identity, each "What" a possibility, each "Why" a purpose, each "Where" a field, each "How" a method, each "Cause" an origin, and each "Effect" a result.

---

### Phase 1: The Identity (WHO)
We begin with the **Who**. This is the Meta-Physical foundation.
1.  **Who of Who**: To ask "Who" of the identity is to find the **Self**.
    *   *The 3rd Layer (c):* When we ask **How of the Who of Who**, we find the **Willpower**—the method by which the Self acts.
2.  **What of Who**: To ask "What" of the identity is to find its **Capability**.
    *   *The 3rd Layer (c):* When we ask **Why of the What of the Who**, we find the **Mission**—the reason why those capabilities exist.
3.  **Why of Who**: To ask "Why" of the identity is to find its **Motivation**.
    *   *The 3rd Layer (c):* When we ask **Cause of the Why of the Who**, we find the **Inception**—the historical event that created the motive.
4.  **Where of Who**: To ask "Where" of the identity is to find its **Locus** or Presence.
5.  **How of Who**: To ask "How" of the identity is to find its **Character** or Manner.
6.  **Cause of Who**: To ask "Cause" of the identity is to find its **Ancestry**.
7.  **Effect of Who**: To ask "Effect" of the identity is to find its **Reputation**.

### Phase 2: The Possibility (WHAT)
Next, the identity looks at the **What**.
1.  **Who of What**: The **Subject** of the object. Who is this thing for?
2.  **What of What**: The **Substance**. What is it actually made of?
    *   *The 3rd Layer (c):* **Effect of the What of What** is the **Durability**—how the substance holds up over time.
3.  **Why of What**: The **Justification**. Why does this object or idea exist?
4.  **Where of What**: The **Scope**. Where does this idea reach its limits?
5.  **How of What**: The **Function**. Through what process does it operate?
6.  **Cause of What**: The **Discovery**. What event led to this thing being known?
7.  **Effect of What**: The **Utility**. What is produced by its existence?

### Phase 3: The Purpose (WHY)
The "What" leads to the **Why**—the heart of the matter.
1.  **Who of Why**: The **Beneficiary**. Who gains from this reason?
2.  **What of Why**: The **Concept**. What is the central idea of this purpose?
3.  **Why of Why**: The **Resonance**. The "Why" behind the "Why" is the core value.
4.  **Where of Why**: The **Context**. In what field does this purpose apply?
5.  **How of Why**: The **Strategy**. The method of achieving the purpose.
6.  **Cause of Why**: The **Inspiration**. What historical spark ignited the intent?
7.  **Effect of Why**: The **Fulfillment**. The emotional result of achieving the goal.

### Phase 4: The Field (WHERE)
Purpose must be grounded in a **Where**.
1.  **Who of Where**: The **Occupant**. Who resides in this space?
2.  **What of Where**: The **Terrain**. What is the physical nature of the field?
3.  **Why of Where**: The **Sanctity**. Why is this place important?
4.  **Where of Where**: The **Map**. The internal coordinates of the space.
5.  **How of Where**: The **Access**. How does one move through this place?
6.  **Cause of Where**: The **Geography**. The historical forces that shaped the land.
7.  **Effect of Where**: The **Atmosphere**. The environmental impact of the space.

### Phase 5: The Method (HOW)
In the field, we apply a **How**.
1.  **Who of How**: The **Agent**. Who is performing the action?
2.  **What of How**: The **Tool**. What instrument is used in the method?
3.  **Why of How**: The **Efficiency**. Why is this method chosen over others?
4.  **Where of How**: The **System**. Where is the process being executed?
5.  **How of How**: The **Logic**. The internal steps of the method.
6.  **Cause of How**: The **Tradition**. The history of how this was done before.
7.  **Effect of How**: The **Output**. The direct result of the action.

### Phase 6: The Origin (CAUSE)
The method is born from a **Cause**.
1.  **Who of Cause**: The **Originator**. Who started this sequence?
2.  **What of Cause**: The **Event**. What was the specific occurrence?
3.  **Why of Cause**: The **Intent**. Why was the sequence triggered?
4.  **Where of Cause**: The **Source**. Where did the trigger originate?
5.  **How of Cause**: The **Mechanism**. How did the cause translate into effect?
6.  **Cause of Cause**: The **Prime Mover**. The history behind the history.
7.  **Effect of Cause**: The **Momentum**. The energy carried forward from the start.

### Phase 7: The Consequence (EFFECT)
Finally, all paths lead to the **Effect**.
1.  **Who of Effect**: The **Witness**. Who experiences the result?
2.  **What of Effect**: The **New Reality**. What state exists now that didn't before?
3.  **Why of Effect**: The **Lesson**. Why did it end this way?
4.  **Where of Effect**: The **Domain**. Where does the impact manifest?
5.  **How of Effect**: The **Feedback**. How does the result communicate back to the start?
6.  **Cause of Effect**: The **Action**. What specifically caused this final state?
7.  **Effect of Effect**: The **Legacy**. What ripple does this result leave in history?

## The Coherence of the Whole
When we map the **Effect of the Cause of the How of the Where of the Why of the What of the Who**, we aren't just looking at seven words; we are looking at the **Birth of a Legacy** from a **Person's Intent**. 

The recursion allows us to ask:
"What is the **Logic (How)** of the **Lesson (Why)** of the **Legacy (Effect)**?"
It turns the simple question "Did it work?" into a clinical analysis of structural integrity.
