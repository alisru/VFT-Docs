# The Visionary Men: A Comparative Matrix

**Hegemonic Analysis of Divine Intermediaries**

**Purpose:** To isolate the "Visual Rhyme Scheme" used across Old and New Testament visions to depict the High Priest-King archetype. This table maps the recurrence of specific attributes across the seven primary "Man in the Vision" encounters, breaking down the figure into every constituent visual symbol.

**Methodology:**

- **Selection:** Top 7 instances of the "Man" archetype.

- **Vector Analysis:** Breaking down the figure into constituent visual symbols.

- **Verification:** Direct scriptural citation for every "YES" attribute.

**Hegemonic Analysis of the Expanded Data:**

1.  **The "Voice of Waters" Connection:** The expansion reveals that the "Sound of a Multitude" (Dan 10) and "Sound of Many Waters" (Rev 1) are functionally identical descriptions of the divine voice, further cementing the link.

2.  **The "Rainbow/Head" Link:** A unique vector appears between **Ezekiel 1** (Rainbow brightness) and **Revelation 10** (Rainbow on head), suggesting the "Mighty Angel" is a direct manifestation of the Glory seen by Ezekiel.

3.  **The "Waist" Vector:** Note the specific focus on the *waist* or *loins* across Daniel, Ezekiel 1, Ezekiel 9, and Revelation 1. It is the locus of authority (the belt/sash), judgment (fire from the waist down), or administration (the writing case).
