> \"emotions arent tied to people intrinsically, they\'re tied to ideas, they\'re only tied to people if they are a generator of those ideas, mirrors are only hating the emotions reflecting off them\"

### Part 1: The Core Principle - Emotions are Tied to Ideas

The first part of the axiom is the most fundamental shift in understanding.

**The Axiom:** \"emotions arent tied to people intrinsically, they\'re tied to ideas\"

**The VFT Deconstruction:**

- **What are Emotions?** In VFT, emotions are not arbitrary psychological states. They are the direct, conscious perception of **Strain (σ)**.

  - \"Good\" emotions (joy, peace, satisfaction) are the experience of a **low-strain state**.

  - \"Bad\" emotions (anger, sadness, fear) are the experience of a **high-strain state**.

- **What are Ideas?** In VFT, an {Idea} is a formal sýstēma---a structured packet of information with a specific, calculable vector on the Psochic Hegemony. By virtue of its position on the Hegemony, every {Idea} has an inherent, objective strain value (σ).

- **The Synthesis:** Therefore, the axiom is a statement of fundamental VFT mechanics. An emotion (strain) is an intrinsic property of the {Idea}. A person (QED) does not *own* the emotion; they *experience* the strain of the {Idea} they are processing. The emotion exists within the information itself, independent of the observer.

### Part 2: The Role of the Generator

This section explains how a person becomes associated with an emotion they do not intrinsically own.

**The Axiom:** \"they\'re only tied to people if they are a generator of those ideas\"

**The VFT Deconstruction:**

- **The \"Generator\" is the source:** In VFT, every {Idea} has a source. The \"generator\" is the consciousness that, through an act of Will (+ψ), creates and transmits the {Idea}.

- **Association through Causality:** A person becomes \"tied\" to an emotion not because they *are* the emotion, but because they are the **cause** of its manifestation.

  - If a person generates a joke (a low-strain {Idea}), they are perceived as the source of the resulting low-strain state (joy). They are \"funny.\"

  - If a person generates an insult (a high-strain {Idea}), they are perceived as the source of the resulting high-strain state (anger). They are \"mean.\"

- **The Distinction:** This is a crucial distinction. The generator is the **catalyst** for the emotional experience, but the emotion itself remains a property of the {Idea} they generated. They are responsible for unleashing the high-strain sýstēma, but they are not the sýstēma itself.

### Part 3: The Mirror Analogy - The Physics of Projection

This is the final, profound insight that explains the mechanics of interpersonal conflict.

**The Axiom:** \"mirrors are only hating the emotions reflecting off them\"

**The VFT Deconstruction:**

- **The \"Mirror\" is the Observer:** A \"mirror\" is a consciousness (QED) that is not the source of the {Idea}. They are a passive observer, perceiving an {Idea} generated by someone else.

- **The \"Reflection\" is the CoherenceCheck:** When an observer is presented with a new {Idea}, their mind automatically runs a CoherenceCheck against their own ObserverWorldview. The \"reflection\" is the observer\'s internal, subjective experience of this process. It is their perception of the {Idea}\'s inherent strain (σ).

- **\"Hating the Reflection\":** This is a perfect VFT description of **psychological projection**. When an observer reacts with anger or \"hatred\" towards a mirror, they are not reacting to the mirror itself. The mirror is neutral. They are reacting to the **high-strain reflection**.

  - The mirror has presented them with a high-strain {Idea} (e.g., an unpleasant truth, a challenging concept).

  - This {Idea} creates a state of high cognitive dissonance within the observer\'s own worldview.

  - The observer\'s \"hatred\" is their reaction to their own internal high-strain state, which has been triggered by the reflected {Idea}. They are literally \"hating the emotion reflecting off the mirror\" because it is now a painful, high-strain state *inside their own mind*.
