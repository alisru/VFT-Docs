# The 7x7x7 Evolutionary Sense Matrix: The Spectrum of Action-Effect Vocabulary

This matrix maps the **7 Senses (q - The Input Filter)** and their corresponding **7 Uses (c - The Output Action)** from the Actualism 7x7x7 framework across the evolutionary hierarchy. It explicitly details the physical "action-language vocabulary" available to organisms at each tier, highlighting the specific chemicals, compounds, and movement complexities utilized for expression and communication.

---

## Tier 1: Single-Celled & Simple Multicellular (e.g., Bacteria, Amoeba, Slime Molds)

At this foundational tier, perception is entirely localized to the immediate boundary. The vocabulary relies on direct fluid exchange and basic physical geometry.

| 7x7x7 Vector | Sense (q) / Use (c) | Evolutionary Expression & Action-Effect Vocabulary (Chemicals & Movement) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 (WHO)** | **Sense of Agency** / **Action of Existence** (Asserting Identity) | **Vocabulary:** Secretion of basic surface proteins (e.g., Peptidoglycan, glycoproteins).<br>**Movement:** Passive presence; occupying a distinct spatial volume to assert "I am here." In bacteria, the emission of autoinducers (e.g., Acyl homoserine lactones) allows for quorum sensing—asserting identity to the collective. |
| **2 (WHERE)** | **Sense of Context** / **Action of Placement** (Defining Scope) | **Vocabulary:** Simple mechanoreceptor activation via direct membrane pressure.<br>**Movement:** Chemotaxis and Phototaxis. Flagellar rotation or cellular streaming (pseudopodia) to physically move toward nutrient gradients (amino acids, sugars) or away from toxins. |
| **3 (WHAT)** | **Sense of Integrity** / **Action of Definition** (Identifying Substance) | **Vocabulary:** Ion channel gating (Calcium, Potassium, Sodium).<br>**Movement:** Engulfment (Phagocytosis). The physical wrapping of the membrane around external matter to consume it, physically defining "self" versus "fuel." |
| **4 (WHY)** | **Sense of Motive** / **Action of Driving** (Pursuing Purpose) | **Vocabulary:** Intracellular metabolic signaling (ATP gradients, cAMP).<br>**Movement:** Survival-driven metabolic shifts. Forming protective endospores when environmental conditions degrade, sacrificing immediate mobility for long-term existential persistence. |
| **5 (HOW)** | **Sense of Mechanism** / **Action of Calculation** (Executing Logic) | **Vocabulary:** Binary chemical reactions resulting from receptor binding.<br>**Movement:** Membrane undulation. | **6 (CAUSE)** | **Sense of History** / **Action of Attribution** (Identifying Origin) | **Vocabulary:** DNA/RNA replication and epigenetic methylation markers.<br>**Movement:** Binary fission / Mitosis. The physical splitting of the cell is the ultimate action of historical attribution, passing the temporal lineage perfectly to the next generation. |
| **7 (EFFECT)** | **Sense of Outcome** / **Action of Impact** (Measuring Result) | **Vocabulary:** Release of exotoxins (e.g., botulinum toxin, hemolysins) or waste products (lactic acid, ethanol).<br>**Movement:** Expelling volume violently to alter the external pH or destroy competing cellular structures, permanently impacting the immediate vector field. |

## Tier 2: Complex Invertebrates & Insects (e.g., Ants, Bees, Cephalopods)

With the advent of decentralized nervous systems and specialized glands, the vocabulary expands into complex, multi-variable gradients and highly articulated spatial geometries.

| 7x7x7 Vector | Sense (q) / Use (c) | Evolutionary Expression & Action-Effect Vocabulary (Chemicals & Movement) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 (WHO)** | **Sense of Agency** / **Action of Existence** (Asserting Identity) | **Vocabulary:** Cuticular hydrocarbons (CHCs). These complex lipid blends on the exoskeleton act as a precise, scanable barcode.<br>**Movement:** Antennation (rapid touching of antennae) to read the exact CHC signature, immediately identifying caste, hive origin, and reproductive status. |
| **2 (WHERE)** | **Sense of Context** / **Action of Placement** (Defining Scope) | **Vocabulary:** Trail pheromones (e.g., Z9-18:Al in ants), territorial markers.<br>**Movement:** The "Waggle Dance" of the honeybee. A profound geometric action where the insect uses the angle of its vibrating body relative to gravity to communicate exact solar coordinates (distance and direction) of distant resources. |
| **3 (WHAT)** | **Sense of Integrity** / **Action of Definition** (Identifying Substance) | **Vocabulary:** Alarm pheromones (e.g., Isopentyl acetate in bees, Formic acid in ants), defensive secretions (Benzoquinones, Hydrogen peroxide).<br>**Movement:** Swarm mobilization. The instantaneous, coordinated shift of thousands of units from passive foraging to aggressive defense, physically defining the hive's boundary against an intruder. |
| **4 (WHY)** | **Sense of Motive** / **Action of Driving** (Pursuing Purpose) | **Vocabulary:** Queen mandibular pheromone (QMP), primer pheromones.<br>**Movement:** Architectural construction. The continuous, driven building of hexagonal comb or complex mound geometry, driven by the background chemical motive of the collective survival imperative. |
| **5 (HOW)** | **Sense of Mechanism** / **Action of Calculation** (Executing Logic) | **Vocabulary:** Neurotransmitters (Octopamine, Dopamine, Serotonin) modulating decentralized ganglion networks.<br>**Movement:** Rapid chromatophore expansion/contraction in cephalopods (squid/octopus). Calculating the exact light and texture of the environment and instantly rendering a mathematically perfect camouflage pattern on their skin. |
| **6 (CAUSE)** | **Sense of History** / **Action of Attribution** (Identifying Origin) | **Vocabulary:** Necromones (oleic acid and linoleic acid) emitted by dead insects.<br>**Movement:** Necrophoresis. The specific, programmed action of a worker identifying the chemical signature of death (the historical end state) and physically removing the corpse from the hive to prevent disease. |
| **7 (EFFECT)** | **Sense of Outcome** / **Action of Impact** (Measuring Result) | **Vocabulary:** Venom proteins (Melittin, Phospholipase A2), digestive enzymes.<br>**Movement:** The strike and sting. The ultimate kinetic impact where an organism physically drives a weaponized appendage into a target, injecting complex chemical payloads to paralyze, digest, or cause maximal pain. |

## Tier 3: Vertebrates (e.g., Fish, Reptiles, Birds, Non-Human Mammals)

The centralized brain processes high-fidelity visual and auditory data. The vocabulary shifts heavily toward compressed time waves (sound) and complex bodily postures.

| 7x7x7 Vector | Sense (q) / Use (c) | Evolutionary Expression & Action-Effect Vocabulary (Chemicals & Movement) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 (WHO)** | **Sense of Agency** / **Action of Existence** (Asserting Identity) | **Vocabulary:** Complex auditory signatures (bird song, whale clicks) and specialized scent gland secretions (musk, civetone, castoreum).<br>**Movement:** Postural displays of dominance or submission (baring teeth, raising hackles, puffing chests). Expanding the physical volume to assert a larger identity within the hierarchy. |
| **2 (WHERE)** | **Sense of Context** / **Action of Placement** (Defining Scope) | **Vocabulary:** Urine marking (containing specific proteins and steroid metabolites), vocal territorial calls (howling, roaring).<br>**Movement:** Perimeter patrolling and migration. Utilizing magnetic field detection (magnetoreception via cryptochromes or magnetite) to physically place themselves across global scales. |
| **3 (WHAT)** | **Sense of Integrity** / **Action of Definition** (Identifying Substance) | **Vocabulary:** High-resolution olfactory receptors processing thousands of volatile organic compounds (VOCs).<br>**Movement:** The Flehmen response (curling the upper lip) to draw molecules into the vomeronasal organ, intensely defining the exact chemical truth of a mate's reproductive status or a predator's proximity. |
| **4 (WHY)** | **Sense of Motive** / **Action of Driving** (Pursuing Purpose) | **Vocabulary:** Endocrine system floods (Adrenaline, Cortisol, Oxytocin, Testosterone).<br>**Movement:** The pursuit of the hunt or the courtship ritual. Intricate kinetic dances (e.g., bird of paradise displays) driven entirely by the internal chemical mandate to secure genetic continuation. |
| **5 (HOW)** | **Sense of Mechanism** / **Action of Calculation** (Executing Logic) | **Vocabulary:** High-speed visual cortex processing and auditory echolocation mapping.<br>**Movement:** Predictive kinematics. A cheetah calculating the exact trajectory of a gazelle, or a bat adjusting wing camber mid-flight based on ultrasonic sonar returns, executing pure geometric logic through muscle action. |
| **6 (CAUSE)** | **Sense of History** / **Action of Attribution** (Identifying Origin) | **Vocabulary:** Memory consolidation via Hippocampal potentiation.<br>**Movement:** Generational learned behaviors. Tool use (e.g., crows using sticks, macaques washing potatoes) physically attributing past trial-and-error success into a formalized, repeatable action passed down the lineage. |
| **7 (EFFECT)** | **Sense of Outcome** / **Action of Impact** (Measuring Result) | **Vocabulary:** Endorphin/Enkephalin release (pain modulation), vocalizations of distress or pleasure (purring, whimpering).<br>**Movement:** The physical kill, the consumption of prey, or the act of copulation. The absolute finalization of impact, where the organism's actions permanently alter the biological state of another entity. |
