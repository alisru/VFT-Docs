### SENSE q5: THE HOW OF THE EFFECT (Measurement)

*Scanning the logic of measuring the effect. How do we know the impact is real?*

**c1 \[Use: Who\] - Anecdotal Evidence**

- **The Glitch:** "It worked for Me, so it works."

- **The Diagnosis:** Violates Existence. Sample-size error. N=1.

- **The Example:** "I ate dirt and got better."

- **Direct Method:** "It happened to me."

- **Indirect Method:** Elevating personal experience to universal law.

- **The Resolution:** Expand the N. "You are one data point. What does the aggregate say?"

**c2 \[Use: Where\] - The Cherry Picking**

- **The Glitch:** Showing only the Positive Effects and hiding the Negative.

- **The Diagnosis:** Violates Magnitude. Sales-job. Presenting the highlight reel as the movie.

- **The Example:** Drug companies publishing only the successful trials.

- **Direct Method:** "Look at the winners."

- **Indirect Method:** Filtering the dataset to create a false signal.

- **The Resolution:** Show the Trash. "Show me the failed trials. Show me the losers."

**c3 \[Use: What\] - The False Precision**

- **The Glitch:** Measuring the Effect with decimal points that don't exist.

- **The Diagnosis:** Violates Polarity. Illusion of accuracy.

- **The Example:** Ranking happiness as 8.43/10.

- **Direct Method:** "The data is precise."

- **Indirect Method:** Using digits to simulate rigor.

- **The Resolution:** Margin of Error. "You are measuring fog with a micrometer. Round off."

**c4 \[Use: Why\] - Goodhart's Law**

- **The Glitch:** When a Measure becomes a Target, it ceases to be a good measure.

- **The Diagnosis:** Violates Velocity. Gaming the system.

- **The Example:** A call center measured by call time just hanging up on people.

- **Direct Method:** "Hit the numbers."

- **Indirect Method:** Optimizing the metric by destroying the value.

- **The Resolution:** Multiple Metrics. "Measure speed AND satisfaction. You can't game everything."

**c5 \[Use: How\] - Campbell's Law**

- **The Glitch:** The more a metric is used, the more it corrupts the process.

- **Direct Method:** "We improved scores."

- **Indirect Method:** Teaching the test, not the student.

- **The Resolution:** Low Stakes. "Metrics are for diagnosis, not punishment."

**c6 \[Use: Cause\] - The McNamara Fallacy**

- **The Glitch:** Ignoring what cannot be measured and focusing only on what can.

- **The Diagnosis:** Violates Origin. Quant-bias. The map is not the territory.

- **The Example:** Ignoring employee morale because there isn't a KPI for it.

- **Direct Method:** "Show me the data."

- **Indirect Method:** Assuming the unmeasurable is non-existent.

- **The Resolution:** Qualitative Input. "Some things count that can't be counted."

**c7 \[Use: Effect\] - The Survivor Bias**

- **The Glitch:** Measuring the Effect only on those who survived.

- **The Diagnosis:** Violates Limits. Ghost-data. Missing the dead.

- **Indirect Method:** Analyzing the bullet holes on the returning planes.
