## Part 1: The Taxonomy of the 42 (The Partial Vectors)

There are **42 Ideological Vectors** (6 Modes × 7 Planes) that represent the human attempt to solve the Equation of Reality using only one variable. These are "Partial Truths" which, when isolated, become "Hegemonic Lies."

### I. The Anchored Ideologies (Focus: WHERE)

Obsession with the Physical Plane, Matter, and Location.

The Question: "Where is the material proof?"

1.  **Materialism:** Believes only the *Where* (Matter) exists. Consciousness is a byproduct.

2.  **Empiricism:** Truth is only valid if it can be measured in the *Where*.

3.  **Consumerism:** Fulfillment is found by accumulating objects in the *Where*.

4.  **Deep Ecology:** The *Where* (Biosphere) is the supreme moral agent; humanity is secondary.

5.  **Geopolitics (Realpolitik):** Power is derived solely from the control of the *Where* (Geography).

6.  **Physicalism:** Mental states do not exist; there is only the biological *Where* (Brain states).

### II. The Agentic Ideologies (Focus: WHO)

Obsession with the Conscious Plane, Identity, and the Ego.

The Question: "Who is in control?"

7.  **Solipsism:** Only "I" (*Who*) exist; everything else is a projection.

8.  **Egoism:** The self-interest of the *Who* is the only valid compass.

9.  **Identity Politics:** Truth is determined solely by *Who* you are (Group/Tribe).

10. **Humanism:** The *Who* (Humanity) is the measure of all things.

11. **Narcissism:** The universe exists to reflect the glory of the *Who*.

12. **Great Man Theory:** History is driven solely by the will of specific powerful *Whos*.

### III. The Narrative Ideologies (Focus: WHY)

Obsession with the Lyrical Plane, Meaning, and Purpose.

The Question: "Why are we here?"

13. **Theism:** There is a divine *Why* provided by a Creator.

14. **Nihilism:** There is absolutely *no* *Why* (The fixation on the void).

15. **Existentialism:** The universe has no *Why*, so you must manufacture one.

16. **Absurdism:** The conflict between the search for *Why* and the silence of the world.

17. **Gnosticism:** The *Why* of this world is evil; we must escape to the true *Why*.

18. **Teleology:** Every object has a specific natural end-goal (*Why*) embedded in it.

### IV. The Root Ideologies (Focus: CAUSE)

Obsession with the Historical Plane, Origins, and Lineage.

The Question: "What caused this?"

19. **Determinism:** The *Cause* (Past) dictates the present; free will is an illusion.

20. **Traditionalism:** The *Cause* (Ancestors) is the only valid guide.

21. **Marxism (Historical Materialism):** History is a sequence of economic *Causes*.

22. **Creationism:** Reality is defined entirely by a specific divine *Cause* event.

23. **Psychoanalysis:** Behavior is ruled by the *Cause* (Childhood/Trauma).

24. **Reactionaryism:** The goal is to return to a previous state (*Cause*).

### V. The Visionary Ideologies (Focus: WHAT)

Obsession with the Possible Plane, Potential, and Imagination.

The Question: "What could be?"

25. **Idealism:** The *What* (Idea/Form) is more real than the physical.

26. **Utopianism:** We can engineer a perfect *What* (Society).

27. **Transhumanism:** We must transcend biology to become a new *What*.

28. **Futurism:** The value of the present is determined by the speed of the *What*.

29. **Surrealism:** The *What* (Dream) is the superior reality.

30. **Anarchism:** If we remove rules, the natural *What* (Order) will emerge.

### VI. The Mechanical Ideologies (Focus: HOW)

Obsession with the Logical Plane, Systems, and Efficiency.

The Question: "How does it work?"

31. **Rationalism:** Pure Reason (The *How*) is the only path to truth.

32. **Pragmatism:** If the *How* works, it is true (Results over Process).

33. **Utilitarianism:** The best path is a calculation of the *How* (Greatest good).

34. **Technocracy:** Society should be ruled by experts who understand the *How*.

35. **Legalism:** Adherence to the strict *How* (Code) is the highest virtue.

36. **Positivism:** Only logical *How* statements are meaningful.

### VII. The Energetic Ideologies (Focus: EFFECT)

Obsession with the Emotive Plane, Impact, and Sensation.

The Question: "How does it feel?"

37. **Hedonism:** The *Effect* (Pleasure) is the only intrinsic good.

38. **Emotivism:** Moral statements are just expressions of *Effect* (Feelings).

39. **Pacifism:** Avoiding the negative *Effect* (Pain) is the absolute rule.

40. **Romanticism:** The intensity of the *Effect* (Emotion) is the essence of life.

41. **Altruism:** The positive *Effect* on others is the sole justification.

42. **Sensationalism:** Value is measured by the intensity of the *Effect* (Awe).
