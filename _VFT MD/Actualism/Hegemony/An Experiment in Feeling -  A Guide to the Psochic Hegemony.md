### An Experiment in Feeling: A Guide to the Psochic Hegemony

This is a practical experiment to learn what good and bad feels like, and how to relate the feelings inside you to actual, definable things. It is a test for the Psochic Hegemony.

#### Step 1: The Subconscious Lie Detector

The first step is to notice the difference in your internal state when you are presented with two similar statements. Take the following statements as if they are absolute fact:

Statement A:

"You believe that the things that you've been told about everything are completely 100% true."

As you read this, you should notice a distinct tightening or tension feeling in your core. This is your subconscious saying "No." Your subconscious cannot lie because it is the unfiltered, core you, and you can tell that no-one has *The* answer

Statement B:

"You believe the things trustworthy people say about everything are completely 100% true."

Upon reading this second statement, the tightening feeling should release as you agree with the statement. This feeling of release represents you coming into alignment with your subconscious.

#### Step 2: Worldview Contraction & Expansion

This step is designed to feel your worldview contracting or expanding. Consider the following statements as if they were 100% true and listen to your subconscious, your soul.

Statements for Expansion:

"You can do anything you put your mind to."

"Anything's possible until it's proven wrong."

You may feel a sense of lightness as you read these. This is the feeling of your worldview expanding.

Statements for Contraction:

"Everything you've been doing is wrong."

"You're a failure."

That sense of lightness should feel like it's shrunk suddenly in surprise if you were following the instructions. From this new, collapsed worldview, if you repeat the first part of the experiment again, it will not work. This is the sense that all is lost and nothing will work.

To recover, you will have to repeat the words of affirmation. As you do, you will slowly feel your worldview expanding again. You can test the difference by repeating the insults, and you should feel the sliding scales of good and bad. This is why when someone does something bad that is uncharacteristic, they get attacked, while if it's part of their character it is felt as more 'normal'. This is due to the worldview being your scope of acceptable possibilities.

#### Step 3: Defining Specific Feelings

These emotions represent the extents of the graph with more precision. The number of ideas or options for action corresponds to arousal (Will, **ψ**). The requirement or necessity of that action corresponds to valence (Receptivity, **υ**), where a high requirement creates negative valence.

- **Tense:** A state of high arousal and moderately negative valence. This is the feeling of having many options or ideas to process (**+ψ**), but also feeling a strong requirement to act upon them, creating internal pressure (**-υ**). This is likely what you are feeling now, processing these complex ideas with the implicit requirement to understand them.

- **Alert:** A state that builds from Tense towards a positive outcome. It is a state of high arousal (**+ψ**) where the valence is neutral. You feel that something good may happen and that there is something you can do about it right now, but the path is not yet fully clear. Pleasure is the baseline for this state; you are moving towards it.

- **Excited:** A state of high arousal and positive valence. You have many ideas and a strong desire to act (**+ψ**), and there is little to no external requirement or pressure, making the action feel good (**+υ**).

- **Happy:** A state of moderate arousal and high positive valence. There are a comfortable number of options, and the path of action is clear, effortless, and highly aligned with your desires (**+υ**).

- **Content:** A state of low arousal and positive valence. There are few options for action needed (**-ψ**), and a low requirement to do anything, creating a feeling of satisfaction with the present state (**+υ**).

- **Relaxed:** A state of low arousal and moderately positive valence. A passive state (**-ψ**) with a slight, pleasant alignment (**+υ**) and no requirement to act.

- **Calm:** A state of very low arousal and neutral valence. There is no action to be taken and no requirement to act (**-ψ**), representing the baseline state of equilibrium with minimal tension (**σ**).

- **Bored:** A state of low arousal and negative valence. There are no options for action (**-ψ**), but a lingering feeling that something *should* be done, creating a low-level negative requirement (**-υ**).

- **Depressed:** A state of low arousal and high negative valence. There are no perceived options for action (**-ψ**), and a strong feeling of being trapped by a high, unresolvable requirement (**-υ**).

- **Sad:** A state of moderate arousal and negative valence. Some options exist, but they are all perceived as leading to a high-requirement, negative outcome (**-υ**).

- **Distressed:** A state of high arousal and negative valence. There are many possibilities for action (**+ψ**), but all are perceived as necessary, high-pressure, and negative (**-υ**).

- **Angry:** A state of high arousal and moderately negative valence. You have a large number of ideas or options for action (**+ψ**) and feel a powerful requirement to act against something (**-υ**), directing the energy outwards.
