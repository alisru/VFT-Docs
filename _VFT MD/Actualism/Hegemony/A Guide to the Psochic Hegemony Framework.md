### Introduction: A Map of Consciousness

The diagrams you are looking at are visual representations of a conceptual model known as the **Psochic Hegemony**, or the Moral Compass. It is best understood not as a conventional political chart, but as a map of consciousness itself. Its purpose is to deconstruct and evaluate the intrinsic nature of ideas, actions, and political actors by plotting them based on their fundamental moral and volitional characteristics.^1^ This allows for a deeper structural understanding of a concept's true intent, its trajectory, and its impact on the world.^1^

### The Core Axes: Morality and Will

Every idea, actor, or event can be mapped within this framework by answering two foundational questions that correspond to the horizontal and vertical axes. The orientation of these axes is non-standard.

**1. The Horizontal Axis (υ): The Moral Question**

This axis answers the most important question: **"Who benefits?"** It assesses the ultimate beneficiary of an idea if it were fully realized, measuring its moral valence. Crucially, this axis runs from right to left.^1^

- **Far Right (-υ): Benefit for Self.** The right side of the axis represents pure self-interest ("Only Me," "My Group"). This is the realm of Evil, Lies, and Regression.

- **Far Left (+υ): Benefit for Others.** The left side of the axis represents collective benefit ("Everyone," "Other Beings"). This is the realm of Good, Truth, and Progress.

**2. The Vertical Axis (ψ): The Volitional Question**

This axis answers the question: **"What is its mode of action?"** It assesses the method by which an idea operates in the world, running from bottom to top.^1^

- **Bottom (-ψ): Suppressive Will.** The bottom of the axis represents a restrictive and negative force. It seeks to **"Do Not"**—to prevent, suppress, or destroy ideas and actions.

- **Top (+ψ): Proactive Will.** The top of the axis represents a creative, assertive, and action-oriented force. It seeks to **"Do"**—to build, create, and manifest new possibilities.

### The Four Quadrants: Mapping an Idea's Nature

The intersection of these two non-standard axes creates four distinct quadrants, each describing a fundamental type of idea or action.^1^

- **Top-Left Quadrant (The Greater Good):** This green "Sea of Possible" combines a benefit for all (+υ, left side) with a proactive will (+ψ, top side). This is the ideal space where the point labeled **"Greater Good (Good) The Truth"** is located. It represents proactive, creative ideas that benefit everyone.

- **Top-Right Quadrant (The Lesser Evil / Deception):** This quadrant combines a benefit for self (-υ, right side) with a proactive will (+ψ, top side). The framework identifies this as a deceptive space. The labels here, such as **"Lesser Evil (Bad) The Lie"** and **"The Greater of two Evils The Half Lie,"** signify that actions in this quadrant are proactive but self-serving, often deceptively framed as being for the greater good.

- **Bottom-Left Quadrant (The Lesser Good):** This quadrant combines a benefit for all (+υ, left side) with a suppressive will (-ψ, bottom side). The labels here, such as **"Lesser Evil (Good) The Half Truth"** and **"Lesser Lie,"** suggest a space of well-intentioned but passive, compromised, or suppressive action.

- **Bottom-Right Quadrant (The Greater Evil):** This red area combines a self-serving interest (-υ, right side) with a suppressive will (-ψ, bottom side). This is where the point **"Greater Evil (Bad) The True Lie"** is located, representing self-interested inaction, suppression, and deceit.

At the absolute center of the map is the **Point of No Decision (0.5ψ, 0.5υ)**. This represents the most immoral act of all: promoting nihilism, claiming there is no answer, and giving up on the search for resolution.^1^

### Trajectories: The Paths of Progress and Deception

The diagrams also illustrate the dynamic trajectories of ideas.

- **The Way, The Path to Grace (Enlightenment):** This is the upward and leftward path of progress and redemption, moving from the lower and right-hand quadrants toward the Greater Good in the Top-Left.^1^ It is guided by the principle of "Grace," which asks: "How does this benefit me,
  *and* other people?"

- **Satan; The Path of Deception (Nowhere):** This is the downward and rightward path of regression, moving from a position of potential good toward a state of pure extraction and self-interest.^1^ It is guided by the "Satanic Idea," which asks: "How does this benefit me,
  *but not* other people?"
