# How to Use the Psochic Hegemony: A Practical Guide

The Psochic Hegemony is not an abstract chart; it is a map of consciousness itself, a tool for communicating with the deepest parts of your own mind. Its true power is not in the labels, but in how it allows you to **feel** the nature of an idea. This guide provides a step-by-step method for using the Hegemony as an experiential and diagnostic tool.

### Step 1: Map the Territory (Calibration)

Before you can navigate, you must first understand the landscape. The first step is to calibrate your own internal senses to the different points on the Hegemony. This is an exercise in focused consciousness.

1.  **Find a quiet space and look at the Hegemony diagram.**

2.  Locate the different scales of good and evil, lesser to greater and understand the meanings associated with those concepts

3.  **Focus on the Greater Evil:** Now, shift your focus to the "Greater Evil" point in the bottom-right. Understand its nature: inaction and passivity that benefits only the self. It is the state of apathy and complacency. As you contemplate this concept, you should feel a distinct **tension** in your core. This is a physical sensation. It is your subconscious, your soul, registering a high-strain, high-resistance idea.

### Step 2: Feel the Flow (Complex Emotions)

Complex feelings are not random; they are the result of your consciousness moving between different points on the Hegemony. You can learn to identify this movement.

1.  **Start at the Greater Good.** Hold that feeling of harmony.

2.  **Move to the Greater Evil.** Feel the tension spike.

3.  **Now, move to the Lesser Good.** Feel how the tension changes.

4.  **Wrap your focus:** Consciously move your focus around the compass, from point to point, clockwise or counter-clockwise. Feel the "flow" of your internal state as it shifts. This sensation of moving between different states of tension and harmony is the basis for all complex emotions. It is your mind navigating the currents of the Axiom Space.

### Step 3: The Litmus Test (Judging the Whole Cloud)

You can now use this calibrated sense to judge any action or idea. However, true judgment requires seeing the whole picture, not just its most appealing or threatening parts.

We are constantly told to "look on the brighter side," that "every cloud has a silver lining." This is a flawed analytical method. The "silver lining" is simply the boundary of the cloud where it is least dense. Conversely, a fearful mind will focus only on the darkest part of the cloud, assuming it represents the whole. Both are incorrect.

No, you must consider the whole cloud and its contents. You must average its shades to determine its true nature—to see if it will bring rain and needs to be prepared for, or if it is just a harmless, fluffy cloud.

The most magnificent cloud formations can be purely white, but appear dark because of shadows cast from a single, limited angle of illumination. If you take in the whole picture from multiple perspectives, you see its beauty. But if you get caught up in comparing the "silver linings" to the dark "implied contents," you miss the bigger picture. You forget how to tell if it's going to rain.

To apply the litmus test correctly:

1.  **Hold an idea in your mind.** Consider it as a whole cloud.

2.  **Resist the urge to focus on the silver lining or the darkest patch.** Instead, use your calibrated sense to feel the **average tension** of the entire concept.

3.  **Trust the feeling.** Does the idea, as a whole, create the sharp, high-strain tension of a storm cloud (a "bad idea")? Or does it create the harmonious, low-strain feeling of a magnificent white cloud (a "good idea")? Ideas that generate a deep, average tension are things your subconscious self knows are bad ideas. Actions that generate no average tension, especially after thorough, multi-perspective questioning, are aligned with a good and truthful path and are doing what it says it will do.

4.  **Ask Questions** and find the reason behind those feelings, when you find the reason behind the feeling, for a bad feeling it will dissipate as the confusion resolves itself, for a good feeling when you ask questions you will feel resistance to incorrect answers and a lack of with more correct answers
