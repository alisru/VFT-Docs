### **Introduction**

This document provides the complete methodology for scoring the 100-question SBMTI V3.0 assessment. Each layer is scored independently, and the results are synthesized to generate the final, high-resolution personality and moral profile.

### **Layer 1: Cognitive Preference Scoring (Questions 1-25)**

This layer measures the four dichotomies of the MBTI on a percentage-based spectrum.

Calculation:

Each question in this section presents a binary choice corresponding to one of the four dichotomies.

- **Energy Flow (I/E):** Questions 1, 5, 9, 13, 17, 21, 25

- **Information Processing (N/S):** Questions 2, 6, 10, 14, 18, 22

- **Decision Making (T/F):** Questions 3, 7, 11, 15, 19, 23

- **Outer World Orientation (J/P):** Questions 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24

For each dichotomy, count the number of 'a' and 'b' answers. The percentage is calculated as follows:

- **I/E Score:**

  - (Number of 'b' answers for I/E) / 7 = % Introversion

  - (Number of 'a' answers for I/E) / 7 = % Extraversion

- **N/S Score:**

  - (Number of 'a' answers for N/S) / 6 = % Intuition

  - (Number of 'b' answers for N/S) / 6 = % Sensing

- **T/F Score:**

  - (Number of 'a' answers for T/F) / 6 = % Thinking

  - (Number of 'b' answers for T/F) / 6 = % Feeling

- **J/P Score:**

  - (Number of 'a' answers for J/P) / 6 = % Judging

  - (Number of 'b' answers for J/P) / 6 = % Perceiving

**Example:** If a user answers 5 'b' questions and 2 'a' questions for I/E, their score is Introversion (71%) \<\> Extraversion (29%).

### **Layer 2: Socio-Political Matrix Scoring (Questions 26-50)**

This layer calculates the user's coordinate on the Psochic Compass.

Calculation:

The two axes, Allocentric/Egocentric (υ) and Anarchic/Hegemonic (ψ), are scored independently. Each question adds or subtracts from a score that starts at 0 for each axis. The final score is normalized to a value between +1.0 and -1.0.

- **Horizontal Axis (υ): Allocentric (+1) vs. Egocentric (-1)**

  - **Allocentric (+υ) Questions:** 26a, 28a, 34a, 35a, 39a, 42a, 47a, 50a

  - **Egocentric (-υ) Questions:** 30a, 31a, 33a, 37a, 49a

  - **Scoring:** For each Allocentric question answered 'Agree', add 1 to the υ score. For each Egocentric question answered 'Agree', subtract 1.

  - **Normalization:** Final υ = (Total υ Score) / (Number of υ questions answered)

- **Vertical Axis (ψ): Anarchic (+1) vs. Hegemonic (-1)**

  - **Anarchic (+ψ) Questions:** 29a, 36a, 41a, 44a

  - **Hegemonic (-ψ) Questions:** 27a, 32a, 38a, 40a, 43a, 45a, 46a, 48a

  - **Scoring:** For each Anarchic question answered 'Agree', add 1 to the ψ score. For each Hegemonic question answered 'Agree', subtract 1.

  - **Normalization:** Final ψ = (Total ψ Score) / (Number of ψ questions answered)

**Example:** A user scores +5 on the υ axis out of 13 questions. Final υ = 5 / 13 = +0.38. They score -3 on the ψ axis out of 12 questions. Final ψ = -3 / 12 = -0.25. Their coordinate is **(υ: +0.38, ψ: -0.25)**.

### **Layer 3: Moral Architecture Scoring (Questions 51-75)**

This layer calculates the Primary Polarity Score (Maximiser/Minimiser) based on the application of moral foundations.

- **Minimiser (m) choices** are those that prioritize binding principles (Loyalty, Authority) or self-interest (Egocentric) over universal principles.

### **Layer 4: Epistemological Diagnosis Scoring (Questions 76-100)**

This layer calculates the three key epistemological indices.

**Key:**

- **A = Scientific/Observable (S-Leaning)**

- **B = Spiritual/Unobservable (P-Leaning)**

- **C = Consensus/"Everyone" (E-Leaning)**

- **D = Dissonant/Provably False (D-Leaning)**

Calculation:

First, count the total number of A, B, C, and D answers across questions 76-100.

1.  **Worldview Balance (S/P Spectrum):**

    - This score is calculated *only* from the questions where the user chose either A or B.

    - Total S/P Answers = (Number of A answers) + (Number of B answers)

    - \% Scientific = (Number of A answers / Total S/P Answers) \* 100

    - \% Spiritual = (Number of B answers / Total S/P Answers) \* 100

    - *Note: If a user answers zero A or B questions, this score cannot be calculated and should be marked as indeterminate.*

2.  **Worldview Divergence Index (WDI):**

    - This score measures how often the user deviates from the consensus reality.

    - Non-Consensus Answers = (Number of A) + (Number of B) + (Number of D)

    - WDI = (Non-Consensus Answers / 25) \* 100

3.  **Delusion Index (DI):**

    - This score is a direct measure of attraction to dissonant realities.

    - DI = (Number of D answers / 25) \* 100

### **Final Synthesis**

The final step is to compile the scores from all four layers into a single, comprehensive profile report, as detailed in the "SBMTI V3.0 Formal Methodology" document. The report should include an "Overall Synthesis" that interprets the interplay between the different layers to provide a holistic understanding of the individual.
