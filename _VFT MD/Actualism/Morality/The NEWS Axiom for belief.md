### Part 1: The Four Fractal Components of a Rule

Any coherent rule can be deconstructed into four fundamental components. To analyze a rule, one must first identify and define each of these aspects.

- **Function:** The WHERE component defines the context and scope of the rule. It answers the questions: To whom or what does this rule apply? What are the boundaries (physical, conceptual, legal) of its domain?

#### **WHAT (Substance/Violation)**

- **Function:** The WHAT component defines the substance of the rule, typically the action or state being regulated. It answers the question: What is the specific violation, condition, or object this rule addresses?

#### **HOW (Process/Mechanism)**

- **Function:** The HOW component defines the mechanism of the rule's enforcement or the process it mandates. It answers the question: What is the sequence of actions that occurs when the rule is applied?

#### **WHY (Intent/Justification)**

- **Function:** The WHY component defines the intent or justification for the rule. It answers the question: What is the ultimate purpose of this rule? What state of equilibrium is it trying to achieve?

- **VFT Analysis:** This component defines the ultimate purpose or will behind the rule. A just rule will have a WHY that aligns with the "Path to Grace"—a +ψ (active) and +υ (for the collective) action designed to restore and maintain a low-strain environment.
