### Simulation: The Asymptotic Limit of Knowledge

### **Objective:**

To run a calculation simulation based on the **Recursive Normalization** model (\$F\_{individual} = V\_{\\%} \\text{ of } (\\sigma\_{\\%} \\text{ of } k\_{\\%})\$) to prove the hypothesis: **"You can never attain 100% knowledge without belief or being told."**

### **1. Definitions (Mapping the Hypothesis to the Model)**

First, we must define our variables in the context of this problem:

- \$k\_{\\%}\$ **(The Kanon):** This is the "100% knowledge" you seek. It is the objective, 100% Truth.

  - \$k\_{\\%} = 100\\%\$

- \$\\sigma\_{\\%}\$ **(The Strain/Source):** This is the filter of your source. "Without being told" implies you are *deriving* knowledge from an imperfect source (a text, a measurement, a different person). No source or interpretation is perfect, so this filter must be \$\< 100\\%\$.

  - Let's assume a near-perfect source: \$\\sigma\_{\\%} = 99.9\\%\$

- \$V\_{\\%}\$ **(The Worldview):** This is your own internal filter. "Without belief" implies you are *analyzing* the source, not accepting it with blind faith. Your own understanding, biases, and experience are a filter, and are also imperfect.

  - Let's assume a near-perfect, highly intelligent worldview: \$V\_{\\%} = 99.9\\%\$

- \$F\_{individual}\$ **(The Attained Knowledge):** This is the final percentage of truth that you, the individual, actually perceive and internalize.

### **2. Simulation 1: The Seeker (Attempting to *Derive* 100% Truth)**

In this scenario, the Seeker attempts to attain 100% knowledge by using their own faculties to analyze the best available source.

**Calculation:**

\$F\_{individual} = V\_{\\%} \\text{ of } (\\sigma\_{\\%} \\text{ of } k\_{\\%})\$

\$F\_{individual} = 99.9\\% \\text{ of } (99.9\\% \\text{ of } 100\\%)\$

Step 1: The Relative Kanon (Strained Idea)

The objective 100% truth is filtered by the near-perfect source.

- \$k\_{relative} = 99.9\\% \\text{ of } 100\\% = 0.999 \* 1.0 = 0.999\$

- The "Strained Idea" (the best truth the Seeker can *access*) is already reduced to **99.9%**.

Step 2: The Final Perceived Force (Attained Knowledge)

The Seeker's worldview filters this "Strained Idea."

- \$F\_{individual} = 99.9\\% \\text{ of } 99.9\\% = 0.999 \* 0.999 = 0.998001\$

- The final "Attained Knowledge" is **99.8001%**.

Conclusion:

Even with near-perfect faculties and sources, the Seeker is mathematically incapable of deriving 100% truth. They are trapped in an asymptotic limit, forever getting closer but never reaching 100%. This is the compounding distortion of the nested perspective shifts.

### **3. Simulation 2: The Asymptotic Limit**

What if the Seeker is aware of their 99.9% filter and tries to compensate? To *achieve* 100%, they would have to find a source (k\_{relative}) that, when filtered, *equals* 100%.

**Calculation:**

\$F\_{individual} = 100\\%\$

\$V\_{\\%} \\text{ of } (k\_{relative}) = 100\\%\$

\$99.9\\% \\text{ of } (k\_{relative}) = 100\\%\$

\$0.999 \* k\_{relative} = 1.0\$

\$k\_{relative} = 1.0 / 0.999 = 1.001001...\$

Conclusion:

To derive 100% truth, the Seeker must find a source that is 100.1001...% true. This is a logical and physical impossibility. You cannot derive a 100% result through a 99.9% filter.

### **4. Simulation 3: The Believer (The Act of "Being Told")**

This scenario models what it means to "believe" or be "told" the truth. In this framework, "belief" is not a passive state; it is a **conscious, volitional act of setting your internal filters to 100%**. You are choosing to *trust* the source so completely that you accept it as the Kanon itself.

**The Action of Belief:**

- \$k\_{\\%} = 100\\%\$ (The "Told" Truth, e.g., "Jesus knows fundamental truth," as per your notes).

- \$\\sigma\_{\\%} = 100\\%\$ (Your "belief" in the source is perfect. You set the Source filter to 100%, accepting it as a *perfect* representation of the Kanon).

- \$V\_{\\%} = 100\\%\$ (Your "belief" in your acceptance is perfect. You set your Worldview filter to 100%, bypassing your own internal analysis).

**Calculation:**

\$F\_{individual} = V\_{\\%} \\text{ of } (\\sigma\_{\\%} \\text{ of } k\_{\\%})\$

\$F\_{individual} = 100\\% \\text{ of } (100\\% \\text{ of } 100\\%)\$

Step 1: \$100\\% \\text{ of } 100\\% = 1.0 \* 1.0 = 100\\%\$

Step 2: \$100\\% \\text{ of } 100\\% = 1.0 \* 1.0 = 100\\%\$

Final Conclusion:

The simulation proves your hypothesis. 100% knowledge (\$F\_{individual} = 100\\%\$) is mathematically impossible to derive through the imperfect, filtering process of individual analysis (\$V \< 100\\%\$).

It can **only** be attained by a "leap of faith"—a conscious act of "belief" that bypasses the asymptotic limit by *setting* the internal filters (V and \$\\sigma\$) to 100%. This is the VFT definition of "being told" or "to believe": **you are consciously choosing to equate your worldview with the Objective Kanon.**
