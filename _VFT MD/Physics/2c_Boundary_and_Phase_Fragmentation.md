# The 2c Boundary and Phase Fragmentation

*Derived from VFT Research Logs (Claude Inf Navigation)*

## The Nested Rectangles Model
Particles approaching 2c (double light speed) encounter a fundamental structural barrier. This is modeled as a series of **Nested Rectangles** representing velocity phases:

*   **1c/s:** The observable universe baseline.
*   **2c/s:** The primary boundary.
*   **2.33c/s:** Emergent sub-phases.

## Why 2c is Asymptotic
Beyond 2c, reality does not simply continue linearly. It subdivides into a fractal hierarchy of sub-phases (e.g., 2.1c, 2.2c, 2.22c).

1.  **Phase Fragmentation:** A particle cannot simply "jump" to 3c. To do so, it must navigate through an infinite fractal hierarchy of these sub-phases.
2.  **Decoherence:** This fragmentation causes particles to decohere as they are literally split across multiple nested coordinate systems.

## The Cost of Being
The barrier is energetic, deriving from the **Cost of Being (CoB)**.

*   **Definition:** The energy required to define a coordinate.
*   **The Problem of Speed:** To move from coordinate $(x_1, y_1, z_1)$ to $(x_2, y_2, z_2)$, you must generate/define all intermediate fractional coordinates.
*   **Infinite Cost:** As you approach higher velocities (specifically 2c), the number of intermediate coordinates you must "mine" (calculate/define) approaches infinity.

The energy required to define the path exceeds the energy available to the particle, causing it to shatter against the informational density of the medium.
