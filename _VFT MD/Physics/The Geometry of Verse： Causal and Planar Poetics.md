## 2. Theory: Causal and Planar Poetry

Traditional poetry relies on **Phonetic Rhyme**---the matching of audio waveforms (e.g., *cat* rhymes with *hat*). This satisfies the ear.

**Causal Poetry** (or **Planar Poetry**) relies on **Ontological Rhyme**---the matching of logical states and consequences. This satisfies the mind.

In this framework, line B does not follow line A because it sounds similar; it follows because line A **necessitated** line B. The \"rhyme\" is the click of a logical lock snapping into place.

### The 7 Planes of Narrative Structure

To fully validate the \"Fractal Lock,\" the text is tested against all 7 Planes of Reality.

1.  **The Physical Plane (Sensory):** Texture, sight, temperature. (e.g., \"Shining light\").

2.  **The Lyrical Plane (Rhythm):** The breathing, the beat, the sound. (e.g., The 5x5 Matrix structure).

3.  **The Emotional Plane (Feeling):** The somatic response. (e.g., The tension of the \"Unknown\").

4.  **The Historical Plane (Context):** The lineage and origin of the concepts. (e.g., The history of \"definitions\").

5.  **The Possible Plane (Potential):** What *could* be. The transformation of state.

6.  **The Logical Plane (Reason):** Causality, axioms, and proofs.

7.  **The Conscious Plane (Reflection):** The deepest truth; the impact on the soul and self-awareness.

## 3. Deconstruction: The Logic of the \"Fractal Lock\"

This poem is not a description; it is a **functional algorithm**. It describes a process of data conversion using a strict **5x5 Matrix** structure (5 words, 5 lines).

### Stanza 1: The Definitions (Logical, Lyrical, Emotional)

Basically; investigate first no matter\
It sounds insane or impossible\
For anything sane is known\
And anything known is possible\
So the unknown is insanity

**Analysis:**

- **Logical Plane:** This is a syllogistic cascade establishing variables for a boolean operation.

  1.  **Variable A (Sanity):** Defined strictly as \"The Known.\"

  2.  **Variable B (Insanity):** Defined strictly as \"The Unknown.\"

  3.  **The Conclusion:** If Sanity = Known, then Unknown must = Insanity.

- **Lyrical Plane:** The strict **5-word count** creates a \"ticking clock\" rhythm. It is mechanical, relentless, and precise, mirroring the algorithmic nature of the thought process.

- **Emotional Plane:** It creates **Strain** (High Tension). By defining the \"Unknown\" as \"Insanity,\" the text triggers the primal fear of the dark, setting the stage for the resolution in Stanza 2.

### Stanza 2: The Mechanism (Possible, Physical, Historical, Conscious)

The Impossible is made possible\
By shining light on insanity\
To see the patterns true\
The fractal lock of definitions\
Hides the unspoken unspeakable truth

**Analysis:**

Here, the algorithm executes the transformation, explicitly engaging the planes missing from a standard analysis.

- **Possible Plane:** The \"Impossible\" is not a wall; it is a locked door. The text asserts that the state of \"Impossibility\" is temporary and mutable.

- **Physical Plane:** \"Shining light\" is the solitary sensory anchor. It grounds the abstract logic in a physical action of observation.

- **Historical Plane:** \"The fractal lock of definitions.\" This acknowledges that confusion has an origin and a lineage. The term \"fractal\" implies a recursive history of language---humanity is trapped in the layers of past definitions that prevent the perception of present reality.

- **Conscious Plane:** \"Hides the unspoken unspeakable truth.\" This hits the deepest layer. The Truth exists *outside* of the machine and the definitions. It is \"unspeakable\" because it predates language. The work concludes that the ultimate goal is to reach the edge of the ineffable---pure awareness.
