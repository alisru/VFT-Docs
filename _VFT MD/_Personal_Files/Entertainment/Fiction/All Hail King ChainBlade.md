guy times travels back to the past with a suit of power armor completely covered in chainblades

- goal is to take over the world one country at a time

- each level may have a different specific goal, kill all enemies, kill the commander, destroy all structures etc

- gameplay is mainly running into enemies/structures to kill/destroy them while revving the chainblades while also dodging enemy projectiles

- the suit comes with a passive auto-repair feature so health comes back slowly over time after not taking damage

- it can also aquire materials from destroyed structures & armored enemies to gain a small boost to hp

- the chainblades on the suit heat up while being revved, this doesn't have any negative effect, it adds extra damage & sets enemies on fire when you touch them when you’re running hot enough & you also deal significantly more damage to structures & can set them on fire after \~1s of touching them

- which deals \~15% of its life as a dot effect

- however, your average heat over the level is used in determining your ranking, with a higher average leading to a lower ranking

- each level has a ranking like, F D C B A S, determined by time to clear, damage taken & average heat level

- however these factors would have to be modified when a level stipulation is to run hot or take a certain amount of damage

- Enemies:

  - Ballista

    - Slow moving siege unit that shows up in later levels & is manned by 4 swordsmen

    - Shoots fast high damage projectiles that can’t be deflected, stops shooting while under attack as the swordsmen go for you

    - Swordsmen will return to ballista after you go a certain distance away

    - Takes damage like a structure though has a lot less hp

  - Crossbowmen

    - Moves faster than swordsmen & tries to keep a distance away from the player, stands still while firing & reloading

    - Shoots slower, low damage projectiles at a good pace

    - Shots can be deflected if revving

    - Dies almost immediately on contact

  - Swordsmen

    - Moves slower than crossbowmen & actively seeks the player, stands still for a moment while attacking

    - Relatively harmless if you don’t care about heat since they die quickly, though can be painful if you get swarmed &/or want to go for a cool run

    - Attack does slightly higher damage than crossbow shots & has a faster RoF, attack is dealt in a small area in front of the swordsmen

    - Will typically come in groups & when enough of its group is killed they will instead run away from the player(ASSUMING NOT FEATURE CREEP)( or surrender & beg for their life with the option at the end of the level to decide whether or not each prisoner should be let go, killed or recruited)

  - General

    - Is the end goal for levels that stipulate to kill the general to disband the rest of its forces or convince them to join you

    - Moves faster than a swordsmen but slower than a crossbowmen

    - Is surrounded by a posse of swordsmen & crossbowmen, usually a ratio of \~3:1

    - Is also normally behind some fortifications/structures that need to be destroyed

    - Once enough of its guard is taken out it will flee to a get-away point with the rest of its guard escorting it

    - If it reaches the get-away point the level is failed like death

- The end level for each country ends with the ruler for that country either giving a last stand or swearing fealty to you, with the option of course to straight up depose them (with chainblades)

- Your decisions throughout change the ending, with your total army count & your actions dealing with other leaders determining it, ie;

  - If you end up killing every leader who swears fealty to you then you’d end up with a bad ending depending on how large your army is, like;

    - if you take no prisoners & kill everyone then the entire world turns on you & one of the assassins make it through sheer numbers along with all the uprisings

    - however if you have a medium sized army then you survive but with near constant assassination attempts

    - though if you have a large army then you end up with an uneasy relative peace with relatively frequent, though small, uprisings

  - If you allow enough rulers who swear fealty to you survive then;

    - you face many uprisings though few assassins with a small or nonexistent army though you can still hold on barely with help from other countries

    - With a medium sized army you can deal with the uprisings more efficiently though they’re still as frequent & while your nations aren’t suffering or prospering you now have to deal with a higher amount of assassins sent your way in response to the army

    - With a larger army your nations can start to prosper while dealing with less frequent uprisings & assassins, the larger army makes them easier to rout out & makes it more intimidating -:
