## **Part II: The Simulation | Case Study: Price Ceilings (General)**

**Input Statement:**

*"The Government (Who) enforces a strict Price Ceiling on apartments (How) to ensure housing is affordable for the poor (Why)."*

### **1\. The Variable Map**

We strip the emotion and map the physics of the market.

* **Sign (±):** External Authority (F\_gov).  
* **Variable (x):** Housing Units (Q) and Quality (K).  
* **Function (f(x)):** Constraint: P\_legal \< P\_equilibrium.  
* **Stated Vector (v\_intent):** Increase Access (+Q), Lower Cost (P ↓).

### **2\. The Derivation (Calculating the Outcome)**

We do not guess. We run the function f(x) against the Axioms of Supply and Demand.

#### **Step A: The Instantaneous Impact (t=0)**

The Authority applies force.

P\_market → P\_legal

* **Observation:** The price on the contract drops.  
* **Initial Check:** The Stated Vector (v\_intent) appears satisfied.

#### **Step B: The Derivative (The Reaction Rate dx/dt)**

Markets are dynamic. Capital flows toward profit and away from loss.

**Axiom:** Investment (I) only occurs where Return (ROI) \> Cost (C).

ROI \= P\_rent \- (Maintenance \+ Taxes \+ Risk)

When P\_rent is artificially capped:

1. **Profit Collapse:** ROI → 0 or ROI \< 0\.  
2. **Capital Flight:** Landlords stop maintaining buildings to restore margins.  
   dK/dt \< 0 (Quality decays: mold, leaks, disrepair)  
3. **Supply Halt:** No new units are built because construction costs exceed P\_legal.  
   dQ\_supply/dt \= 0

#### **Step C: The Integral (The Accumulation ∫)**

We sum these rates of change over 10 years.

**The Demand Gap:**

Because P\_legal is low, Demand (D) spikes to infinity.

Because ROI is low, Supply (S) remains static or shrinks.

Gap \= ∫(0 to 10\) (D(t) \- S(t)) dt → ∞

**The Emergence of Shadow Variables:**

Since the system *must* clear (people need homes), a hidden variable (z) emerges to bridge the gap between P\_legal and Reality.

P\_actual \= P\_legal \+ P\_shadow

Where P\_shadow includes:

* **Wait Time:** 5-10 year waiting lists (Time \= Money).  
* **Corruption:** "Key money," bribes, or nepotism.  
* **Decay:** The cost of living in a slum.

## **Part III: The Variable Merge | Case Study: The Australian Paradox**

In specific domains (like the Australian Housing Market), the variables merge. The "Class War" model (Landlord vs. Tenant) fails because the "Normal Person" is simultaneously a **Resident** (seeking Utility) and an **Investor** (seeking Appreciation).

**Input Context:**

*"The average citizen wants affordable housing, but also relies on property values for retirement."*

### **1\. The Variable Collision**

Instead of two opposing actors, we have one actor with opposing internal vectors.

* **Vector A (As Resident):** Wants P → 0 (Affordability/Utility).  
* **Vector B (As Investor):** Wants P → ∞ (Growth/Equity).

### **2\. The Internal Contradiction (The Wealth Trap)**

If the Government enforces a ceiling or suppresses the market:

* **The Intent:** "We will make houses cheap so you can afford one."  
* **The Reality:** By capping the Price (P), they cap the Growth (G).  
* **The Reaction:**  
  Since the citizen treats the house as a **Savings Account** (S):  
  S\_future \= P\_today \* (1 \+ r)^t  
  If r (Growth Rate) is forced to 0 by the price ceiling, then S\_future collapses.

### **3\. The Result: Eating the Seed Corn**

This policy creates a "High Win" at t=0 but a "High Loss" at t=30.

* **t=0:** You get in cheap.  
* **t=30:** You retire poor.  
* **Net Result:** The Asset (House) is converted into a Consumable (Shelter). The mechanism solves the hunger of today by destroying the harvest of tomorrow.
